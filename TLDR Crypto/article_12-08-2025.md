 STRING IT TOGETHER: HOW STABLECOINS GO MAINSTREAM (16 MINUTE READ)
[8] 

 Stablecoins can achieve mainstream US consumer payments adoption by
repeating credit cards' two-step path: first win niche segments on
intrinsic advantages like comparative convenience and powerful
incentives, then connect those â€œfiefdomsâ€ via an open,
standardized, participant-owned network. Near-term wedges include
cross-border shoppers underserved by dollar rails and incentive-first
programs where merchants launch transferable, whitelabeled stablecoins
funded by float yield, banks' ability to custody stables, and UX
improvements like account abstraction and gas sponsorship.
Programmability enables novel rewards and financing models that can
pull balances into stables and drive spend if checkout is seamless. To
scale, stablecoins need a neutral, user-owned network to enforce
interoperability, compliance, and consumer protections across chains
and issuers.

 DESIGN TESTNETS FOR FEEDBACK, NOT FARMING (11 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Fluent is launching a testnet experience designed around collecting
app feedback rather than traditional "chain farming," arguing that
current testnets have devolved into spam-filled environments where
users perform mindless tasks hoping for token rewards. The company
criticizes how chains typically optimize for vanity metrics like
transaction volume and user counts instead of fostering the
development of useful applications that drive long-term success.
Fluent's approach focuses on helping builders iterate toward
product-market fit by systematically collecting and analyzing user
feedback across areas like usability, engagement, and performance.
This "apps first" philosophy positions chains as infrastructure that
succeeds when the applications built on them succeed, similar to how
AWS benefits from Netflix's success.
