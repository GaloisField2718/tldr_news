 STABLECOIN YIELD SPAT PUTS CLARITY ACT AT RISK (3 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 The White House is reportedly weighing whether to abandon the CLARITY
Act after Coinbase pulled support ahead of a Senate Banking markup.
The dispute centers on restrictions against paying yield for simply
holding stablecoins, a key issue for Coinbase, given estimates that
stablecoin-related revenue could exceed $1B in 2025. 

 BITCOIN DROPS BELOW $92.5K AS TARIFF-WAR HEADLINES TRIGGER CRYPTO
SELLOFF (2 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Bitcoin slid about 3% in a few hours to roughly $92,474, dragging
ETH, XRP, and SOL lower and triggering over $750M in long liquidations
as markets reacted to escalating US-EU tariff-war fears tied to a
Greenland dispute. Analysts said the geopolitical shock hit a market
already on edge from the stalled US crypto market structure bill and
weakening crypto-specific risk appetite. Technical breaks and ETF
outflows provided additional pressure.

 THE HIDDEN DANGERS & COSTS OF TOKENIZED ASSETS (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Most tokenized assets trade in illiquid, fragile markets, creating a
self-reinforcing cycle where shallow liquidity discourages
participation. The first hidden cost appears in slippage, with
tokenized gold showing striking differences versus traditional
markets. Despite the promise to bring trillion-dollar TradFi markets
onchain, liquidity as a prerequisite for composability has not been
realized. 

 IS CRYPTO HIRING COOKED IN 2026? (5 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Crypto job postings plummeted 80% YoY in early January, dropping from
~38 jobs/day to ~6.5 jobs/day across major boards. Hiring is
concentrated in infrastructure, stablecoins, and payment rails (60%),
with growth-stage companies leading demand for senior talent. Solana
captured 22% of new developers in 2024 vs Ethereum's 16%. It is the
first ecosystem since 2016 to challenge ETH's talent dominance.

 SUNSETTING YAPS & INTRODUCING KAITO STUDIO (3 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Kaito is shutting down Yaps, its permissionless incentivized
engagement platform, and launching Kaito Studio as the company pivots
away from high-volume airdrop distribution models. Despite experiments
with tighter eligibility filters, social and onchain verification, and
varied incentive designs, Yaps struggled with persistent spam and
quality issues exacerbated by X algorithm changes and the
proliferation of low-threshold InfoFi competitors. The decision
reflects broader industry trends toward targeted user acquisition
rather than global, volume-based reward programs. 

 INTRODUCING $GWEI: THE GOVERNANCE TOKEN FOR ETHGAS (5 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 $GWEI is the governance token for ETHGas' "Realtime Ethereum"
infrastructure. It transforms Ethereum blockspace into a structured,
tradable asset enabling instant execution, budgetable costs, and
gasless user experiences through blockspace commitments,
pre-confirmations, and real-time settlement. The token operates on a
vote-escrow model where staking $GWEI for periods ranging from one
week to four years grants $veGWEI voting power proportional to lock
duration, allowing holders to govern protocol parameters, treasury
allocation, smart contract upgrades, and emergency actions with voting
influence deliberately weighted toward long-term participants over
short-term speculators. ETHGas aims to solve Ethereum's blockspace
allocation inefficiencies that haven't evolved alongside application
development.
