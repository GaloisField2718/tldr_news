 SEC SAYS LIQUID STAKING DOESN'T RUN AFOUL OF SECURITIES LAWS (6
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 The SEC's Division of Corporation Finance clarified in a non-binding
staff statement that liquid staking, where users deposit covered
crypto assets to receive receipt tokens, does not require
securities-law disclosures as long as the assets aren't part of an
investment contract. With nearly $67 billion in TVL across protocols
like Lido, Jito, and Rocket Pool, the market saw only muted price
changes after the announcement. The guidance emphasizes that liquid
staking providers act as agents without entrepreneurial or managerial
efforts in staking decisions. By following this statement, industry
participants should avoid enforcement actions under current securities
laws.
