 EL SALVADOR SPLITS BITCOIN ACROSS 14 WALLETS TO REDUCE QUANTUM RISK
(4 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 El Salvador has transferred its entire Bitcoin reserve of 6,274 BTC
($678 million) from a single wallet into 14 separate addresses, with
each new wallet holding up to 500 BTC. The Central American nation
implemented this security measure as a precaution against potential
future quantum computing threats, which could theoretically crack
Bitcoin's elliptic curve cryptography and compromise funds stored in
addresses with exposed public keys. While quantum computing currently
poses no immediate threat to Bitcoin security, existing quantum
computers are unable to crack even basic encryption keys. Distributing
funds across multiple addresses would minimize the impact of any
potential quantum attack, as public keys are only revealed after BTC
is spent. 

 STRATEGY'S PREFERRED SHARES FORM A BULLISH CIRCLE AROUND BITCOIN (5
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Strategy raised $5.6 billion year-to-date through four preferred
stock offerings (STRK, STRF, STRD, and STRC), representing 12% of all
U.S. IPO issuance in 2025 and cementing its position as the largest
corporate Bitcoin holder with 632,457 BTC. Performance across the
preferred shares has diverged significantly, with STRF leading at 31%
returns, STRK at 19%, STRC at 8%, while STRD posted a negative 6%
return. Despite the company's aggressive Bitcoin accumulation
strategy, MSTR shares have underperformed Bitcoin itself, gaining 13%
year-to-date compared to Bitcoin's 18% rise, as the stock trades at a
1.60x multiple to its Bitcoin net asset value.

INNOVATION & LAUNCHES

 BETH: ETHEREUM'S PROOF OF BURN TOKEN (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Ethereum's new BETH token represents provably burned ETH, formalizing
scarcity and making destruction auditable onchain. Users can burn ETH
via a contract and receive BETH 1:1, turning Ethereum's burn mechanic
into a transferable asset. This opens up new design space for DeFi,
governance, and financial instruments built around proof of burn. 

 ETHEREUM FOUNDATION AIMS TO MAKE LAYER 2S FEEL LIKE ONE CHAIN (5
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 The Ethereum Foundation has unveiled a roadmap to address Layer 2
fragmentation through three workstreams: Initialisation, Acceleration,
and Finalisation, aimed at making cross-L2 activity feel seamless. The
initiative introduces an Open Intents Framework and Ethereum
Interoperability Layer (EIL) to enable trustless cross-chain
transactions, while targeting faster L1 confirmations of 15-30 seconds
and exploring potential slot time reductions from 12 to 6 seconds. Key
deliverables include production contracts for the intents framework by
Q4 2025, broad availability of fast confirmation rules by Q1 2026, and
ongoing research into zero-knowledge proof integration for real-time
settlement.
