 RATE-CUT OPTIMISM FUELS $921M IN GLOBAL CRYPTO FUND INFLOWS (3 MINUTE
READ) [4] 

 Crypto investment products saw $921 million in weekly inflows,
rebounding sharply from the prior week's $513 million outflow. The
increase was driven by rate-cut optimism after lower CPI data and

 A NEW PATH FOR PROPRIETARY AMMS (16 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Proprietary AMMs managed by professional market makers have captured
40%+ of Solana trading volume by updating pricing curves up to 74
times per second, enabled by extremely cheap updates compared to swaps
that allow market makers to achieve Top of Block priority with minimal
fees and avoid front-running. EVM's expensive storage writes versus
swaps create only a 1:10 ratio instead of Solana's 1:1000, making
updates costly and vulnerable to arbitrageur front-running since
outbidding requires only 10x the priority fee. A proposed solution
combines a Global Storage smart contract for price curve parameters
with a builder policy that reserves the first 5-10% of block gas
exclusively for these updates ordered by priority fee, creating a
protected "fast lane" that guarantees Top of Block execution and
establishes a local fee market competing only against other updates
rather than all DeFi transactions.

 WTF IS ORCHESTRATION (OR CLEARING) (OR SETTLEMENT) (8 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Orchestration (coordinating between parties), clearing (reconciling
who owes what), and settlement (actual fund transfers) evolved in
traditional finance to coordinate transactions across networks of
intermediaries maintaining separate ledgers. Crypto's multi-chain
reality has recreated the same coordination challenges that shaped
traditional finance, despite blockchain's promise of instant
settlement on a single shared ledger. The opportunity lies in
rebuilding these functions through smart contracts, cryptographic
proofs, and programmable liquidity rather than institutional
intermediaries, creating systems that are more performant,
upgradeable, and transparent to handle crypto's expected
orders-of-magnitude higher velocity of money.
