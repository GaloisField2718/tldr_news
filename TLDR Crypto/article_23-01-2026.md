 FARCASTER ACQUIRED BY NEYNAR AFTER FOUNDERS PIVOT (4 MINUTE READ) [5]


 Neynar, a Farcaster-focused infrastructure provider, announced it
will acquire Farcaster, a decentralized social protocol. Founders
Varun Srinivasan and Dan Romero will step back from day-to-day
operations. Despite raising $150 million from Paradigm and a16z in
2024, Farcaster acknowledged in December that it failed to achieve
sustainable growth after 4.5 years of pursuing a social-first
approach. The acquisition will transfer ownership of Farcaster's
protocol contracts, repositories, flagship app, and the Clanker
project to Neynar, which will handle protocol governance and
infrastructure operations.

 LASER DIGITAL LAUNCHES TOKENIZED BITCOIN YIELD FUND (4 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Nomura's crypto subsidiary, Laser Digital, launched the Bitcoin
Diversified Yield Fund SP (BDYF), the world's first natively tokenized
Cayman-domiciled Bitcoin yield fund targeting institutional and
accredited investors. The fund aims to generate over 5% excess net
returns above Bitcoin performance through market-neutral strategies,
including arbitrage, lending, and options, while maintaining long-only
BTC exposure with institutional-grade risk controls. Unlike
traditional tokenized fund structures that use SPVs or feeder funds,
BDYF issues tokenized share classes directly at the main fund level,
enabling true on-chain ownership alongside traditional shares.

 CONVEX AND CONCAVE DISPOSITIONS (6 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Vitalik Buterin introduces a decision-making framework that
distinguishes "concave disposition" (favoring compromise/middle
ground) from "convex disposition" (favoring commitment to extremes),
using Bitcoin's all-or-nothing approach to decentralization versus
Ethereum's pragmatic willingness to accept tradeoffs as primary
examples. He argues most real-world decisions are multidimensional
"saddle points" that are simultaneously convex in some dimensions and
concave in others, meaning effective protocol design requires
identifying which aspects demand purity versus which benefit from
balance. The framework challenges the crypto industry's tendency
toward single-dimensional thinking in governance debates.
