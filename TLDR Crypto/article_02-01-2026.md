 JAPAN, SOUTH KOREA LEAD ASIA'S LOCAL STABLECOIN PUSH IN 2025 (4
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Asia spent 2025 building the regulatory and institutional scaffolding
for local-currency stablecoins, even while USD stablecoins still
dominate onchain liquidity. Japan saw a legally recognized yen
stablecoin launch plus megabank pilots supported by the FSA, and South
Korea saw multiple won-pegged initiatives across different chains and
more work toward a formal framework. 

 CRYPTO ETFS HEAD INTO 2026 WITH REGULATORY TAILWINDS (3 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Bitcoin and ether ETFs still captured the vast majority of 2025
inflows, even as dozens of altcoin ETFs launched and expanded the menu
of products. New SEC listing standards can shrink launch timelines to
~75 days from up to ~240, which is fueling a crowded pipeline. The
tension heading into 2026 is that although there will be more
approvals, smaller products will fail to gather durable AUM.

 PENDLE REWRITES LIQUIDITY INCENTIVE RULES (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Pendle's Yield Token (YT) subsidy mechanic lets protocols buy
liquidity more efficiently. Subsidizing YT boosts demand and raises YT
prices, which mechanically pushes Principal Token (PT) prices down (PT
+ YT = underlying). A cheaper PT implies a higher fixed APY, creating
market-leading fixed rates with less incentive spend because subsidies
target the smaller YT slice that captures the full yield stream.

 DEEP DIVE: ALPENGLOW CONSENSUS PROTOCOL (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Consensus protocols form the basis of state machine replication
(SMR), defining how distributed sets of parties reach agreement on
transaction ordering and state updates. Alpenglow represents a new
approach to blockchain consensus that aims to improve on existing
mechanisms in terms of throughput, latency, and finality guarantees.
The protocol's design choices and trade-offs compared to established
consensus mechanisms are state-of-the-art and designed to remedy
common tradeoffs and problems within the consensus mechanism space. 

 DO WE NEED DAT 2.0? (5 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Digital Asset Treasuries (DATs) expanded institutional crypto
adoption in 2025 but underwent valuation-driven consolidation by
year-end, with mNAV compression suggesting the DAT cycle may be
maturing. Future iterations will likely shift away from pure
buy-and-hold strategies toward specialized models focused on
professional trading, storage, and procurement of sovereign block
space, recognizing block space as a vital commodity for the digital
economy. This evolution requires a deep understanding of duration
risks and cyclicality in blockchain economics, and clearer regulatory
frameworks are critical for scaling institutional adoption as
real-world use cases expand through stablecoin payments, tokenization,
and collateral applications in financial transactions.

 WHY PREDICTION MARKETS MATTER: VITALIK'S EXPLANATION (2 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Prediction markets represent a fundamental shift from social media's
broken incentive structure by making wrong predictions financially
costly rather than rewarding extreme, loud opinions. Unlike
traditional information ecosystems, where confidence is free and lies
face no consequences, prediction markets force accountability by
requiring capital commitment behind beliefs. This mechanism creates
healthier information environments where reality ultimately settles
arguments, positioning prediction markets as one of the most honest
coordination tools for the crypto ecosystem.
