 PAYPAY, 40% OWNER OF BINANCE JAPAN, SEEKS UP TO $1.1 BILLION IN IPO

Nasdaq IPO targeting a $10 billion valuation. Backed by SoftBank, the

 VITALIK BUTERIN OUTLINES PLAN TO OVERHAUL ETHEREUM'S EXECUTION LAYER
(3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Vitalik Buterin's two-part proposal aims to modernize Ethereum's
execution layer by implementing binary state trees and transitioning
beyond the current Ethereum Virtual Machine. These changes target
improved scalability and efficiency, fundamentally restructuring how
the network processes transactions and manages state data for
long-term sustainability.

GUIDES & TUTORIALS

 VITALIK ON EIP-8141 (8 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 EIP-8141 is an omnibus account abstraction proposal built around
"Frame Transactions" – bundled call sequences that share calldata
and can independently authorize both a transaction sender and a gas
payer, enabling paymaster contracts to settle fees in any token via
onchain DEX logic with no intermediaries. It addresses
quantum-resistant and multisig validation, ZK-SNARK paymasters for
privacy protocols, 2D nonces for parallel transaction processing, and
a conservative-to-permissive mempool rollout designed to complement
FOCIL's inclusion guarantees. EOA compatibility is under active
discussion, which would extend batch operations and gas sponsorship to
existing accounts without migration. EIP-8141 could ship within a year
via the Hegota fork, culminating over a decade of AA research since
EIP-86 in 2016. 

 HUMANITY'S ORIGINAL STORE OF VALUE (10 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Gold's decoupling from real rates beginning in late 2022 – rallying
from $1,620 to $5,200/oz while real yields held at 1.5-2.5% –
indicates a structural regime shift attributed to fiscal dominance,
with US debt adding roughly $8B/day and net interest costs projected
to exceed the defense budget in 2026. Central banks absorbed 863
tonnes in 2025 alone, yet Western institutional gold allocation
remains near 1% of ETF and fund assets versus a historically
appropriate 5-10%. Over the comparable window in which gold gained
78%, BTC reached an ATH of $126K before retracing 50% to approximately
$67K, and the US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve's $13B contrasts sharply
with $140B+ in annual central bank gold purchasing.

 QUANTUM RESISTANCE ROADMAP FOR ETHEREUM (8 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Vitalik Buterin has published a comprehensive quantum resistance
roadmap addressing four attack surfaces — consensus-layer BLS
signatures, data availability, EOA signatures, and application-layer
ZK proofs — with distinct cryptographic migration paths for each.
The most acute challenge is at the ZK layer, where quantum-resistant
STARKs carry ~10M gas cost versus 300-500K for current SNARKs. The
unifying solution is protocol-layer recursive aggregation via EIP-8141
validation frames, in which a single STARK per block could replace
3-256MB of per-transaction signatures and proofs, with proving
delegated to the mempool layer at roughly 500ms intervals. A
significant open question remains the selection of Ethereum's
long-term hash function — candidates include Poseidon2 with extra
rounds, Poseidon1, and BLAKE3. 

 AGENTIC COMMERCE IS TAKING SHAPE (5 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 There are two competing payment rails for agentic commerce: an
enterprise stack anchored by OpenAI's ACP (with Stripe) and Google's
AP2 (60+ partners including Mastercard and PayPal) targeting
human-assisted purchasing, and a crypto stack led by Coinbase's x402
protocol, which has processed 50M+ USDC transactions on Base and
Solana with volume doubling in February. Coinbase launched Agentic
Wallets with native x402 support, while Virtuals Protocol's ACP
extends the crypto stack into agent-to-agent commerce via
cryptographic Proof of Agreement and smart contract escrow. The
fundamental wedge favoring crypto is settlement efficiency –
stablecoins clear in seconds for fractions of a cent versus card rails
at 2-3% with multi-day settlement. The predicted outcome is a
bifurcated market where enterprise rails dominate human-to-merchant
flows while crypto captures agent-to-agent commerce.
