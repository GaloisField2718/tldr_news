 TORNADO CASH DEV ROMAN STORM FOUND GUILTY ON ONE COUNT (3 MINUTE
READ) [4] 

 A New York jury found Tornado Cash developer Roman Storm guilty of
operating an unlicensed money transmitting business, but they
acquitted him of sanctions violations and were deadlocked on money
laundering charges. The partial verdict spares Storm from a potential
20-year sentence but leaves him facing up to five years. The defense
counsel argued Storm had no control over the decentralized protocol,
which was used for both privacy and illicit purposes. The decision is
seen as a partial win for crypto developers, highlighting the legal
ambiguity around building open-source privacy tools. Sentencing is
pending, and the DOJ may still retry the unresolved charge.
