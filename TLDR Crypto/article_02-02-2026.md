 BITCOIN DROPS BELOW $78K (5 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Bitcoin dropped over 10% Saturday to $75,700, extending a 30%+
decline since April 2025 that erased $111 billion in crypto market cap
and liquidated $1.6 billion in leveraged positions over 24 hours. The
selloff pushed Strategy's bitcoin position underwater below its
$76,037 cost basis, while Bitcoin's realized capitalization has
flatlined, indicating new capital inflows have stopped even as
long-term holders continue taking profits. Bitcoin failed to rally
despite typically bullish catalysts, including a weaker dollar and
gold hitting record highs, with delays in US crypto market-structure
rules further dampening sentiment. Some analysts expect a prolonged
sideways consolidation rather than a swift rebound.

 DOLLAR STABLECOINS ARE A GEOPOLITICAL WEAPON (1 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Dollar stablecoins let the US export dollar liquidity without sending
physical dollars. Foreign firms hand local FX to a US issuer, the
issuer converts it into dollars, buys T-bills, and issues stablecoins.
This creates a COMECON-style settlement loop that funnels dollars into
US Treasuries and could let the US sidestep the Triffin dilemma by
making stablecoins a strategic geopolitical tool.
