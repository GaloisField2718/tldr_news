 COINBASE SEEKS OCC CHARTER TO BRIDGE CRYPTO AND TRADFI (3 MINUTE

 CRYPTO'S ENDGAME: PROOFS AND PRAGMATISM (8 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Zero-knowledge proofs excel in specific contexts like rollup security
and high-stakes nation-state protection but are overkill for everyday
transactions, with proof generation costing around ten cents per
simple action versus near-free signatures, leading to adoption
primarily as invisible infrastructure rather than user-demanded
features. ZKML compounds this problem by stacking GPU-intensive proof
generation on top of GPU-intensive inference, creating costly
solutions seeking problems to solve, while fully homomorphic
encryption still depends on MPC/TSS for distributed key control to
avoid centralized trust points. Smart accounts face fundamental
limitations by trapping automation inside single blockchain state
machines, unable to enforce simple cross-chain rules like daily
spending limits that span multiple networks and contexts where real
user identities operate. The pragmatic alternative uses threshold
cryptography with MPC/TSS+TEE as a layered model where smart accounts
handle narrow on-chain guardrails while threshold systems enforce
sophisticated cross-chain policies, multi-context identity, and
higher-order automation above individual blockchains.

 ETHREX: MINIMALIST L2 WITHOUT THE TOKEN PROBLEM (3 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Layer-2 stacks are structurally unsustainable because their tokens
undermine long-term developer incentives. Once vesting ends, core
teams lose motivation, leaving bloated, hard-to-maintain codebases
behind. Ethrex avoids this trap with a minimal, maintainable design
that functions as both an L1 and L2 client and carries no token,
enabling anyone to launch an L2 with a single command. Its open,
tokenless model and forthcoming documentation aim to make Ethereum
scaling infrastructure fully self-sustaining and community-driven.
