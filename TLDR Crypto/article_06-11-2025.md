 GOVERNMENT SHUTDOWN CONTINUES RISK OF DERAILING CRYPTO BILL (6 MINUTE
READ) [5] 

 The US government shutdown reached a record 36 days on Wednesday,
potentially delaying budget dealmaking and further pushing out crypto
market structure legislation. Policy experts predict that a markup by
Thanksgiving remains possible, but passage by the end of 2025 is
unlikely, with legislation more likely moving through both houses by
the end of 2026, as furloughed government experts leave fewer people
capable of crafting legislative language. Patrick Witt, White House
executive director of the President's Council of Advisors for Digital
Assets, maintains optimism that Trump still wants the bill by end of
2025 and argued the shutdown paradoxically helped by allowing more
time to engage lawmakers on substance "in a way that we might not have
otherwise been able to, had there been a bunch of other competing
priorities." 

 RLUSD TO POWER MASTERCARD SETTLEMENTS ON XRP LEDGER (5 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Ripple partnered with Mastercard, WebBank, and Gemini to pilot RLUSD
stablecoin settlement for credit card transactions on the XRP Ledger,
potentially marking the first time a regulated US bank settles fiat
card payments via stablecoin on a public blockchain. RLUSD launched in
December 2024 under a New York Trust Charter with full cash backing
and surpassed $1 billion in circulation, aiming to replace traditional
1-3 day settlement rails with near-instant blockchain settlement while
maintaining compliance. WebBank, issuer of the Gemini Credit Card,
will explore settling Mastercard transactions using RLUSD, with
onboarding beginning in the coming months, pending regulatory
approvals.

 OPENFX HITS $34B TPV AS CROSS-BORDER PAYMENTS SURGE (2 MINUTE READ)
[12] 

 OpenFX has reached an annualized $34B in total payments volume (TPV)
after adding $14B in October alone, more than its previous five months
combined. The firm's rapid growth is fueled by its Latin American
expansion, including corridors in Mexico, Argentina, Brazil, and
Colombia, where 86% of transactions now settle in under 10 minutes
despite volatile FX and regulatory conditions. OpenFX distinguishes
its platform from ‚Äúinstant settlement‚Äù competitors by proving real
institutional-scale settlements in minutes, not hours. Next, the
company is expanding into Southeast Asia (Hong Kong, Singapore, and
the Philippines) in Q1 2026, applying its deep liquidity, local rail
integrations, and automated treasury model. 

 SOLANA'S VALIDATOR COUNT FALLS SHARPLY AS ETHEREUM EXPANDS (3 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Solana's validator count has dropped over 60%, falling from roughly
2,500 in early 2023 to under 900 today, raising fresh concerns about
the network's decentralization and security. The decline, down 33%
year-to-date, reflects rising hardware costs, high bandwidth demands,
and limited economic incentives for smaller validators. In contrast,
Ethereum continues to grow its validator base, now nearing 1 million
active validators, securing billions in real economic activity and
attracting new entrants daily. While some chains scale by compromising
decentralization, Ethereum's modular architecture allows it to scale
through L2s without sacrificing security, reinforcing its position as
the world's trusted settlement layer.
