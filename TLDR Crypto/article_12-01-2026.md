 EIP-8004: ROAD TO MAINNET (4 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 EIP-8004 proposes establishing Ethereum as a coordination layer for
autonomous AI agents through three on-chain registries: identity for
agent identification, reputation for tracking behavioral history and
trustworthiness, and validation for verifying capabilities and
permissions. The specification has progressed from v0.4 to v1.0 as it
matures with community input. This standard would enable agents to
transact and coordinate with verifiable credentials and reputation
systems built directly into Ethereum's base layer.

 PAYMENT TOKENIZATION ISN'T ONE-SIZE-FITS-ALL (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 There are three distinct payment token types: network tokens
(Visa/Mastercard, best for recurring payments), Payment service
provider tokens (Stripe/Adyen, secure but processor-locked), and
digital wallet tokens (Apple Pay/Google Pay, great UX but not the best
for subscriptions). Confusing them can lead to vendor lock-in, failed
payments, or lost revenue. As payments scale globally, choosing the
right tokenization strategy is a growth and reliability decision, not
just a security upgrade. 

 HOW I MADE 152% APY ON STABLECOINS (5 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 A liquidity provider on ZKP2P generated 152% APY over 9 months by
earning spreads on peer-to-peer fiat-to-crypto conversions, producing
$12.7K yield on an average $6.3K USDC deposit through 276 capital
recycling loops totaling $1.57M in volume. The strategy involved
setting above-market rates for USDC sales, receiving fiat payments
verified via zero-knowledge proofs, then moving funds back onchain
through Monerium and redepositing, with 32% of cycles completing in
under 6 hours enabling multiple daily rotations. Market maturation is
evident as competition compressed EUR spreads from 2-3% in March 2025
to 0.5-1% currently, while ZKP2P processed over $20M total volume in
2025.

 HOW I GOT DRAINED AFTER USING PUBLIC HOTEL WI-FI (4 MINUTE READ) [10]


 A crypto user experienced a complete wallet drainage one day after
connecting to public Wi-Fi at a high-end hotel during a holiday stay,
demonstrating that even reputable establishments present significant
security risks for digital asset holders. The incident serves as a
stark reminder that public networks at any venue can expose users to
attack vectors capable of compromising wallet security, including
man-in-the-middle attacks, malicious captive portals, and session
hijacking. The timing of the breach suggests the attacker may have
harvested credentials or session data during the Wi-Fi connection for
later exploitation. 

 THE THREE GAMES OF STABLECOIN FINTECH (4 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Building stablecoin-native financial services requires competing on
three simultaneous fronts: achieving feature parity with incumbent
neobanks like Mercury and Brex, architecting systems that are
stablecoin-first from the ground up rather than retrofitting crypto
onto legacy infrastructure, and innovating on capabilities that only
blockchain rails can enable. There is a unique competitive challenge
facing crypto fintech builders, who must meet existing user
expectations from traditional fintech while also delivering novel
onchain functionality. Success in stablecoin financial services
requires both defensive parity plays and offensive innovation beyond
what traditional payment rails allow.
