mining network growth and institutional credibility through mining

 7 DEADLY SINS OF TOKENOMICS (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Overinflated fully diluted valuations at launch that create immediate
downside risk and discourage organic entry, low-float traps with tiny
circulating supply creating artificial scarcity that pumps early but
crashes when steep unlocks begin, and insider-dominated allocations
exceeding 60% to VCs and teams with weak lockups that prioritize
extraction over community alignment. Additional failures include
utility theater, where tokens serve only governance or inflationary
staking without real demand sinks, emissions without corresponding
burn mechanisms that reward mercenary farmers who dump immediately,
liquidity starvation through inadequate market-making and shallow
pools that prevent scalable trading, and narrative disconnection where
tokenomics don't align with product models or ecosystem narratives.
These design flaws create predictable failure patterns, including
retail exit liquidity scenarios, unsustainable hype cycles followed by
sharp crashes, perpetual sell pressure from misaligned insiders,
speculative churn without structural demand, TVL death spirals when
rewards fade, high slippage preventing institutional participation,
and token irrelevance due to lack of coherence between narrative,
product, and token mechanics. 

 STABLECOINS 2030: WEB3 TO WALL STREET (45 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Citi has revised its 2030 stablecoin issuance forecasts upward to
$1.9 trillion base case and $4 trillion bull case, driven by 40%
year-to-date growth, regulatory clarity from the GENIUS Act, and
accelerating institutional adoption following what the bank calls
blockchain's "ChatGPT moment." The growth drivers include partial
reallocation of USD bank deposits and offshore holdings, continued
crypto market expansion, and substitution of physical banknotes, with
stablecoins potentially supporting $100 trillion in transaction
activity by 2030 at 50x velocity similar to traditional payment rails.
Citi expects bank tokens like tokenized deposits to potentially exceed
stablecoin transaction volumes, reaching $100-140 trillion annually if
just 5% of global large-value payments migrate onchain, as multiple
digital money formats will coexist rather than compete in a "format
war." Challenges include fragmentation and interoperability issues,
privacy concerns on public blockchains, scalability limitations for
large-value transactions, and the need for accounting recognition as
cash equivalents. Emerging solutions include zero-knowledge proofs and
the rush by Circle, Stripe, Bitfinex, and others to build specialized
Layer-1 blockchains for payments infrastructure.

 EVERYONE'S WRONG ABOUT STABLECOINS VS VISA (9 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 The comparison between stablecoins and Visa/Mastercard reflects a
fundamental misunderstanding of payment architecture, as these
technologies operate at completely different layers, with stablecoins
competing against ACH and SWIFT settlement infrastructure rather than
payment networks that handle authorization and routing. When you swipe
a credit card, no money moves initially - only data flows through
Visa's messaging network for authorization, while actual settlement
happens days later through traditional banking rails that are slow,
expensive, and operate only during business hours. Stablecoins operate
at the settlement layer where authorization and settlement occur
simultaneously in atomic blockchain transactions, offering 97-99% cost
reduction and 100x faster finality compared to traditional wire
transfers, which is why Visa and Mastercard are integrating
stablecoins into their settlement infrastructure rather than fighting
them. Crypto-native payment products like Base Pay, Solana Pay, and
Coinbase Commerce compete directly with card networks by offering
complete payment solutions with lower merchant fees but sacrifice
consumer protections like chargebacks and dispute resolution that
remain critical for mainstream adoption.
