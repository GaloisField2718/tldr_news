 STABLECOINS DO CREATE CREDIT, JUST NOT LIKE BANKS (4 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Stablecoins, such as Circle's USDC, actively create credit through
their reserves, including treasuries (loans to the government), repos
(secured loans to financial institutions), and bank deposits (loans to
banks). This structure qualifies as credit creation, even without
fractional lending. Banks account for just 20% of U.S. credit, with
trillions more generated through money market funds and securitized
debt like MBS. Narrow-bank stablecoins are less prone to runs, need no
bailouts, and offer safer intermediation, making them a financial
upgrade, not a threat.
