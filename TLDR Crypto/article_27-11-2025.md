GUIDES & TUTORIALS

 NARRATIVE > REVENUE (11 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Despite profitable companies like Pumpfun reaching $100 million
revenue in six months and Hyperliquid generating $875 million with 11
employees, revenue shows weak correlation with token prices.
Hyperliquid's $3 million daily buybacks correlate 0.7 with HYPE price,
but PUMP's $460 million projected annual buybacks correlate only 0.38
with price at a 3.7x revenue multiple, while Uniswap's fees show -0.14
correlation with UNI despite the fee switch proposal spiking price 43%
to $10 before settling at a 35x revenue multiple. The revenue meta
disconnect mirrors traditional markets where TSLA trades at 260x P/E
and PLTR at 350x P/E as the average S&P 500 P/E ratio doubled from 13
in 2011 to 30 today, with revenue mattering less in bubbles and more
in bear markets as 2025 marks crypto's first year with multiple
projects delivering hundreds of millions to token holders. 

 CRYPTO IS PRICED FOR NETWORK EFFECTS IT DOESN'T HAVE (13 MINUTE READ)
[9] 

 Crypto is valued at $2,500-$23,000 per user versus Meta's $400-$500
per user, despite lacking comparable retention, monetization, or
network effects, with most crypto "network effects" being negative
congestion effects where more users increase fees and slow
transactions. L1s capture 90% of total market cap but only 12% of
fees, down from 60%, while DeFi generates 73% of fees but represents
under 10% of valuation, contradicting the fat protocol thesis as value
migrates to app and user aggregation layers. Metcalfe's Law analysis
reveals crypto's k coefficient is 10-1,000x higher than Facebook's,
meaning each user is priced vastly more valuable despite lower
stickiness as liquidity remains mercenary, developers churn through
forkable codebases, and retention fails to compound across cycles.
Crypto might grow into powerful network effects if it becomes an
internet settlement fabric, but it is currently far overvalued.

MISCELLANEOUS

 WHY CAPITAL IS LEAVING BITCOIN (15 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Bitcoin functioned as a regulatory battering ram that survived where
E-gold failed by removing centralized attack vectors, but its three
trillion dollar valuation collapses now that tokenized US stocks,
gold, and stablecoins are legal with several hundred billion in market
cap. The monopoly dissolved as stablecoins migrated from Bitcoin to
Ethereum to Tron treating blockchains as disposable plumbing, while
Nasdaq outperformed Bitcoin over four-year cycles as risk premium
vanished and developer commits plunged to 2017 levels. People prefer
Tether's ability to freeze stolen funds and recover misplaced tokens
over Bitcoin's permanent irreversibility, revealing the market wants
powerful rails with error correction rather than pure code-is-law
decentralization. 

 MAPPING THE FIRST WAVE OF AGENT PAYMENTS (20 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 x402 payment infrastructure analysis shows USDC accounts for 99% of
volume, with Base handling 89% of transactions, while agent-like
wallets generate 90% of transaction count. Coinbase leads facilitator
transaction volume, but Questflow routes to the widest recipient set,
positioning itself as the long-tail marketplace aggregator versus
vertical SaaS-style competitors. USDC's
ERC-3009/transferWithAuthorization standard enables the
authorization-then-settle flow optimized for Coinbase and Base
infrastructure, though this concentration creates platform risk as
agents dominate micro-payment volumes while humans execute fewer,
larger-ticket transactions.
