 THE INTERNET'S BLUEPRINT FOR ETHEREUM (30 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 You can't value Ethereum like a company or a normal L1. Instead, you
have to treat it like the internet's base protocols (TCP/IP, DNS). It
is a public good, so its main value comes from enabling everyone else.
Ethereum should be valued based on captured value, Ethereum-dependent
economic flow, and trust surplus. Captured value is the current market
cap of ETH + L2s + DeFi blue chips, which is ~$0.6-0.9T today.
Ethereum-dependent economic flows are like digital GDP, which is the
annual value of activity that actually relies on Ethereum (e.g.,
stablecoins, tokenized assets, DeFi, NFTs, etc. which is capitalized
at ~$0.3-3T. Trust surplus is the invisible value from lower fraud,
counterparty risk, and rent extraction. It is estimated to be around
$0.15-0.6T.

Hearn's 2016 Bitcoin exit over centralization concerns, both stemming

 ETHEREUM'S IRREPLICABLE MOAT & WWIII-RESISTANT SETTLEMENT (3 MINUTE
READ) [10] 

 Crypto natives are undervaluing how uniquely hard it would be to
recreate Ethereum's politically neutral, globally shared settlement
layer. The historical window that allowed Bitcoin and Ethereum to
bootstrap legitimacy with open, general-purpose launches is closed, so
new L1s are competing under very different, more financialized
conditions. Meanwhile, institutional behavior is quietly confirming
the thesis that when banks, fintechs, and tokenization projects pick a
base chain, they increasingly default to Ethereum for its reliability,
neutrality, and minimized counterparty risk. As more assets, rollups,
and devs pile onto the EVM and rollup stack, its network effects
compound, and with Fusaka as well as sharper EF execution, Ethereum's
moat is widening rather than eroding.
