 ETHEREUM FOUNDATION RESEARCHERS WARN OF STORAGE BURDENÂ (3 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 Ethereum's state only grows. It is getting harder and more expensive
for nodes to store, serve, and sync. Centralization is a risk if only
large operators can run full infra. There are three potential initial
directions: State Expiry (prune inactive state with proof-based
revival), State Archive (separate hot vs. cold state so node
performance stays stable), and Partial Statelessness (nodes hold
subsets while clients/wallets cache what they need). 

 BITCOIN'S 'REALIZED CAP' HOLDS AT RECORD HIGH OVER $1 TRILLION (5
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Bitcoin's realized cap hit a record $1.125 trillion and continued
rising through the 36% correction from October's all-time high,
valuing each bitcoin at its last-moved price to show actual capital
inflows rather than speculative market cap. The metric's resilience
contrasts with 2022's bear market, when realized cap fell from $470
billion to $385 billion during capitulation, with a similar pause
during April 2025's tariff tantrum preceding new highs. Bitcoin
materially underprices the supportive macro backdrop of resilient
global growth, ongoing rate cuts, and steepening yield curves that
weaken the dollar, casting doubt on the four-year cycle narrative with
potential 2026 upside.

enabling Coinbase's "universal exchange" strategy expanding beyond
spot crypto into derivatives, tokenized assets, and prediction
