delta-neutral strategies through xTokens ($xHYPE, $xBTC, and $xETH)

efficiency. The system offers two product lines: Customized for
bespoke self-custody strategies and Tokenized for pooled, scalable
positions that can serve as collateral or yield sources for retail
users, institutions, and protocols.

 ZORA FLYWHEEL EXPLAINED (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Zora's tokenomics flywheel operates through a 3% trading fee
structure (1% each to creator, platform, and LP) where all assets are
paired with $ZORA, creating consistent buy pressure of 2.5% on every
trade through fees and token sinks. The model addresses a critical
flaw in Virtuals' design by routing all trading volume through
canonical pools, preventing the liquidity fragmentation that weakened
Virtuals when mature tokens migrated to more efficient venues like
Uniswap v3. Despite concerns about the 3% fee sustainability,
precedent exists in NFT markets that sustained $5B monthly volumes
with ~10% fees, and current Solana meme coin trading effectively
approaches 3% when factoring in platform fees, interface costs, and
MEV. Trading volume of Zora coins will be the key performance
indicator as this flywheel mechanism scales, with the token sink
remaining active throughout a creator coin's entire lifecycle rather
than just during initial launch phases.

 THE STATE OF CRYPTO LEVERAGE â€“ Q2 2025 (30 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Crypto leverage expanded significantly across all major sectors in Q2
2025, with DeFi lending reaching an all-time high of $26.47 billion
(+42.11% QoQ), CeFi lending growing to $17.78 billion (+14.66% QoQ),
and futures open interest surging to $132.6 billion (+37.47% QoQ).
Growth was driven by three primary factors: reflexivity between rising
asset prices and borrowing activity, increased demand from digital
asset treasury companies, and the scaling of capital-efficient
collateral strategies in DeFi, particularly Ethena's Liquid Leverage
program with Aave and Pendle principal token looping strategies. The
quarter highlighted DeFi's systemic importance when large ETH
withdrawals from Aave caused borrowing rates to spike above staking
yields, creating a cascading effect that pushed Ethereum's unstaking
queue to a record 13 days. Market concentration remains high, with
Tether commanding 57% of CeFi lending and the combined leverage
ecosystem reaching $53.09 billion in total crypto-collateralized
lending, approaching levels last seen in early 2022.
