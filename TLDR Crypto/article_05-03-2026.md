 KRAKEN WINS DIRECT ACCESS TO THE FED'S PAYMENT RAILS (3 MINUTE READ)
[4] 

 Kraken's Wyoming-chartered banking arm has secured a Federal Reserve
master account, granting it direct access to Fedwire and allowing it
to settle US dollar transfers without intermediary banks. The move
marks the first time a crypto-native firm has plugged into the Fed's
core payment infrastructure, potentially speeding up fiat flows for
institutional clients and strengthening Kraken's positioning ahead of
its planned IPO. 

 TETHER INVESTS IN EIGHT SLEEP, PLANS QVAC INTEGRATION (2 MINUTE READ)

 Tether has announced a strategic investment in Eight Sleep, a sleep

stablecoin issuer's ongoing diversification beyond its core USDT

positions QVAC as a commercializable layer for personalized health
data processing, with Eight Sleep serving as a go-to-market vehicle
for the platform's real-world applications in sleep tracking,
recovery, and longevity monitoring.

 ACHIEVING QUANTUM SAFETY THROUGH EPHEMERAL KEY PAIRS (5 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Ethereum wallets can achieve quantum safety without protocol changes
by leveraging account abstraction. This design makes ECDSA key pairs
single-use, rotating the signer during every transaction while keeping
the contract address constant. It eliminates long-term public key
exposure, protecting assets from future quantum computing threats.

 HOW CIRCLE'S NANOPAYMENTS WORK (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Circle's Nanopayments enables USDC transfers as small as $0.000001 by
having buyers sign offchain EIP-3009 authorization messages routed
through the Circle Gateway, which aggregates thousands of those
signatures and settles them in a single periodic onchain batch
transaction, reducing effective gas costs to near-zero. The buyer's
initial deposit is the only onchain transaction, with subsequent
payments locked against balance in the Gateway and fulfilled
immediately upon signature verification, enabling real-time resource
delivery via the x402 protocol. The Circle Gateway operates inside an
AWS Nitro Enclave TEE, with batch signing keys protected by AWS KMS
and inaccessible to Circle personnel, providing a verifiable trust
boundary without requiring a decentralized sequencer. 

 SEC SUBMITS CRYPTO SECURITIES FRAMEWORK TO WHITE HOUSE (3 MINUTE
READ) [9] 

 The SEC submitted commission-level interpretive guidance to the White
House outlining how federal securities laws apply to crypto assets,
currently undergoing interagency review at OIRA. The guidance likely
focuses on establishing a "token taxonomy" to categorize which crypto
assets qualify as securities, affecting how firms register, disclose,
and operate. Unlike staff-level statements, commission-level guidance
is considered more enforceable and does not require a commission vote.
Separately, the CFTC submitted a prediction markets measure to OIRA,
with Chairman Selig announcing plans for an advanced notice of
proposed rulemaking to set clear standards for event contract markets
amid state-federal jurisdictional disputes.

 CRYPTO CARD SPEND UP AS ADOPTION EXPANDS BEYOND EMERGING MARKETS (2
MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Fireblocks' latest Stablecoin Signals highlights that monthly crypto
card spend surpassed $115M in January 2025, up from roughly $25M a
year earlier, with new players like Ether.fi gaining traction
alongside established providers. The data challenges the narrative
that crypto cards are confined to developing regions, showing
accelerating adoption across EEA countries and rapid growth in Asia,
Europe, and Latin America as providers like Gnosis Pay, RedotPay, and
Avici Money scale programs. 

 FATF WARNS STABLECOINS DOMINATE ILLICIT ACTIVITY (3 MINUTE READ) [11]


 FATF's March 2026 report identified stablecoins as the dominant
vehicle for illicit crypto activity, accounting for 84% of the $154
billion in illicit virtual asset transaction volume recorded in 2025,
with Iran and North Korea cited as primary state-linked actors.
Sanctions evasion represented 86% of illicit crypto flows, with
peer-to-peer transfers via unhosted wallets flagged as a critical AML
vulnerability enabling activity to bypass compliance controls. FATF's
recommended remediation framework includes mandatory AML obligations
for stablecoin issuers, wallet freezing capabilities, restrictions on
certain smart-contract functions, and enhanced oversight of unhosted
wallet activity.
