 MEMECOINS LEAD CRYPTO MARKET GAINS (5 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Bitcoin remains range-bound below $70,000 while speculative assets
drive market gains, with the AI tokens leading the sector. Derivatives

 GENIUS ACT AND MICA WILL SPLIT STABLECOINS (6 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 The GENIUS Act and MiCA are creating a bifurcated stablecoin market
between Tier-1 "constitutional cash" tokens with strict redemption
rights, liquid reserves, and no-yield provisions designed to prevent
bank-like runs, and Tier-2 "synthetic cash" products that behave like
money in calm markets but reprice as risk assets during redemption
stress. MiCA reserves the authority to throttle stablecoins that
achieve excessive payment ubiquity as a financial stability measure,
while GENIUS explicitly bans yield to prevent digital dollars from
mimicking uninsured deposits. The most critical unresolved
vulnerability is cross-border "multi-issuance" structures where single
stablecoin brands are issued by multiple legal entities across
jurisdictions, creating concentration risk if global redemption runs
overwhelm the jurisdiction with the strongest legal rights.

 Ledger Wallet will integrate OKX DEX, allowing users to swap tokens

 THE ZKEVM IS THE NEW 'MERGE' (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 zkEVM is Ethereum's new unifying narrative. It should replace the
rollup-centric roadmap now that zkEVM technology has progressed faster
than anticipated. The zkEVM would enable Ethereum's L1 to accept
validity proofs of EVM execution rather than re-executing blocks,
delivering an order-of-magnitude scalability improvement with low fees
and fast transactions directly on mainnet. Unlike previous technical
upgrades, this would directly address user experience by bringing
massive scalability to L1 itself rather than pushing activity to L2s.

 ENSV2 TO DEPLOY ON ETHEREUM L1, NAMECHAIN CANCELLED (4 MINUTE READ)
[10] 

 ENS announced ENSv2 will deploy exclusively on Ethereum L1, canceling
plans for Namechain L2 while maintaining its product roadmap,
including per-name registries and expanded customization. The protocol
will enable one-click .eth registration from any EVM chain without
bridging, alongside continued non-EVM support for Solana and Bitcoin.
Vitalik Buterin has endorsed the decision, arguing that ENS represents
central Ethereum ecosystem state functioning as a semi-financial
application where L1 placement maximizes accessibility and aligns with
an optimal decentralized identity architecture that keeps core
account/profile data on mainnet while routing user actions to
specialized L2s.
