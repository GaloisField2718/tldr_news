MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Circle earned $625M in Q2 interest income from USDC reserves but paid

 $3.5B LUBIAN BITCOIN THEFT WENT UNDETECTED FOR 5 YEARS (5 MINUTE
READ) [5] 

 Arkham Intelligence claims to have uncovered evidence of the theft of
127,426 BTC from Chinese mining pool LuBian in December 2020, which
would represent the largest cryptocurrency heist in history at $3.5
billion at the time of theft, now valued at $14.5 billion. The breach
went undetected for nearly five years, with neither LuBian nor the
suspected hacker ever publicly acknowledging the incident, though
Arkham discovered over 1,500 onchain messages from LuBian pleading for
the funds' return. The theft likely resulted from weak private key
generation that enabled a brute-force attack, with the stolen Bitcoin
remaining largely dormant except for a wallet consolidation in July
2024. LuBian controlled nearly 6% of Bitcoin's hash rate in 2020. The
scale of this theft exceeds other notorious hacks like Mt. Gox and
Bitfinex by nominal value at the time of loss.

 STABLECOIN ADOPTION ISN'T A TECH PROBLEM, IT'S AN INCENTIVE PROBLEM
(2 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Stablecoin adoption is less about lower fees or faster settlement,
and more about solving a multiplayer coordination game. Each
transaction touches users, banks, and processors, none of whom want to
move first due to switching costs like lost float or operational
retraining. To succeed, builders must find wedge use cases where a
single actor (like a cardholder) can adopt unilaterally and extract
clear value. Stablecoin cards, for instance, grow faster than merchant
rails because the user doesn't need to wait for ecosystem buy-in.
Incentives, not just infrastructure, ultimately determine stablecoin
success.
