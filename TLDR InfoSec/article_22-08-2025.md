ATTACKS & VULNERABILITIES

 ORANGE BELGIUM DISCLOSES DATA BREACH IMPACTING 850,000 CUSTOMERS (2
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Attackers breached Orange Belgium's IT systems in July, stealing
personal data of 850,000 customers, including names, phone numbers,
SIM card numbers, PUK codes, and tariff plans. The company detected
the breach at the end of July and confirmed that passwords, emails,
and financial information were not compromised. Orange Belgium
identified the threat group responsible, but hasn't disclosed its
identity. The company confirmed this was unrelated to China's Salt
Typhoon attacks targeting telecoms worldwide. 

 APPLE PATCHES CVE-2025-43300 ZERO-DAY IN IOS, IPADOS, AND MACOS
EXPLOITED IN TARGETED ATTACKS (1 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Apple has patched CVE-2025-43300, a zero-day exploit in the ImageIO
framework that causes memory corruption via malicious images. It
affected iOS 18.6.2, iPadOS 18.6.2/17.7.10, and macOS Ventura 13.7.8,
Sonoma 14.7.8, and Sequoia 15.6.1. This is Apple's seventh zero-day
patch in 2025. 

 TPG TELECOM ESTIMATES 280K AFFECTED BY SUBSIDIARY BREACH (2 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 TPG Telecom's subsidiary iiNet suffered a data breach due to a stolen
employee login, exposing the data of around 280,000 customers. The
exposed information includes emails, phone numbers, addresses, and
some setup passwords. The breach was contained, and affected customers
are being notified.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 RUSSIA-LINKED EUROPEAN ATTACKS RENEW CONCERNS OVER WATER
CYBERSECURITY (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Russian hackers probably sabotaged a Norwegian dam in April by
remotely opening valves for four hours. Poland also reported a
thwarted cyberattack that could have cut water to a major city. These
attacks, linked to groups like Z-Pentest Alliance, show water
facilities as vulnerable targets that could lead to bigger threats.
Water utilities should boost cybersecurity with more funding,
consulting, or free resources like the DEF CON Franklin project.

MISCELLANEOUS

 COPILOT BROKE YOUR AUDIT LOG, BUT MICROSOFT WON'T TELL YOU (8 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 Microsoft's Copilot AI allowed users to access files in M365 without
leaving audit log records, creating potential security and compliance
risks. This was reported to Microsoft, which quietly fixed the issue
but chose not to notify customers or issue a formal security alert.
Any organization using Copilot before August 18 may have incomplete
audit logs and might be unaware of what happened. 

 HACKERS WHO EXPOSED NORTH KOREAN GOVERNMENT HACKER EXPLAIN WHY THEY
DID IT (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Two ethical hackers accessed a North Korean state hacker's computer
for months and uncovered evidence of cyberespionage and theft. They
leaked their findings to support cyber defense and expose
government-backed hacking activities, hoping their action would
inspire more transparency and accountability in global cybersecurity. 

 YOU DON'T NEED A VENDOR TO AUTOMATE SECURITY QUESTIONNAIRES (7 MINUTE
READ) [16] 

 Security questionnaires are a standard part of the security review
process that can often be tedious, time-consuming, and mechanical for
vendors' teams. Instead of buying a tool to handle this process,
vendors can either build their own RAG or use Claude/ChatGPT. To use
Claude/ChatGPT, prepare a folder with the relevant documents that
would be included in a Trust Center, as well as some previously
manually completed questionnaires.
