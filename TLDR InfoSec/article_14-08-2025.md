ATTACKS & VULNERABILITIES

 INTERLOCK RANSOMWARE GROUP LEAKS 43GB OF DATA IN CITY OF ST. PAUL
CYBERATTACK (3 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 The Interlock ransomware group attacked the City of St. Paul,
disrupting payments and essential services. The city refused to pay
the ransom and launched Operation Secure St. Paul with support from
the FBI and National Guard while investigating the breach. Interlock
claims to have leaked 43GB of HR, financial, and identification
documents, prompting a citywide password reset and warnings about
increased phishing risks. 

 HACKERS LEAK 2.8M SENSITIVE RECORDS FROM ALLIANZ LIFE IN SALESFORCE
DATA BREACH (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Threat actors linked to ShinyHunters have leaked 2.8 million records
from Allianz Life's Salesforce CRM, exposing personal details
including names, addresses, birth dates, and Tax IDs of customers and
business partners. The July 16 breach occurred through social
engineering attacks targeting the third-party cloud CRM system,
affecting the majority of Allianz Life's 1.4 million customers. The
attackers have formed a joint Telegram channel with other groups,
including Scattered Spider and Lapsus$, to claim credit for this and
other major Salesforce-related breaches. 

 NEW DOWNGRADE ATTACK CAN BYPASS FIDO AUTH IN MICROSOFT ENTRA ID (2
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 ProofPoint researchers discovered a new downgrade attack on Entra ID
that downgrades the MFA from FIDO to a less secure fallback. The
attack uses a custom evilgnix attacker-in-the-middle module to spoof a
browser agent that lacks FIDO support, such as Safari for Windows. It
can be prevented by disabling fallback methods.

 DETECTION ENGINEERING: PRACTICING DETECTION-AS-CODE (6 MINUTE READ)

 RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT HACKERS SAID TO BE BEHIND US FEDERAL COURT FILING
SYSTEM HACK (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Officials blame Russian government hackers for breaching the U.S.
federal court filing system (PACER), reportedly searching mid-level
criminal cases, including some with Russian/Eastern European surnames,
with stolen data including sealed dockets and informant identities.
The judiciary confirmed the cyberattack and is enhancing security. 

 NEW YORK CLAIMS ZELLE'S SHODDY SECURITY ENABLED A BILLION DOLLARS IN
SCAMS (3 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 New York Attorney General Letitia James is suing Zelle's owner, Early
Warning Services, alleging the platform's weak verification and
safeguards enabled over $1 billion in scams through 2023. The suit
cites lax registration, slow fraud reporting, misleading â€œsafeâ€
marketing, and poor scammer removal. Zelle says the suit is a
political stunt and claims it has strong anti-fraud efforts.
