NEWS & TRENDS

 FIX DELIVERY FIRST (7 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Product transformations fail when teams chase OKRs, discovery,
strategy, or AI before fixing delivery. If teams cannot ship quickly
and reliably, everything else becomes slower, weaker, and harder to
trust. 

 CUSTOMER DEVELOPMENT HAS AN AI PROBLEM (4 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI makes it easy to build too much, too fast. Product teams need
clearer outcomes, direct customer validation and the discipline to
stop work that does not move toward a Minimum Productive Outcome. 

🚀 

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 INTENT IS THE DESIGN SUPERPOWER AI CAN'T REPLACE (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 AI makes prototyping fast, but speed is no longer the designer's
advantage. The real edge is intent: understanding the problem clearly
before using AI to build. 

 APPLICATION PERFORMANCE IS A PRODUCT REQUIREMENT (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Application performance is a product requirement. Product and
engineering must define what “fast enough” means for key user
journeys, then balance performance work against the need to keep the
system changeable. 

🧑‍💻 

RESOURCES & TOOLS

 HOW VULNERABLE IS YOUR IDENTITY STACK? (SPONSOR) [9] 

 Many ID verification providers are "black boxes" built on third-party
wrappers, leading to hidden latencies and brittle integrations. LEARN
WHY A VERTICALLY-INTEGRATED STACK [9] IS ESSENTIAL to eliminate
vendor-on-vendor lag, ensure architectural integrity, and truly own
your user's trust by verifying the tech behind your verifier. Read the
blog here. [9] 

 HIDDEN TECHNICAL DEBT OF AI SYSTEMS: AGENT HARNESS (15 MINUTE READ)
[10] 

 Agent harnesses are hidden AI technical debt. As models improve,
today's prompts, workflows, and tool wrappers will become obsolete, so
teams should keep harnesses thin, invest in durable skills and evals,
and build systems they can replace easily. 

 THE COMPUTERS TALK TO US NOW (4 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Computers can now talk to us and do real skilled work. Software,
teams, and organizations must adapt to a world where agents are no
longer just tools, but active participants in how systems get built
and used. 

🎁 

MISCELLANEOUS

 ESCAPE FROM AGENTIC LOOP (5 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI agent orchestration can become “doom building,” draining
attention while feeling productive. Most agent work should be
supervised through clear scopes and review blocks, while staying
deeply involved only in the creative work that needs human judgment. 

 MINIMALLY VIABLE CONSISTENCY (5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Organizations need enough consistency to stay aligned, but not so
much that teams lose flexibility. AI can help translate across
different ways of working, as long as it does not replace the human
sensemaking that creates real alignment.
