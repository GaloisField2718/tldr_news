TLDR PRODUCT MANAGEMENT 2026-04-17

 WHY ZENDESK AND POSTMAN STOPPED BUILDING INTEGRATIONS IN-HOUSE
(SPONSOR) [4] 

 Your customers expect your product or AI agent to work with the tools
they already use (like Slack or Salesforce). But every integration
your team builds means owning the auth, the webhooks, the schema
changes, and the maintenance indefinitely.

That's why product teams at Zendesk and Postman chose Paragon as their
integration platform rather than building integrations themselves.

This Build vs. Buy guide [4] gives product leaders a clear-eyed look
at the real tradeoffs so you can make the right call before it lands
on the roadmap.

 A PLATFORM IS A PLACE MORE THAN A PRODUCT (38 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Platforms are not just products - they are environments that shape
how teams grow and operate. Success comes from building a system that
makes teams more capable, not just more compliant. 

 AI'S DESIGN PROBLEM IS ORGANIZATIONAL, NOT TECHNOLOGICAL (20 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 AI's core problem is organizational, not technical. The winners will
design workflows that make AI usable, trustworthy, and effective in
real teams.

RESOURCES & TOOLS

 WHY DASHBOARDS GET IGNORED (SPONSOR) [9] 

 Even when dashboards are embedded, usage still drops off. The issue
isn't access, it's action. Analytics show what's happening but leave
users figuring out what to do next. In this blog [10], Reveal breaks
down why that gap kills adoption and what teams are doing to close it.
See how teams are turning insight into action → [10] 

 BUILD TO LEARN VS BUILD TO EARN (6 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Product success now depends on building to learn before building to
earn. The best teams prototype rapidly to discover what works, rather
than just shipping faster. 

 INSIDE LENNY RACHITSKY'S DEMANDINGLY CHILL LIFE (18 MINUTE READ) [12]


 Lenny Rachitsky's success comes from obsessive focus on quality and
saying no. His “chill life” is built on disciplined prioritization
and relentless standards.

 FOUR MODELS FOR CREDIT GOVERNANCE: HIERARCHICAL SPEND CONTROL (10
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 AI credit systems need hierarchy, real-time enforcement, and clear
visibility. The best designs unify decisions, track usage, and let
