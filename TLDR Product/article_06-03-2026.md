NEWS & TRENDS

 EVERY PRODUCT STARTS IN OKLAHOMA (4 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Early products succeed by attracting curious early adopters who are
willing to try unfinished ideas. As they scale, they must shift to
providing clear value and ROI to more skeptical mainstream customers. 

 SOFTWARE ISN'T DYING, BUT IT IS BECOMING MORE HONEST (5 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Software isn't disappearing, but the old subscription model is
fading. AI is enabling outcome-based pricing where companies pay for
real results instead of seats or access. 

🚀 

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 WHY PRODUCT SENSE IS THE ONLY PRODUCT SKILL THAT WILL MATTER IN THE
AI AGE (8 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 As AI tools become table stakes, product people won't stand out by
using more tools, but by making better calls than AI can make on its
own. The differentiator is product sense: empathy, foresight,
strategy, taste in tradeoffs, and creative execution. 

 FORCE MAPPING: A TOOL FOR FIXING SYSTEMIC ORGANIZATIONAL ISSUES (8
MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Organizations often mistake symptoms for real problems. Force Mapping
reveals the underlying forces, behaviors, and beliefs shaping those
symptoms so teams can target the root causes and run better
experiments to improve the system. 

🧑‍💻 

RESOURCES & TOOLS

 STAKEHOLDER MANAGEMENT FOR PRODUCT TEAMS: SHOW YOUR WORK, DON'T SELL
YOUR CONCLUSIONS (9 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Product teams lose stakeholder battles when they present conclusions
instead of their reasoning. Showing the discovery journey (outcome,
opportunity, solution, and evidence) turns stakeholders into
collaborators and builds stronger buy-in. 

 MINIMALLY VIABLE CONSISTENCY (7 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Minimally viable consistency is about standardizing only what truly
reduces coordination cost while preserving local flexibility. Be
cautious during strategy shifts, avoid false standard frameworks, and
treat consistency as temporary scaffolding that should expire once
teams can operate without it. 

🎁 

MISCELLANEOUS

 SIX RULES FOR DESIGNING COMPANY GOALS (14 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Goals should create clarity, not complexity. Focus on a few core
metrics, choose one priority, assign clear ownership, declare what you
will not do, and review progress regularly so the company learns what
actually drives results. 

 THINGS YOU THINK ARE HELPING YOUR CAREER BUT ARE ACTUALLY HURTING IT
(5 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Many high performers unintentionally stall their careers by being
overly helpful, quiet, and low-maintenance, which makes their most
valuable work invisible. To grow, you must prioritize visible impact,
communicate the problems you solve, and engage your manager with
opinions and ambition so your scope expands rather than stays hidden.
