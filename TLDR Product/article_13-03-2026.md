NEWS & TRENDS

 5 WAYS PRODUCT DISCOVERY BREAKS DOWN (6 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Product discovery breaks down when companies fast-track
“must-have” features and lack clear outcome-based goals.
Evidence-guided discovery with shared metrics and measurable outcomes
helps teams prioritize better ideas and avoid feature-factory
behavior. 

 WTF DOES A PRODUCT MANAGER DO? (AND WHY ENGINEERS SHOULD CARE) (7
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 As AI reduces the cost of building software, the real challenge
becomes deciding what to build, pushing engineers to adopt product
management skills. The three most important are gathering context,
tracking results through feedback loops, and communicating decisions
in a way that drives action.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 DANCING WITH PROBLEMS (8 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Product problems rarely have a single clear definition. Teams must
continually move between behavioral, contextual, and strategic
perspectives to understand them. Product leaders create shared
understanding by helping teams explore these layers together and
refine the real problem over time. 

 WILL FEATURES EVEN EXIST? HOW AI IS FORCING SAAS TO RETHINK THE
PRODUCT ITSELF (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI is eroding the traditional SaaS model built around predefined
features. The real moat will shift to platforms that let customers
generate and govern their own workflows securely and reliably.

 FORRESTER + HARVARD BUSINESS REVIEW REPORT: REIMAGINING PRODUCT
DEVELOPMENT WITH AI (SPONSOR) [9] 

 AI has launched product development into uncharted terrain. While
many are thriving in this turbulence, others still struggle to unlock
AI's full potential. Miro commissioned Forrester Consulting and
Harvard Business Review to conduct a widescale study of global product
leaders [9] -- covering potential wins, current roadblocks, and
research-backed solutions. Read the report.  [9] 

 HOW GOOD IS YOUR BACKLOG? HOW TO SHAPE THE WORK FOR PRODUCT SUCCESS
(5 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 When backlog items feel too big or vague, the problem is usually poor
shaping around customer needs, not just missing detail. Teams should
shape work together through problem clarification, workshops, and
spikes so they can learn faster, avoid handoff friction, and deliver
value in smaller, clearer steps. 

 KILL YOUR DARLINGS (22 MINUTE PODCAST) [11] 

 Flatlining products that generate some revenue but show no growth
often block better opportunities. Product leaders must deliberately
sunset them as portfolio decisions to free teams to discover the next
source of growth.

MISCELLANEOUS

 VISHAL KAPOOR'S 10 RULES FOR BUILDING HONEST PRODUCTS WITH AI (10
MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI can accelerate experimentation and prototyping, but building
trustworthy products still requires human judgment, customer
understanding, and first-principles thinking. Teams should use AI to
explore ideas faster while prioritizing simplicity, transparency, and
real customer value. 

 WHAT EXCELLENT GROWTH TEAMS SEE THAT OTHERS MISS (43 MINUTE VIDEO)
[13] 

 Great growth teams don't chase experiments or tactics. They identify
and remove the real friction blocking users from getting value.
Sustainable growth comes from deep customer understanding, faster
learning, and compounding the insights that truly matter.
