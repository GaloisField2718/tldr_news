 CLAUDE CODE TURNED EVERY ENGINEER INTO THREE. NOW COMPANIES NEED MORE
PRODUCT THINKERS (7 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 AI coding tools have shifted the software bottleneck from writing
code to deciding what to build. As agents compress engineering output,
teams need more product thinking and stronger fundamentals, not just
more developer headcount or more trust in AI output.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 “IT'S HARD TO EVAL” IS A PRODUCT SMELL (9 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 AI products should make their outputs easy to verify by showing their
sources and breaking complex work into smaller, reviewable pieces. 

 GENERATIVE UI DOESN'T MAKE SENSE FOR STARTUPS (4 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Generative UI is practical only for established companies, not
early-stage startups. Since startups lack evidence of customer needs
to encode correct interaction components, they should wait until
product-market fit is established.

RESOURCES & TOOLS

 THE BOTTLENECK STRIKE AGAIN! (5 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI speeds up coding, but product development doesn't have just one
bottleneck. Constraints constantly shift between judgment,
coordination, incentives, and learning. Due to this complexity, it is
too early to say exactly how AI will reshape work, and making bold
claims can often be misleading. 

 BUILDING WITHOUT THE HANDOFFS (5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Builder.io helps teams ship faster by letting more people build
directly with AI while keeping every change within trusted brand,
security, and approval guardrails.

MISCELLANEOUS

 THE OPERATIONAL REALITY OF MARKETING INSIDE AN AI COMPANY (3 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 Treat marketing and growth in AI companies as a single operational
system focused on compounding product adoption, rather than separate
awareness and downstream funnels. Fal uses AI selectively for
practical tasks to tighten feedback loops and accelerate
decision-making, rather than fully automating marketing. 

 SOFTWARE IS BECOMING MARKETING (8 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 As AI lowers barriers to entry, software becomes more accessible and
easier for outsiders to evaluate, which compresses pay and prestige.
Product markets will likely shift toward services, with mid-market
SaaS vendors declining, niche utilities rising, and a few aggregators
dominating through trust and distribution.
