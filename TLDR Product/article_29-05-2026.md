OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 WHY AI PRODUCTS LOSE USERS AFTER THE FIRST TRY - HOW TO DESIGN FOR
ADOPTION (7 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 AI products often lose users not because the model is weak, but
because adoption design decisions are missing or wrong. There are four
common failure modes: activation cliffs, a trust gap, broken
correction loops, and an agent autonomy ceiling. 

 I THINK ANTHROPIC AND OPENAI HAVE FOUND PRODUCT-MARKET FIT (9 MINUTE
READ) [10] 

 Anthropic and OpenAI have achieved PMF for enterprise
coding/general-purpose agent tools. The shift is driven by real
enterprise demand for products like Claude Code/Cowork and Codex,
despite thin “AI cost failure” anecdotes.

 OPENPROSE: GIVE YOUR AGENTS SLAS THAT HOLD OVER TIME (SPONSOR) [11] 

 Tired of babysitting agents? With OpenProse [11], you define your
outcome ("Inbox at zero by 5pm") - the runtime executes, watches for
drift, and pings you when it's done. Write your goal once in plain
English, let the system keeps it true. To start, simply open terminal
and type _npx skills add openprose/prose_ [12] 

 TO REACH YOUR BIG GOAL, YOU NEED TO SELL WHERE YOU'RE HEADING NEXT (4
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 To achieve a big long-term goal, teams must “sell Point C”- a
concrete, vivid next destination they will reach in the next 12–24
months - rather than settling for “continue existing.” This should
be a strategic, fundable milestone aligned with mission, vision,
strategy, and anchored in the needs of the community the team serves. 

 HOW USER SEGMENTATION, RATHER THAN PERSONAS, HELPS YOU GET DESIGN
BUY-IN (7 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 User segmentation gets more design buy-in than relying on personas
alone. Personas often get dismissed, and there are four segmentation
elements to reinforce personas: total audience size, time
period/seasonality, device/tech context, and new vs. returning
customer.

MISCELLANEOUS

 SLACK AI: THE PATH TO MULTI-CLOUD (17 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Slack evolved its AI infrastructure from self-managed AWS SageMaker
to managed Amazon Bedrock to solve scaling and latency issues. By
moving to Bedrock, Slack reduced engineering overhead and improved
efficiency through Provisioned Throughput and on-demand capacity. 

 AI APPS AREN'T DEAD, BUT THIN WRAPPERS ARE (11 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 AI labs will own many horizontal AI tools, but startups can still win
in complex vertical workflows. The strongest AI app companies will own
the system of work, including customer data, governance, integrations,
and measurable business outcomes.
