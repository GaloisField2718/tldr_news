 PRICING IS A PRODUCT (SPONSOR) [4] 

 Pricing used to be a number you wrote in a spreadsheet, negotiated
once a year with the customer, and forgot about until renewal.

This is no longer the caseâ€¦

With usage-based models, AI-powered agents, and real-time
infrastructure, pricing is something your customers feel every time
they log in. Pricing is your most visible product experience [4] and
builds user trust - or destroys it with one bad experience.

This means you have to start thinking about pricing decisions like
product features [4] - with frequent iterations, experiments, and user
observations.

 PRODUCT OPS WITHOUT PERMISSION (7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A new CPO asking tough questions is your chance to spotlight how
product ops can drive strategic execution. Document existing ops work,
run a pilot that aligns with leadership priorities, and collaborate
with program managers to show the value of formalizing the role
without overstepping. 

 WHY YOUR PRODUCT DECISIONS NEED DESIGN EXPERTISE (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Companies mistakenly equate business expertise with design skill,
creating cluttered products that frustrate users and require excessive
documentation. Investing in user-centered design reduces friction,
boosts conversions, and lowers support costs.

 A QUICK PRODUCT SIMPLICITY TEST: REMOVE ALL THE EXPLAINERS (3 MINUTE
READ) [12] 

 Test product simplicity by removing explainers and NUX. If users can
still navigate easily, your design is strong. Relying on NUX often
hides design flaws, so focus on making the core experience intuitive.

 WHERE PRODUCT MEETS FINANCE (5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Effective collaboration between Product and Finance requires early
engagement, precise language, and a clear understanding of financial
impacts like revenue recognition and forecasting. Product managers
should mirror Finance's structured approach, clarify assumptions
collaboratively, and communicate concisely to build trust. 

 THE EVOLUTION OF SOFTWARE PRICING (8 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Software pricing has evolved through six eras, from bundled hardware
to hybrid models blending subscriptions with usage. As AI introduces
new cost structures, vendors must prioritize simple, transparent
pricing tied to customer value to maintain trust and growth.
