 BATTERIES INCLUDED, OPINIONS REQUIRED: THE SPECIALIZATION OF APP GEN
PLATFORMS (5 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 AI app-generation platforms aren't competing in a zero-sum game -
they're thriving by specializing in niches like prototyping, personal
tools, and production apps. Like foundation models, they grow through
complementary strengths, not direct competition. 

 AI'S "JUST SHIP IT." PROBLEM (7 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI significantly boosts productivity for simpler tasks but offers
minimal benefits for complex work, leading companies to mistakenly
overestimate AI's impact on overall efficiency. True productivity
gains depend heavily on ideation and validation, processes AI can't
substantially improve.

 SUPERHUMAN: WHY GOOD BEATS PERFECT IN SURVEY DESIGN (3 MINUTE READ)
[7] 

 Companies often sabotage user surveys by demanding overly precise
answers, causing users to abandon them. Simplifying questions,
removing cumbersome dropdowns, and clearly indicating progress boosts
completion rates and gathers better insights. 

 PARADIGM SHIFTS AND THE WINNER'S CURSE (20 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Success in one era can block vision in the next. Apple and Amazon
risk missing the AI shift, while Google may thrive by staying
adaptable.

RESOURCES & TOOLS

 ARE YOU PRICING FOR FEATURES, ACCESS, OR OUTCOMES? (SPONSOR) [9] 

 AI is changing the basic definitions of what software is, what it can
do, and how it delivers value. Pricing models designed around gating
features or access might no longer be fit for purpose. Read _From
Features to Outcomes: The 3 Eras of Software Value_ [9]on the
Metronome blog [9]. 

 THE REWARD IS...MORE WORK (6 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 PMs often get stuck in ops after early success makes them
indispensable, limiting focus on growth. To escape, they should
productize repeatable tasks, set transition boundaries, and position
ops maturity as key to scale. 

 THE BEST VALIDATION HAPPENS BEFORE YOU WRITE A SINGLE LINE OF CODE (5
MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Testing assumptions early protects a PM's credibility and avoids
costly mistakes by replacing guesswork with evidence. Simple tools
like prototypes, fake-door tests, and interviews validate ideas fast.

 Product marketers must avoid weak, polluted, or conflicting context,
