ARTICLES & TUTORIALS

 BEYOND BOOLEANS (19 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 In TypeScript, logical expressions are of type boolean, with values
true or false. The Lean language, on the other hand, treats logical
statements like "2 + 2 = 4" as a distinct value of type Prop
(proposition). This allows Lean to have "typed truthfulness," where
facts are established and safely passed around a program. 

 FASTER INDEX I/O WITH NVME SSDS (20 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Marginalia Search improved its index performance by rewriting data
structures to better use modern hardware (NVMe SSDs). The redesign
involves a deterministic block-based skip list replacing B-trees and
utilizing direct-mode reads, which surprisingly revealed that larger
block sizes increase read speeds by a lot on NVMe SSDs. 

 EMBEDDING MILLIONS OF TEXT DOCUMENTS WITH QWEN3 (8 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 The Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B model can be used to embed millions of text
documents, with near-100% GPU utilization. The process involves
reading documents from S3, chunking them into sentences using spaCy,
generating embeddings with Qwen3, and writing results to turbopuffer.

OPINIONS & ADVICE

 EVERYTHING I KNOW ABOUT GOOD SYSTEM DESIGN (21 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Good system design is often underwhelming and prioritizes using
well-tested, boring components. It prioritizes minimizing stateful
components, favoring a single service to manage database interactions
to avoid complications. Background jobs should be used for slow
operations, strategic caching to reduce load, events for decoupled
communication, and differentiation should be made between pushing and
pulling data based on scale and urgency. 

 AI IS DIFFERENT (3 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 While predicting the future progress of AI is difficult, continued
development could lead to significant societal and economic changes.
AI could displace a substantial portion of the workforce and
concentrate power in the hands of a few large companies, which is a
major concern. 

 DO THINGS THAT DON'T SCALE, AND THEN DON'T SCALE (3 MINUTE READ) [13]


 For AI-assisted coding, the focus should shift from scaling projects
to creating small, useful, and personal applications. Examples from
the author include things like a private Slack group, a postcard
mailing service, and a landline pill reminder for his mom, all
designed for a limited audience. In a world obsessed with growth,
there's value in building something small and useful, and then
stopping.

 ELIMINATING JAVASCRIPT COLD STARTS ON AWS LAMBDA (4 MINUTE READ) [14]


 Porffor, a JavaScript engine/runtime, can now run on AWS Lambda to
eliminate JavaScript cold starts. It compiles JavaScript ahead-of-time
to WebAssembly and native binaries, creating tiny (<1MB) and fast
(millisecond-level) executables. 

 MOTIA (GITHUB REPO) [15] 

 Motia is a backend framework that unifies APIs, background jobs,
workflows, and AI agents into a single system. It uses "Steps" as a
core primitive, allowing developers to write code in multiple
languages like JavaScript, TypeScript, and Python within the same
project. Motia provides built-in state management and observability,
making deployments and debugging easier.

 WHO DOES YOUR ASSISTANT SERVE? (17 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 The release of GPT-5 was met with widespread disappointment due to
its uncontrolled, one-way upgrade that altered existing conversations,
especially for users relying on ChatGPT for companionship and
emotional support. There's a trend of people using AI as therapists
and companions, and it can be hard to have your access to a model be
controlled by providers themselves. This is a case for self-hosting
models when possible in order to maintain control over your access to
models. 

 BEST PRACTICES FOR BUILDING AGENTIC AI SYSTEMS: WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS
IN PRODUCTION (12 MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Good production agentic AI systems often have a two-tier agent model
consisting of a context-maintaining primary agent and subagents
focused on specific tasks. In terms of the principles that matter,
subagents are usually pure functions and stateless by default, and are
given clear task definitions with success criteria. 

 THE PECULIAR CASE OF JAPANESE WEB DESIGN (15 MINUTE READ) [19] 

 Japanese web design has a unique maximalist style, contrasting with
the global trend towards minimalism. Japanese websites tend to be
lighter colored and denser in content compared to those in other
countries. This difference is due to factors such as the Japanese
writing system, cultural risk aversion demanding more information, and
Japan's distinct technological evolution, as it had early adoption of
the mobile internet before smartphones.
