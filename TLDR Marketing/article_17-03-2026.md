 NIELSEN: SPORTS CONTINUES TO PROP UP AD-SUPPORTED TV (2 MINUTE READ)
[7] 

 Sports remain the main driver of ad-supported TV. It accounted for
nearly 30% of total viewership in Q4 2025, compared with 9.8% for
broadcast TV without sports and 18% for non-sport cable. Audiences
18-49 rely heavily on ad-supported sources, with 66.7% of that viewing
coming from streaming platforms, mostly on YouTube, Hulu, and Amazon
Prime. Live events like the Super Bowl LX, which drew 125.6M viewers,
and expanded MLB sponsorships show sports continue to deliver a large
and commercially valuable audience. 

 SHORT-FORM VIDEO IS DRIVING VIEWERS TO WATCH FULL TV SHOWS (3 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Short-form videos on social platforms are increasingly functioning as
a discovery mechanism for longer-form television and film, especially
among younger viewers. 85% of 16-24 year olds watch short-form weekly,
with 69% daily, and 87% of them reported watching full programmes
after seeing clips. Social discovery is strongest among this age
group, with 49% finding shows via social platforms and trending audio
influencing half of them. Meanwhile, older viewers are less affected
by social content.

 INVISIBLE PROMPTS: WHY YOU CAN'T SEE THE AI SEARCHES DRIVING YOUR
LEADS (8 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 AI search is driving leads through ‘invisible prompts'. These are
private, highly personalized conversations shaped by a user's context,
not just what is typed. This makes traditional keyword tracking and
optimization far less useful, since brands cannot see or replicate the
exact inputs behind these recommendations. Instead of chasing specific
prompts, the focus should be on creating detailed, use case–driven
content that teaches AI when and why to make recommendations. Over
time, this builds stronger visibility at the topic level and works
more like digital word of mouth, where the depth of content determines
whether a brand appears. 

 7 ORGANIC CONTENT INVESTMENTS THAT DRIVE ECOMMERCE ROI (9 MINUTE
READ) [10] 

 Ecommerce ROI from organic content relies on structured data and
multi-platform discovery rather than publishing large volumes of
content. AI-generated summaries reduce clicks, so content must be
authoritative enough for AI to cite and trustworthy for shoppers.
Social search is essential, with 86% of Gen Z using TikTok weekly for
searches. Visual discovery is also growing, with Google Lens handling
over 100 billion visual searches in 2025, 20% of which were product
related. Brands should focus on strong product pages, structured
product data, reviews and UGC, and decision support guides. Low-value,
scaled SEO blog content and other shortcuts should be avoided.

 SHOPIFY IS PREPARING FOR AI SHOPPING AGENTS TO CHANGE EVERYTHING (2
MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Shopify believes that AI agents will act as merit-based personal
shoppers, surfacing products based on individual preferences rather
than retailer dominance in search results. It is building an AI
merchant assistant called Sidekick alongside a support agent and a
protocol for agents to read merchant product data. With only 18% of US
retail purchases happening online, the company sees agentic commerce
as a major discovery channel especially for smaller and long-tail
merchants.

 HOW MR. CLEAN CAN DOMINATE SOCIAL MEDIA THROUGH MASCOT-LED MARKETING
(5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Mr. Clean is a culturally iconic brand with underused social
potential. Its mascot has driven attention through a Super Bowl ad and
a 2026 retirement stunt, yet content focuses on products rather than
personality. A character-led strategy would give Mr. Clean a humorous
voice, make social channels his main platform, and engage younger
audiences. Campaigns could feature TikTok reactions to messy videos,
structured Magic Eraser UGC, and meme-driven content to turn the
mascot into a living, ongoing brand presence. 

 HOW RETAILERS CAN LEVERAGE THEIR PRIVATE LABEL MARKETING ADVANTAGE
OVER NATIONAL BRANDS (3 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Private label sales reached a record $283B last year, with retailers
expanding well beyond budget options into premium tiers to capture a
wider range of shoppers. Retailers hold a built-in marketing edge at
the point of sale, where they can merchandise private labels alongside
national brands, dedicate separate store sections, and use loyalty
programs and targeted promotions to drive trials. They are also
shortening innovation cycles by leveraging shopper data to quickly
follow trends and launch new products.
