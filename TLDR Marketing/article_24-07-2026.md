NEWS & TRENDS

 THE GREAT DIGITAL FATIGUE: HOW DIGITAL BURNOUT IS CHANGING SOCIAL
MEDIA USE (5 MINUTE READ) [7]

 Users are pulling back from social media, with 55% posting less and
53% limiting audiences, while 47% have deleted apps due to stress.
Over half say maintaining a presence feels like work, especially Gen
Z, and 44% cite political content as a reason to disengage. Stepping
away brings mixed emotions, with 27% feeling peaceful but 22% anxious,
highlighting how platforms now feel more like obligations than
connection.

 THE FORTUNE 500 IS DELETING THE CMO TITLE (4 MINUTE READ) [8]

 The CMO title is disappearing, with only 36% of Fortune 500 companies
still using it, down from 49% last year. Companies are folding
marketing into revenue and growth roles, and fewer marketing leaders
report to the CEO, now at 52%. Roles like Chief Growth Officer and
Chief Commercial Officer are replacing CMOs, as companies push for
direct revenue ownership. The role isn't disappearing, but being
absorbed into broader functions.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 5 STORYTELLING FRAMEWORKS TO ATTRACT B2B BUYERS (5 MINUTE READ) [9]

 A B2B narrative is not an emotional pitch but a logical thread
connecting why a brand exists, what it's fighting against, and how it
helps customers win. Each event in that story needs to happen because
of the one before it, replacing the flat "and then" of a feature list
with a "because of that" chain that creates stakes and payoff. That
thread also needs an opponent, the specific practice or belief the
brand positions itself against, since without one there's nothing for
the story to resolve.

 HOW TO BUILD A BRAND PRESENCE ON REDDIT IN 2026 (2 MINUTE READ) [10]

 Reddit works best when brands learn community behavior before they
try to promote anything. Start by using the free Reddit Pro tools to
track keywords, analyze discussions, and monitor performance. Spend
time in relevant subreddits to understand the tone and common
conversations so your organic posts and ads match community
expectations. Reddit has become more valuable because Google Search
and AI chatbots cite its discussions more often, so a strong presence
can increase product discovery and brand visibility.

 9 TIPS TO APPLYING IN TODAY'S MARKET (2 MINUTE READ) [11]

 Most roles are decided before they're posted, so early outreach and
networking matter more than cold applications. Strong candidates show
proof of work through portfolios, keep resumes clean and focused, and
send short tailored pitches instead of generic intros. Researching the
company, applying early, and asking thoughtful questions helps
differentiate. Posting on LinkedIn also signals how candidates think
and that they can build distribution.

 IF YOUR BRAND ISN'T SHOWING UP IN YOUTUBE AI SEARCH, YOUR COMPETITORS
ARE (4 MINUTE READ) [14]

 Google's AI search is pulling YouTube videos into answers, often
favoring creators over brands. Content that's clearly structured and
built around real questions performs best. Depth across related topics
also matters more than single keywords. Creators are winning by doing
this well, leaving brands that rely on product-led content at risk of
being left out of the answer.

 FUNNY MARKETING 101 (18 MINUTE READ) [15]

 Effective marketing humor comes down to two rules that don't apply to
comedy in general, being specific enough that only a narrow audience
gets the joke, and being surprising enough to subvert what that
audience already expects. Niche recognition works because most brands
never bother creating humor built for one audience's daily
frustrations, which is why an internal joke about IT teams or LinkedIn
cringe can outperform anything broader. The same subversion shows up
in a simple format, one where a familiar structure gets a new idea
poured into it, then twisted in a way the audience doesn't see coming.
