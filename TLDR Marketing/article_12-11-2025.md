 TikTok Shop reached $19 billion in global quarterly sales, matching

 SMALL BUSINESS OPTIMISM TAKES A SMALL STEP BACK AS UNCERTAINTY EASES
IN OCTOBER (5 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Small business optimism fell slightly last month, with the NFIB Index
dropping 0.6 points to 98.2 as owners reported lower sales and
declining profits. Hiring remains difficult, with 32% of firms unable
to fill job openings and 27% citing labor quality as their top
problem. Inflation, supply chain issues, and rising costs continue to
challenge businesses, though fewer cited financing or insurance as
major problems.

 THE GROWTH STRATEGY MOST STARTUPS OVERLOOK: ENGINEERING-AS-MARKETING
(7 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Engineering-as-Marketing turns product or engineering time into a
growth engine by creating free tools that attract and convert users
through usefulness instead of promotion. It builds trust and organic
reach by giving value upfront, leading to long-term traffic and
compounding returns that paid ads cannot match. Examples like
HubSpot's Website Grader, Shopify's Hatchful, and Ahrefs' free SEO
tools show how offering helpful resources drives leads, backlinks, and
brand awareness. Startups can apply this by building small, practical
tools that solve real problems, launching them strategically, and
optimizing for SEO to generate lasting growth.

 WHEN AI GUIDES THE SHOPPING JOURNEY (29 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 About 40% of US consumers now use AI for shopping. Over 70% say AI
anticipates their needs, 85% find its recommendations personalized,
and 83% say it makes shopping more enjoyable. While AI helps people
compare products and make decisions faster, 95% of shoppers still take
extra steps to confirm what it suggests, showing a clear trust gap.
Brands must prioritize accuracy, clear sourcing, and human validation
to maintain confidence throughout the AI-shaped shopping journey.

 THE SCAMMER NEXT DOOR (24 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 An investigation into India's scam economy shows how mass
unemployment and inequality have turned fraud into a booming industry.
What began with call center cons has expanded across social media,
powered by underemployed youth who see deception as legitimate work.
Using scripts and stolen data to target victims worldwide, they
reflect a society where economic desperation and weak enforcement have
blurred the line between ambition and deceit.
