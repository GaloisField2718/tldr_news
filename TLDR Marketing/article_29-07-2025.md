 AMAZON REMOVES ALL GOOGLE SHOPPING ADS GLOBALLY IN 48 HOURS (5 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Amazon pulled its ads from Google Shopping across all major markets
between July 21 and 23. This included the US, UK, Germany, and Japan,
dropping its impression share from up to 60% to zero. This move
follows months of reduced ad spend and appears to be a strategic shift
aimed at cutting costs, testing paid search value, and asserting
greater control over traffic as AI-driven shopping tools gain
traction. The withdrawal marks Amazon's most significant advertising
exit since its 2020 pullback.

 INTRODUCING THE GOOGLE TRENDS API (ALPHA): A NEW WAY TO ACCESS SEARCH
TRENDS DATA (3 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Google launched an alpha version of the Google Trends API. It allows
a limited number of developers to access search trends data
programmatically. The API provides consistently scaled search interest
data over the past 1,800 days, with daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly
aggregations. It supports geographic filtering by region and
subregion. The API improves on the website version by allowing
comparisons across multiple terms and timeframes without re-scaling
each request.

 HOW MCDONALD'S DUG INTO NOSTALGIA AND REIGNITED THE CHICKEN WARS (4
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 McDonald's revived its cult-favorite Snack Wrap in July, boosting
foot traffic and triggering fresh competition in the chicken category.
Popeyes responded with new Chicken Wraps and an AI-generated diss
track, while Taco Bell, KFC, Wingstop, and White Castle launched or
marketed new chicken items. Chicken remains a key innovation driver in
the quick service restaurant space, with brands leaning into
nostalgia, cultural campaigns, celebrity tie-ins, and loyalty plays to
win market share. 

 HOW HIGH-PERFORMING GTM TEAMS TURN BUYER SIGNALS INTO REVENUE-DRIVING
ACTION (3 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 GTM teams now use intent data to drive pipeline and speed up sales by
focusing on signal strength, not lead volume. Effective programs treat
intent as a layer that improves existing channels and combine first-,
second-, and third-party data for broader insight. AI now helps teams
score signals, predict buyer readiness, and automate follow-up, making
intent data more actionable across the entire customer lifecycle.
