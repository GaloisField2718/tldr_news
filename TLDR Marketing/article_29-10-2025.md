 AI IS BREAKING THE ECONOMICS OF CONTENT (3 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 AI is disrupting the economics of content by devaluing evergreen
material like Wikipedia's while increasing the visibility and reward
for additive, insight-driven content. Wikipedia's human traffic fell
5% YoY as scrapers rose 10.5% and bots surged 162.4%, while Google
referrals dropped 35% due to AI Overviews that answer queries directly
using its content. Nearly 50% of Wikipedia's keywords now display AI
Overviews, yet it receives one-third fewer citations, while sites like
YouTube and Reddit have gained visibility and mentions. The shift
favors platforms producing net-new insights, pushing brands to reduce
evergreen content investment and focus on additive formats such as
research, thought leadership, and data storytelling. 

 ARE WE SEEING MORE COMMENT BAIT ON LINKEDIN? (2 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 LinkedIn's reach has dropped. About a quarter of the feed is now
sponsored, and the algorithm favors posts from people users don't
follow but whose content engages their connections. Early in the feed,
users see posts from people they follow mixed with ads and platform
features, but as they scroll, LinkedIn shifts to discovery content to
promote new creators. Regular posts get lower visibility since the
algorithm favors engagement-heavy content like job changes or layoffs,
similar to Instagram and TikTok.
