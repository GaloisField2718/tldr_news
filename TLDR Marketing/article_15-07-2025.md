 WEEKLY SUBSCRIPTIONS DOMINATE IOS APP REVENUE, REPORT FINDS (2 MINUTE
READ) [5] 

 Weekly subscriptions account for 46% of iOS app revenue and grew 9.5%
this year. It outpaced one-time purchases at 6.3%. Average prices rose
about 12% in the US and EU, reaching around $8. The US leads with
48.9% of revenue and generates 3-4 times more per install than other
regions. Weekly plans dominate LATAM and MEA revenue but face sharp
user drop-off after 30 days. Trials boost lifetime value by up to 64%.


 AI OVERVIEWS CITE AI-GENERATED CONTENT MORE THAN HUMAN WRITING (2
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI Overviews on Google tend to cite AI-generated or AI-assisted
content far more than purely human-written pages. A study of 1.9M URLs
found that 87.8% of pages cited were a mix of human and AI content,
with only 8.6% purely human and 3.6% purely AI. This contrasts with
general web content, where 25.8% is purely human and 2.5% purely AI.
Additionally, 86.5% of top-ranking pages contain some AI content.

profile unless published, but still generate engagement metrics within

 MOBILE APP MARKETERS SHOULDN'T IGNORE THE BIGGEST SCREEN IN THE HOME

 Connected TV (CTV) offers high-impact reach with nearly 50% greater

 ADVERTISERS WIN, USERS LOSE IN AN INSTAGRAM SPIN-OFF (2 MINUTE READ)
[12] 

 If Meta is forced to sell Instagram, the split would benefit
advertisers but likely harm users. Ad prices would drop as Facebook
and Instagram compete, leading to more frequent and repetitive ads on
both platforms. Facebook would retain a competitive edge due to its
larger audience, pushing Instagram to serve even more ads at lower
prices. While the divestiture may boost ad competition, it would not
increase user benefits under the FTC's current market definition. 

 THE BOWTIE: A CUSTOMER-CENTRIC FRAMEWORK FOR A RECURRING REVENUE
BUSINESS (26 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 The classic marketing and sales funnel focuses on customer
acquisition through awareness, education, and selection, but ends
before recurring revenue begins. Its key limitations include being
seller-centric, linear, and lacking feedback loops, which makes it
ineffective for subscription-based models. The Bowtie model addresses
these gaps by extending the funnel with stages like onboarding,
adoption, and expansion, placing emphasis on delivering customer
impact rather than just promised value.
