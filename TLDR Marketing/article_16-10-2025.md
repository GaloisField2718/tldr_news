NEWS & TRENDS

 2025 DELOITTE HOLIDAY RETAIL SURVEY (3 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Shoppers are approaching the 2025 holiday season with tighter budgets
and growing economic concerns. Average spending is expected to fall
10% to $1,595. About 77% of consumers expect higher prices, and 57%
anticipate a weaker economy, pushing many to focus on deals and
value-driven purchases. Younger shoppers, especially Gen Z, are more
likely to seek deals (95%), and are turning to influencers and social
media (74%) and AI (43%) for product discovery. Retailers that combine
discounts, quality, and personalized digital experiences powered by AI
are best positioned to capture attention and loyalty this season. 

 GROCERY PRICES KEEP RISING. FRUSTRATED CONSUMERS ARE TRYING TO ADAPT
(3 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Grocery prices are rising sharply, with coffee up 21% and ground beef
up 13% over the past year. Consumers are buying smaller packages,
shopping at cheaper or ethnic markets, and cooking at home more.
Despite reduced store visits, most continue shopping weekly and
adapting creatively to manage costs.

 EXPERIENTIAL TREND OF THE WEEK: HOTLINES (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 This year, hotlines are reemerging as a new tool in experiential
marketing. Canva invited fans to leave feedback through rotary phones,
while Disney and Doritos launched story-driven hotlines tied to major
entertainment properties. Biosil added a direct-to-consumer element
through celebrity-recorded responses tied to product benefits. These
campaigns leveraged customer curiosity and nostalgia while offering
them a new and different way to connect with brand narratives. 

 HOW TOP CREATORS USE SIGNATURE FORMATS (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Top-performing creators in 2025 use signature formats that make their
content instantly recognizable. This strategy drives faster audience
growth, builds authority, and sustains consistency. From SEO carousels
to visual templates, high-growth creators are anchoring their strategy
around repeatable and actionable content structures. The RARE
(Recognizable, Actionable, Repeatable, and Engaging) framework
provides a simple roadmap to build a format that can become a content
trademark.

 DEATH OF THE CREATOR (2 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Social media has moved from follower-based networks to
algorithm-driven â€œrecommendation mediaâ€, and is now entering a
third phase powered by gen AI. In this new stage, platforms will
create personalized content on demand, reducing their reliance on
human creators. While people will still make things, incentives will

communities. Platforms may reward creators for feeding models rather
than reaching audiences. 

 THE CROCODILE MOUTH, THE DEATH OF NUM=100 & HOW TO CUT THROUGH THE
CHAOS (7 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The "Crocodile Mouth" refers to the growing gap between impressions
and clicks in Google Search Console that surfaced in early 2025.
Brands saw impressions rise sharply while clicks stayed flat or
declined. The cause was twofold. First, AI Overviews and zero-click
search behaviors kept users on the search page. Second, the num=100
parameter, which previously allowed tools to track 100 search results
per query, inflated impression data. Cutting through all the noise
requires aligning content with how search is functioning now. This
includes visibility within AI surfaces, clarity in structure, and a
pivot toward transactional queries that still convert. 

 CAN AI REPLACE A CMO FOR CASH-STRAPPED STARTUPS? (3 MINUTE READ) [15]


 AI cannot replace a CMO in startups today. The role requires
strategy, leadership, and turning vision into action. Fractional CMOs
let startups combine part-time human insight with AI-driven execution.
In the future, CMOs will guide AI, ensure ethical use, and merge
machine insights with human judgment, showing that AI supports
leadership rather than replaces it.
