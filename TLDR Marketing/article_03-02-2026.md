 THE 3-EMAIL CART & CHECKOUT ABANDONMENT FLOWS (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Cart abandoners add items to their cart but never start checkout.
They're usually browsing or comparing products. Checkout abandoners
enter their email or shipping info and were ready to buy but got
interrupted. Both flows convert by targeting users with intent,
removing friction, and nudging completion. The 3-email framework sends
reminders at 1 hour, 24 hours, and 48-72 hours. It progresses from a
gentle nudge, to objection handling, and a final push with urgency.

 GSC DATA IS 75% INCOMPLETE (4 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 GSC is unreliable as a single source because Google filters about 75%
of impressions and 38% of clicks for privacy. An analysis of 10 B2B
SaaS sites with ~450M impressions found filtering from 59.3% to 93.6%
for impressions and up to 88.5% for clicks, leaving large gaps in
query data. AI Overviews surged in 2025 and correlate with a 56.6%
click drop since March, while impressions fluctuated due to scraper
activity. Bots also inflate impressions by 0.2% to 6.5%. Relying only
on GSC can mislead teams, so use multiple data sources and sampling
checks. 

 FROM CLAWDBOT TO MOLTBOT TO OPENCLAW: THE AI AGENT GENERATING BUZZ
AND FEAR (4 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 OpenClaw, an open-source AI agent launched weeks ago by Peter
Steinberger, has surged to over 145,000 GitHub stars as interest grows
in agents that can autonomously act across operating systems. The tool
runs directly on user devices and connects to models like Claude or
ChatGPT. It can manage emails, calendars, files, shopping, and web
tasks using persistent memory. Security firms, including Palo Alto
Networks and Cisco, have warned that its deep system access creates
serious risks around data leakage, malicious commands, and enterprise
safety.
