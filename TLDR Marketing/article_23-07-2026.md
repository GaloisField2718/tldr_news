NEWS & TRENDS

 THE B2B BLOG IS COLLAPSING. THE COMPANIES REPLACING IT ARE BECOMING
PUBLISHERS (5 MINUTE READ) [8]

 The SEO blog playbook is losing ground as zero-click search rises and
platform reach declines. Owned media replaces it, with brands building
publications for a defined audience. This model drives distribution
through shares, interviews, and syndication. It also turns content
into pipeline. Early results show traction from niche audiences and
growing AI referrals.

 WHY SAVVY MEDIA BUYERS ARE BETTING ON MALL ADVERTISING (4 MINUTE
READ) [9]

 Media buyers are increasingly turning to mall advertising because it
delivers something scarce in a fragmented media landscape: physically
present and highly attentive audiences rather than sheer digital
reach. Malls have shifted from pure retail destinations into lifestyle
hubs, drawing people in for fitness classes and healthcare
appointments as often as for shopping. That inventory is now largely
digitized, so buyers can target every screen within a set distance of
a specific location and measure results through foot traffic, offsite
visits, and spend data instead of modeled estimates.

 FRENCH LAWMAKERS APPROVE A SWEEPING SOCIAL MEDIA BAN FOR CHILDREN
UNDER 15 (3 MINUTE READ) [10]

 France's ban arrives alongside a broader European push on kids and
social media, with the EU's top official calling for limits and an EU
advisory panel recommending zero access for children under 13 until
platforms can prove their products are safe. Spain and the UK have
each floated similar restrictions, and the European Commission is
expected to propose EU-wide rules for its 27 member countries.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HOW TO BUILD CURIOSITY INTO HIGH-PERFORMING SOCIAL ADS (4 MINUTE
READ) [11]

 Attention gets a viewer to notice a social ad, but curiosity is what
keeps them from scrolling away in the next few seconds. Platforms are
increasingly built to reward the second half of that equation. Meta
and TikTok's AI-driven delivery systems now optimize for watch time,
saves and shares, and clicks that happen after the video ends. These
are all signals a genuinely curious viewer produces without prompting.
Building that gap into the creative means designing open loops, such
as a surprising statement or an unresolved problem, then demonstrating
a result instead of describing it.

 WHEN AND HOW TO CALL CUSTOMERS BY NAME (3 MINUTE READ) [12]

 Using a customer's name can increase spending and satisfaction when
it feels natural and personal. Customers spent 17.3% more on average
and accepted upsell offers more than twice as often when employees
used their name during the interaction instead of only during the
sales pitch. Introduce employees by name first and train staff to use
customer names sparingly because scripted or repetitive use reduces
the effect. Avoid using customer names in private or sensitive
situations because discomfort can reverse the benefit.

 AI VISIBILITY IS A TOPIC-LEVEL GAME: A STUDY OF 50,000 BRANDS IN
CHATGPT (6 MINUTE READ) [14]

 A study of 50K brands found that winning one ChatGPT prompt does not
mean owning a topic. Only 15% of categories had a clear leader,
leaving most topics open for competitors. Traditional SEO metrics like
traffic and authority scores only predicted winners about half the
time. Brands that consistently appear across related questions build
stronger AI visibility. Once a brand gains a wide lead, it becomes
harder to overtake.

 HOW TO KNOW YOU'RE READY FOR A FRACTIONAL CMO AND HOW TO BRIEF ONE
WELL (4 MINUTE READ) [15]

 Most companies wait until something breaks before hiring a fractional
CMO. Readiness isn't about size, it's about having a real strategy gap
and a team that can execute. Common signals include weak positioning
and looming inflection points like a fundraise or expansion. It's not
a fit without product market fit or an execution layer. Bring clear
problems, real data, and defined decision rights to get value quickly.


 MEASUREMENT IS A BUSINESS ADVANTAGE (4 MINUTE READ) [16]

 Better measurement creates a competitive advantage because it
uncovers profitable channels, improves ad platform optimization, and
helps teams make budget decisions with confidence. Click-based
reporting often undervalues channels like podcasts, influencers, and
sponsorships, so companies that combine attribution surveys and brand
signals can find lower cost opportunities before competitors do.
Better conversion data also helps Google and Meta optimize for higher
quality customers instead of low value leads.

 INSIDE GENERAL MILLS'S BRAND ARCHIVE (4 MINUTE READ) [17]

 General Mills's brand archive functions as both a lookup service and
a forward-looking curation system, holding more than 10,000 records
across 5,000 square feet that trace the company's 160-year history.
Brand teams query it for context on products like Bisquick and Betty
Crocker, while the three-person team also flags today's campaigns as
candidates for tomorrow's collection. The volume and variety of modern
marketing content, spanning far more channels than earlier decades,
has made deciding what to keep the hardest ongoing call.
