NEWS & TRENDS

 BRANDED SEARCH IS BECOMING A LESS RELIABLE PROXY FOR BRAND DEMAND (3
MINUTE READ) [4]

 Branded search has long been a proxy for brand demand, but it's
becoming less reliable. Data shows branded queries dropping even as
demand holds steady, pointing to a measurement gap, not a demand
decline. AI search is shifting decisions upstream, answering questions
before users search for brands. The risk is misreading weaker search
as weaker brands and underinvesting in brand building. Branded search
still matters, but it should be one signal, not the source of truth.

 DOES TOPICAL AUTHORITY MATTER IN AI SEARCH? (7 MINUTE READ) [5]

 Topical authority still matters in AI search, with data from
Semrush's analysis of 50,000+ brands showing AI answers develop
category owners over time. In ChatGPT's June 2026 data, only 15.2% of
categories had a clear owner, leaving 89.3% of AI search demand in
categories without one. Category owners also showed stronger brand
search volume, organic traffic, and authority. Build depth in core
topics rather than chase broad content coverage.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 WHAT EVERYONE GETS WRONG ABOUT CATEGORY CREATION (6 MINUTE READ) [6]

 Category creation only works when it reflects a real job to be done
instead of a new marketing label. Companies create confusion when they
invent platform names that don't match a clear customer responsibility
or workflow. It's better to create a new job to be done before naming
a category, sell products as a suite when buyers purchase them
separately, or lead with the product the market already understands
and expand from there.

 THOUGHTLESS FEATURES NEVER ADD UP TO A THOUGHTFUL PRODUCT (4 MINUTE
READ) [7]

 Teams ship “ham biscuit” features fast, solving surface problems
but missing real user needs. Idea generators stay detached, copying
competitors or AI outputs, while execution follows without question.
The result is bloated products that hurt clarity and sales. Better
products come from defining real workflows, not stacking features.
Focus on the job to be done, validate outcomes, and design end-to-end
experiences instead of isolated fixes.

 HOW TO CHECK CHROME'S ACCESSIBILITY TREE (1 MINUTE READ) [8]

 AI agents don't view websites like humans do. They rely on the
Accessibility Tree, a machine-readable version of your page structure.
Check yours in Chrome DevTools to see how agents interpret your
content. Clean up headings, labels, links, and alt text to make your
site easier for AI systems to understand and surface.

 RCS OR SMS? IT DEPENDS ON THE TASK (3 MINUTE READ) [10]

 RCS now reaches 96% of US devices and offers rich media,
interactivity, and branding, but costs at least twice as much as SMS.
Like HTML email, it adds capability, not necessity. Use RCS when
visuals or actions help complete a task like browsing products or
resolving issues. Stick with SMS for simple, urgent updates like order
confirmations or codes.

 AI IS WORKING OVERTIME. SO AM I (10 MINUTE READ) [11]

 AI users save an average of 5.1 hours a week but lose 3.3 hours
learning or troubleshooting the same tools. 77% report a heavier
workload, while 23% report AI-related anxiety. The shortfall comes
from generation time being mistaken for completion time, since a
report that takes one minute to draft still needs its research checked
and its logic fixed before it is usable. The technology accelerates
the first draft while leaving the harder work, review and
accountability, exactly where it was.
