 WHAT AI MARKETING JOBS LOOK LIKE TODAY (6 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI has become a central part of marketing jobs, with many listings
now requiring AI skills or experience. About 75% of workers report
using AI because it is officially or unofficially expected, yet 95% of
genAI programs at work are failing. New roles such as AI marketing
program managers at large companies like Stripe pay up to $210,000,
while startups like Firecrawl are experimenting with fully AI-driven
agent roles offering $5,000 monthly retainers. The future of marketing
jobs is splitting into three paths: fully AI-focused roles,
traditional roles adapted with AI expectations, and fully AI agents
replacing human work, leaving nearly 60% of marketers worried about
job replacement.

 HOW TO RUN GMAIL ADS IN 2025 (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Google has reintroduced Gmail Ads through Demand Gen campaigns,
allowing advertisers to run campaigns exclusively in Gmail. Gmail Ads
can appear as single image, carousel, video, or product feed ads, and
they show in the Promotions or Social tabs with a teaser ad that
expands into a full ad when clicked. Targeting options include
demographics, in-market segments, custom segments, lookalikes, and
retargeting, though content targeting is not available. Success is
measured through engagement rate, click-through rate, and conversion
rate, with strategies varying depending on whether the audience is
warm or cold.

 WILL GOOGLE'S ANTITRUST SEARCH RULING IMPACT MARKETING SEO
STRATEGIES? (7 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Google will be required to share parts of its search index and
user-interaction data with qualified competitors, including AI
platforms such as ChatGPT and Perplexity, marking a major regulatory
milestone in the search market. While immediate effects on rankings
may be limited because core algorithms and advertising data remain
under Google's control, the decision signals a longer-term shift
toward a more fragmented search ecosystem as Google's market share
drops below 90% and AI-driven assistants gain momentum. For marketers,
this creates a future where visibility will depend on building
authority, securing placement across multiple discovery platforms, and
optimizing for both search engines and AI assistants.
