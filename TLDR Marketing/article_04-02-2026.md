 SHORT SUPER BOWL AD TEASERS MISS THE EMOTIONAL MARK, SYSTEM1 ANALYSIS
FINDS (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Short Super Bowl ad teasers create weaker emotional impact than
longer ads and reduce campaign effectiveness. Analysis across six
years shows teaser spots score about 0.5 Stars lower than full length
ads because viewers have less time to connect and build memory. The
research finds that 20 to 40 second ads perform best, and brands
should start Super Bowl campaigns in January and continue running ads
after the game to extend impact. A 30 second Super Bowl ad now costs
about $8 million, so it is important for a brand to run longer pre
game ads and keep top performers in rotation, as seen in a 5.9 Star
rated Lay's ad that still airs a year later.

 THE PERFECT PITCH: WHAT MILLIONS OF EMAILS TAUGHT US ABOUT PITCHING
JOURNALISTS (20 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Short email pitches drive the best journalist engagement. Emails with
100 to 300 word bodies get the highest open rates and reply rates,
while emails under 300 words earn the most replies. About 50% of
pitches paste a full press release into the email body, which is
mainly a UK practice, while US pitches more often use short plain
text. Personalization varies in depth, as 83% use the journalist's
name but only 57% reference past articles, and shallow flattery often
fails. Relevant connections to prior coverage work better. Strong
pitches usually start with a timely stat or trend hook, give a clear
headline takeaway, include about four bolded bullet points, and add
expert quotes that explain why the findings matter instead of
repeating the data.

 THE â€œMOORE-ISHâ€ LAW OF MARKETING (5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Marketing is shifting from campaign launches to continuous systems as
the cost and speed of experimentation collapse. AI reduces the effort
to generate and deploy variations, pushing advantage toward teams that
can measure results and make fast decisions. Content moves from single
assets to always-on streams built from modular components, while
personalization shifts from static segments to real-time situations
like objections or constraints. Winning teams focus less on producing
volume and more on building pipelines that test and learn based on
compounding insights. 

 LIGHT BEER WAS A FLOP, UNTIL THE RIGHT ADVERTISING CAME ALONG (2
MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Early light beers failed not because of taste or formulation, but
because marketing framed them around dieting rather than enjoyment.
Miller Lite broke through in the 1970s by repositioning light beer as
less filling with great taste, using sports figures and barroom humor
instead of health language. That shift created a mass-market category
that later powered brands like Bud Light and Michelob Ultra to
category leadership.
