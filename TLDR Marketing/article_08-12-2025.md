STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 3-STEP FRAMEWORK FOR ACCOUNT-FOCUSED WEBINARS (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Focus webinars on a small set of target accounts and build the
session specifically for them. Drive attendance with a structured
sequence by calling top accounts 3 weeks out, involving a customer
co-presenter 2 weeks out, sending the deck 1 week out, and texting key
registrants the day before. Use a concise 30-minute format that shares
learning, customer results, implementation, and Q&A. Then follow up
within 2 hours with recordings, templates, and personalized sessions
for target accounts. 

 WINNING BACK CUSTOMERS REQUIRES FIXING WHAT DROVE THEM AWAY (5 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Winning back customers or employees is more effective than acquiring
new ones. However, it requires admitting mistakes and proving real
change. People leave when gaps form between expectations and
experience. Most win-back attempts fail because they rely on generic
messages, discounts, or incentives without addressing the root cause.
Effective win-backs start by diagnosing why someone left, fixing the
issue, owning the mistake, and showing concrete proof of improvement.
Outreach should be personalized, returning must feel like an upgrade,
rejoining should be easy, and reinforcement after the return is
essential.

MISCELLANEOUS

 MARKETING MISTAKES MADE BY GOOGLE+ (6 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Google+ failed because millions of sign-ups did not turn into real
use, and 90% of sessions lasted less than 5 seconds. The platform
never built emotional pull or a clear identity, and users spent only
about 3 minutes/month on it despite more than 200M registered
accounts. Over-integration into products like YouTube and Gmail caused
friction instead of adoption, and two data leaks that exposed millions
of profiles damaged trust. Without creator activity or a core action
that kept users returning, the platform could not build habits and
ultimately shut down. 

 NEW RESEARCH: WHAT WE LEARNED FROM ANALYZING 60K+ GOOGLE FAN-OUT
QUERIES (6 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Google uses an average of 9.06 fan-out queries per prompt, and 59% of
prompts trigger between 5 and 11 queries. Software prompts generate
the most fan-outs at 11.7, while Local prompts generate the fewest at
3.79. Fan-out queries average 6.7 words, and 77% fall in the 5 to 8
word range. The most common Ngrams include 2024 or 2025 at 6.26% and
reviews at 2.14%, which shows that fresh content, comparison content,
and review content are major drivers of what Google chooses to
surface.
