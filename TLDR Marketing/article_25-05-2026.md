NEWS & TRENDS

 AI IS NOT THE FUTURE OF SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT, BUT THE LAST DYING GASP
OF THE PAST (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 AI companies are creating more user friction so they can sell tools
that manage the friction. Google's push toward “interactive
experiences” shows how far the industry has moved from solving real
customer problems. Modern software incentives reward engagement and
feature sprawl over efficiency, which turns unnecessary tasks into
profitable business models. The “Annoyance Economy” costs families
in the US at least $165B in wasted time and lost money each year.
Products that remove effort and deliver direct outcomes will stand out
as users grow tired of AI-generated clutter and engagement loops
designed to keep them inside platforms longer. 

 KEEPING CONVERSATIONS REAL ON LINKEDIN (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 LinkedIn wants to preserve authenticity in professional conversations
as AI-generated “low-effort” content becomes more common. The
platform warns that overuse of AI can dilute real human insight and is
rolling out systems to detect and reduce distribution of generic or
automated posts and comments. Early testing shows it can identify such
content with 94% accuracy, limiting its reach beyond immediate
networks. LinkedIn is also expanding verification tools across 100M+
members to help users engage with real professionals and reduce bots
and fake profiles.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 ARE YOU SELLING WHAT YOUR CUSTOMERS ARE BUYING? (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 DiamondBack Covers increased conversions by 26% after replacing
lifestyle-focused creative with product messaging aligned to customer
priorities. Research showed buyers valued security most, but the
homepage emphasized camping and off-road imagery that created
confusion. The winning version used benefit-driven copy and close-up
product visuals highlighting durability and weather protection. The
result reinforced that above-the-fold messaging should lead with the
core purchase driver rather than atmosphere or brand storytelling. 

 B2B INFLUENCER MARKETING IS BECOMING A REVENUE CHANNEL. MOST TEAMS
STILL RUN IT LIKE SOCIAL (4 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 55% of B2B marketers already run influencer programs, and 85% of
enterprises plan to increase investment over the next five years.
However, most teams still manage it like social content. The main
constraint is operational maturity, not creator supply. As a result,
ROI is harder to prove and scaling remains limited. Creator-led
influence is not yet consistently tied to pipeline impact. 

 ‘FIX EVERYTHING' IS THE WRONG SEO STRATEGY (6 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Chasing a perfect audit score is one of the most common resource
drains in SEO, with teams closing tickets on minor HTML issues while
pages sitting just off page one go unoptimized. Up to 67% of in-house
SEO teams cite competing dev priorities as the top barrier to
technical fixes, making prioritization critical. The recommended
approach is "strategic neglect", deliberately deprioritizing
low-impact issues like legacy 404s and cosmetic Lighthouse warnings in
favor of content refreshes and internal linking.

 GOOGLE'S AI CART WILL REMAKE ECOMMERCE (3 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Google is building a Universal Cart that follows users across its
apps, letting them track product prices and complete purchases in one
place. Alongside the Universal Commerce Protocol and Agent Payments
Protocol, it connects merchant data, enables cross-channel checkout,
and supports AI-driven purchasing under user rules. Merchants still
fulfill orders, but Google increasingly handles discovery and
coordination across a persistent shopping experience that spans
platforms.

 WHAT INTUIT'S LAYOFFS MEAN FOR MAILCHIMP CUSTOMERS (6 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 Intuit is repositioning Mailchimp from a core growth engine to a
profit-focused asset after failing to sell it, cutting investment as
growth stalls. User numbers are flat at 11 million, revenue lags other
segments, and pricing remains high versus competitors. While the
platform still offers strong tools and scale, slower innovation and
unclear roadmap direction are pushing marketers, especially those
needing advanced automation, to seriously evaluate alternatives. 

 META BUILT A REDDIT RIVAL OUT OF FACEBOOK GROUPS (2 MINUTE READ) [16]


 Meta's new standalone app, Forum, repurposes Facebook Groups content
into a format designed to compete with Reddit's community-driven Q&A
model. The app leverages Meta's existing advantage of years of
accumulated group discussions, local recommendations, and hobby
knowledge while adding AI-powered search and summarization. 

 ‘AI WASHING': FIRMS ARE SCRAMBLING TO REBRAND THEMSELVES AS
TECH-FOCUSED (3 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 The rush to brand everything as AI is creating a credibility problem
for marketers. PR executives say clients are forcing AI language into
product names, press releases, and executive commentary even when the
technology is simply better automation. With one account director
estimating that half his outbound pitches overstate AI capabilities,
the resulting journalist fatigue is making earned media placement
significantly harder for companies with genuine AI stories to tell.
