 DEBUNKING THE MYTH THAT SEARCH IS DYING (10 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Analysis of 40,000 major sites shows organic search traffic declined
only slightly by 2.5% YoY, with top sites and certain categories even
growing. Claims that AI or LLMs are replacing search or drastically
reducing clicks are based on flawed surveys, small samples, or
anecdotal evidence. Large-scale panel data confirms that Google and
other search engines still drive the vast majority of traffic, and AI
Overviews affect clicks less often than assumed.

 Google Ads campaigns can fail even when optimized because poor unit

had a ¬£309 LTV, which made aggressive bidding profitable.

 THE GREAT DECOUPLING (4 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 SEO traffic no longer predicts pipeline because AI answers remove
clicks while buyer intent remains. Short-head keyword volume grew only
1.2% in the last 12 months and is forecast to fall 0.74% in the next
12 months as demand shifts to long tail queries and AI interfaces.
Pipeline can grow far faster than traffic, such as 32% traffic growth
paired with 75% signup growth or pipeline growing 2.3x faster while
traffic stays flat. SEO now drives brand influence by helping AI
systems understand trust and cite a brand, which lifts demos even when
site visits fall. 

 HOW TO MEASURE BRAND'S IMPACT ON B2B PIPELINE (6 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Most B2B leaders believe brand drives demand, yet few can prove its
impact because traditional attribution models undervalue how brands
actually work. Brands rarely create immediate clicks or conversions.
It builds mental availability that later shows up through search,
trust, and preference. Measuring incrementality by asking what would
have happened without the investment puts brand and demand on equal
footing and reveals its true impact on pipeline and efficiency.
