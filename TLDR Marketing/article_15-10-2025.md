 GLOBAL GROWTH REMAINS SLUGGISH AS TARIFF THREATS LOOM (3 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Global economic growth is expected to slow as trade tensions and
protectionist policies weigh on output. The International Monetary
Fund (IMF) projects global growth will fall to 3.2% in 2025 and 3.1%
in 2026, down from 3.3% last year. The US is forecast to slow to 2%
growth this year from 2.8% in 2024, while China's growth is expected
to drop to 4.8% and continue falling to 4.2% in 2026. The IMF warned
that rising tariffs could fuel inflation in the US as higher import
costs begin to reach consumers. 

 RAGEBAIT IS EVERYWHERE. SHOULD MARKETERS LEAN IN? (5 MINUTE READ) [6]


 Brands and creators are using anger to drive engagement, sometimes on

touching sensitive topics. Reactions can come from personalities,
networks, or unexpected forces. Success comes from cultural awareness,
vetting who's involved, watching responses in real time, and being
clear about values so audiences know where the brand stands.

 THE OPTIMAL EMAIL MARKETING FREQUENCY (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Sending 7 emails monthly is the optimal frequency for maximizing
customer lifetime value. Reducing this to 4 emails cut lifetime profit
by 32%, while increasing it to 10 emails reduced profit by 16%. The
best results come from tailoring email frequency by purchase behavior,
sending 5-7 emails to frequent buyers, 6-10 to medium buyers, and
12-14 to infrequent buyers. Frequent buyers tend to ignore more
emails, while occasional buyers engage more when they receive
additional messages.
