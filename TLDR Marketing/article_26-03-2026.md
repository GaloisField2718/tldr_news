 HOW AN ALARM APP GOT 25 MILLION VIEWS AND 100K DOWNLOADS IN 30 DAYS
(4 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Wayk, a morning alarm app that forces users to complete tasks like
pushups or photos before it turns off, reached 100K downloads and the
#15 spot on the App Store within 30 days. The growth came almost
entirely from TikTok, where the team adapted proven short-form formats
from adjacent productivity apps instead of inventing new content
styles. Each video followed a repeatable structure of a relatable
caption paired with a raw, unpolished reaction to the product. Reddit
and X failed to generate traction, reinforcing that for small consumer
app teams, short-form video is currently the dominant distribution
channel. 

 TIKTOK AD CREATIVE HAS A SHORTER SHELF LIFE (4 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 TikTok ads experience creative fatigue faster than other platforms
due to the algorithm's preference for high-velocity novelty and
entertainment-focused consumption patterns. Advertisers should treat
creative development like a supply chain with modular components,
filming multiple hooks, body segments, and CTAs that can generate 60
ad permutations from one hour of shooting. The hook fatigues fastest
and should be swapped when thumb-stop rates drop below benchmarks for
three consecutive days, while keeping successful body and CTA elements
intact.

 WHY I LOVE THE CONSTRAINTS OF EMAIL (5 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Email's power comes from its constraints. A narrow 600px layout,
outdated HTML, and unpredictable rendering force focus on clarity,
strong copy, and intentional design instead of flashy features. These
limits reduce decision fatigue and spark creativity, pushing marketers
to prioritize the most important message. With fewer distractions and
a defined structure, emails deliver higher ROI and conversions than
more technically capable channels. 

 RAGEBAIT ME, PLS (5 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 Ragebait dominates online culture by provoking outrage for clicks,
turning extreme emotions into entertainment and social currency. It
thrives on polarisation and contradiction, letting people love-to-hate
and hate-to-love the same content. For younger audiences, it becomes a
hyper-literate, playful way to explore culture and subvert norms, but
widespread exposure breeds numbness as emotions are flattened and
gamified by algorithms. The cycle rewards provocation and clarity over
nuance, embedding performative outrage into everyday interaction.
