 IS THE AGE OF READING ACTUALLY OVER? (5 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Nearly 400 independent bookstores opened in 2025, and print book
sales are higher than a decade ago. Readers also spend more time with
books daily than they did twenty years ago. Claims that reading is
dying dismiss this growth as niche while treating declining attention
spans as proof of collapse. Yet people still consume more words across
digital formats. The shift is intentionality. Long-form reading is
becoming a deliberate choice, raising the bar for creators to earn
sustained attention. 

 CONTENT IS NO LONGER ENOUGH (5 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 As content becomes more saturated, brands are focusing less on
producing more and more on building communities. Dentsu, NFL Mexico,
and Warner Bros. Discovery argue that fandom and belonging create
stronger connections than reach alone. Audiences want to participate,
not just consume, making creators and fan communities valuable
partners. The opportunity for brands is to build spaces where people
can engage with shared interests beyond the content itself.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 +39% CONVERSIONS BY REMOVING A PROMINENT CALL-TO-ACTION (2 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 Removing a prominent homepage CTA increased conversions by 39%
because it guided first-time visitors through the service before
asking them to request a quote. The original CTA sent people straight
to a form that asked technical questions many could not answer, so
many left before converting. The updated journey introduced service
details, trust signals, and expert credentials before visitors reached
a quote CTA on the relevant service page. Match each CTA to the
visitor's level of understanding so the next step feels simple and
appropriate instead of asking for a commitment too early. 

 STOP MARKETING TO STRANGERS (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Most marketing over-focuses on acquiring new audiences while
neglecting people who already know you. With declining external
traffic, visitors now arrive with context, yet brands still treat them
like strangers. The real opportunity is вҖңstep-twoвҖқ marketing,
guiding engaged users toward clear next action and deeper content.
Growth comes from improving experience, not just reach. Small
execution gaps like broken promises or poor follow-through quietly
erode retention over time.

[10] 

 AI made producing ads nearly free without creating any new attention
to fill, so more ads now chase the same finite audience and simply
raise the price of being seen. That moves the advantage to the
audience layer, which a competitor cannot reverse-engineer the way it
can copy creative or messaging. Resolving buyer identity before
uploading lists opens the cheaper broad-reach channels that most B2B
teams avoid, and running holdout experiments alongside attribution
reveals which channels actually drive incremental conversions.

 NETFLIX: THE BATTLE FOR ATTENTION (5 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Netflix spent years dismantling scheduled television, and now it's
rebuilding pieces of the same bundle it once disrupted. Live sports,
third-party channels, and short-form video are being layered in to
turn the app into a daily habit rather than a destination for a single
hit show. The tentpole approach, a handful of NFL and MLB games
instead of full season packages, produced six of Netflix's 10 largest
sign-up days over five years despite consuming just over 5% of content
spending. If those additions do not feel cohesive, Netflix risks
becoming the bloated bundle it once replaced. 

 THE $400,000 CHIEF OF STAFF IS THE CEO'S SECRET WEAPON IN THE AI AGE
(5 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI-era CEOs are increasingly hiring chiefs of staff as strategic
partners, not just assistants. The role has grown as leaders face
greater complexity, with chiefs of staff helping execute initiatives
and build workflows. While AI may automate some administrative tasks,
skills like judgment, communication, and influence remain difficult to
replace. The position is also becoming a leadership pipeline, with
many chiefs of staff moving into executive roles.
