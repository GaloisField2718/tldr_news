 THE MOST IGNORED DATA IN MARKETING (SALES CALLS!) (4 MINUTE READ) [8]


 Most marketers ignore sales call transcripts even though about 75% of
startups record them, which makes this data a major untapped
advantage. This article shows how meeting transcripts paired with a
custom GPT create a qualitative engine that reveals intent patterns,
repeated questions, emotional shifts, and messaging gaps that SQL data
cannot surface. The setup requires collecting transcripts into a
single file, uploading it into a custom GPT, and adding detailed
behavior rules so the model can extract patterns and answer strategic
questions. This system becomes a long-term asset that can inform
messaging, coach sales teams, train AI tools, and scale qualitative
analysis without manual work. 

 STELLA ARTOIS TURNS GOOGLE MAPS REVIEWERS INTO BRAND AMBASSADORS (1
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Stella Artois activated an unconventional campaign by targeting
Google Maps' Local Guides. The brand hosted top restaurant reviewers
at over 40 dining locations where Stella was served as part of the
meal. These guests organically posted photos and reviews, integrating
the beer into discovery experiences for future diners. With no option
for traditional ads on Google Maps, the strategy ensured Stella
appeared in highly trusted, user-generated content at key decision
points.

 YOUR DATA ISN'T PERSONAL ENOUGH FOR A WRAPPED EMAIL? (8 MINUTE READ)
[14] 

 Wrapped emails don't need personal data to be effective. The key is
presenting surprising or shareable stats that tell a story.
Highlighting top products, company milestones, and community
achievements makes customers feel part of the brand's success. Simple
visuals, playful comparisons, and a clear narrative keep the email
engaging and memorable. When done right, these emails validate
customer choices and create a sense of collective impact. 

 YOU'RE RUNNING THE WRONG TYPE OF CAB (10 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Customer Advisory Boards are often misused when one board is expected
to solve every problem for every audience. Strong CABs serve a
specific purpose, such as retaining executive relationships, gathering
product feedback, or hosting focused discussions. Companies should
start small, build for exclusivity, measure relationship depth, and
integrate CABs into a larger Customer-Led Growth system. CABs are one
pillar of customer strategy, not a replacement for community,
education, or lifecycle programs.
