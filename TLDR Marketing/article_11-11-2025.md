 WHY ADVERTISERS CAN'T OVERLOOK THE BUYING POWER OF VETERANS (3 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 Veterans are a high-income, tech-enabled audience with large
purchasing power across home improvement, electronics, and digital
shopping. With an average household income of $115K and above-average
engagement with live and cable TV, they represent a premium consumer
segment. Their strong presence in small business ownership and

 WHY DO THE YOUNG TRUST BRANDS MORE THAN THE OLD? (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Research shows that younger people report trusting brands more than
older people. The difference reflects how each group defines trust.
Over-35s tend to trust charities, financial services, and healthcare
providers, valuing ethics and safety. Meanwhile, under-35s trust
everyday retailers and online services for reliability and
performance. For older consumers, trust is a filter that must be
earned through values and customer care. For younger consumers, trust
is a baseline, and marketing should demonstrate consistent service and
reliability.

 HOW DO YOU MEASURE THE ROI OF TIMELESSNESS? (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Timelessness is a brand's ability to stay relevant as trends change.
It signals consistency and endurance seen in brands like Apple and
Chanel. Timeless brands grow through trust, design, consistency, and
resilience. Trust builds loyalty and profit, while design creates
recognition and emotional connection. Consistency drives efficiency,
and resilience protects pricing and market share. Measuring
timelessness means tracking retention and execution efficiency. The
payoff comes from patience and disciplined brand building that
compounds over time.
