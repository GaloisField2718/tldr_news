 WHAT IS AN A/A TEST AND WHEN DO I HAVE TO RUN ONE? (10 MINUTE READ)
[7] 

 A/A testing helps verify that A/B testing tools are functioning
correctly by splitting traffic between identical pages to check for
data accuracy and randomness. It builds confidence in test results by
revealing baseline conversion rates and natural variance, critical for
interpreting future experiments. While not designed to uncover new
insights, A/A tests are valuable for validating tools, identifying
setup errors, and estimating sample sizes.

 HOW BIG ARE GOOGLE'S GROUNDING CHUNKS? (2 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Google uses a fixed grounding budget of about 2,000 words per query,
and page length does not increase that limit. It splits this budget by
relevance rank, where the #1 source gets about 531 words or 28% and
the #5 source gets about 266 words or 13%. Most pages receive about
377 words, and 77% of pages fall between 200 and 600 words selected.
Coverage drops as pages get longer, since pages over 2,000 words
average about 13% coverage and grounding plateaus near 540 words.
Concise and dense content is more effective than long pages.
