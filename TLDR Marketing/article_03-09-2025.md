 GOOGLE WON'T HAVE TO SELL CHROME OR ANDROID, BUT HAS TO SHARE SEARCH
DATA (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Google avoided a forced sell-off of Chrome or Android after the court
ruled these products were not directly used to maintain its search
monopoly. However, the ruling introduces a major shift by requiring
Google to share parts of its search index and user-interaction data
with qualified competitors. This opens the door for rivals to improve
their own search products, potentially impacting search ad performance
and traffic dynamics over time. 

 HOW OFTEN DO AI ASSISTANTS HALLUCINATE LINKS? (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI assistants send visitors to 404 pages 2.87X more often than Google
search. ChatGPT had the highest 404 rates, with 1.01% of clicked URLs
and 2.38% of cited URLs returning errors, compared to Google's 0.15%
and 0.84%. Claude, Copilot, Perplexity, and Gemini had lower error
rates, with Mistral being the lowest, but based on the smallest
sample. Many errors stem from outdated links or hallucinated URLs that
mimic real site structures but never existed. The study suggests using
analytics to identify these links and either redirecting traffic or
improving 404 pages to help visitors.

BRANDS ACROSS THE GLOBE (9 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 The sudden end of the US de minimis exemption, which allowed
shipments under $800 to enter duty-free, is disrupting global retail
supply chains. It will raise costs for businesses and increase prices
for consumers. Initially set to expire in 2027, the policy was
abruptly terminated by executive order, impacting companies from small
Etsy sellers to major brands like Tapestry and Lululemon that had
built business models around it. Used by e-commerce giants like Shein
and Temu, the exemption's removal is forcing businesses to restructure
logistics, delay shipments, and, in some cases, halt US sales.

 THE COUNTERINTUITIVE MODEL: THE GROWTH MODEL THAT OUTPERFORMS
FREEMIUM AND FREE TRIALS (4 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Most SaaS startups struggle with free users converting, as freemium
models only convert about 2-5% and traditional trials lose around 75%
of signups after expiration. A reverse trial gives new users full
premium access for a limited period, then downgrades them to a free
tier if they do not upgrade, combining acquisition benefits of
freemium with conversion benefits of trials. Companies like Canva,
Toggl Track, and Grammarly use this model successfully, with Toggl
Track reporting doubled premium revenue after switching. Reverse
trials work best when the key value sits behind paid features,
onboarding is complex, or standout features need early exposure.

 DISTRIBUTION BEFORE PRODUCT: THE OPERATOR'S 90-DAY GTM PLAYBOOK -
WITH AI PROMPTS (23 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Most startups fail because they lack distribution, not because their
products are bad. Customer acquisition costs have risen 300-400% in
the past 3 years, stretching CAC payback periods to 18-24 months,
which makes traditional paid marketing unsustainable.
Distribution-first companies succeed by building owned channels,
audiences, and credibility before launching products, using tactics
like waitlists, viral loops, and shareable templates. Case studies
such as Suno, Robinhood, and Notion show that strategies like
community-driven growth, gamified referrals, and viral content can
drive rapid adoption. A structured 90-day playbook helps founders
build compounding distribution assets that reduce CAC by at least 40%
and create defensible growth advantages. 

 HOW TO QUICKLY CREATE QUALITY MUSIC AND VIDEO (5 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Music platforms like Udio and Suno allow marketers to define tone,
instruments, and vocals with simple text prompts. Visuals begin with
quick image generation in Midjourney and evolve into animated assets
that fit the overall concept with tools like Runway. By starting with
music, teams can set the emotional and structural foundation for the
final product, then assemble everything in editing software like
Premiere to ensure quality and consistency.
