demand, while massive investments in data centers, chips, and power
push firms to justify usage and accelerate adoption.

 HOW HUBSPOT SOLVED THE TWO-DATE PROBLEM (1 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Showing both publish and updated dates can hurt CTR if Google
surfaces the older one in search results. HubSpot avoids this by
displaying only the updated date in standard format while listing the
original publish timing as plain text, which Google ignores. This
keeps search listings looking fresh while still giving readers
context, helping maintain SEO performance. 

 THE BACKSTORY BEHIND CLAY'S NEW PRICING MODEL (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Clay updated its pricing to reflect platform use beyond data, adding
an “actions” metric alongside data credits and reducing tiers from
5 to 4. The team spent a year testing with agencies and customer
segments to ensure fairness. Actions remain invisible for most users
while guiding power users to upgrade. Transparent communication
included videos, memos, and grandfathering legacy customers.
Short-term revenue may drop 10%, but long-term upgrades are expected
as action usage grows.

 THE SCIENCE OF HOW AI PICKS ITS SOURCES (8 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI citations are concentrated among a few domains, with content
breadth and structure outweighing rankings. About 30 domains capture
67% of citations, and top Google results are cited only 43.2% of the
time. ChatGPT retrieves many sources but cites few, with 85% of pages
never cited and 67% of cited URLs appearing only once. Longer,
structured content covering multiple queries performs best, and
placing key information in the first 30% of a page maximizes
attention. 

 SEARCH CONSOLE'S AVERAGE POSITION, EXPLAINED (2 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Average position in GSC is an aggregated metric that often misleads
and offers little actionable insight, so it is usually best ignored.
An overall average position of 25 means a site ranks around page 3,
since Google shows about 10 organic results per page. Query-level
average position is more useful because it reflects how a specific URL
ranks across users, such as averaging 1.5 if it appears in position 1
for one search and 2 for another. Special elements like image packs
and AI Overviews count as positions, and appearing in them can raise a
URL to position 1 even if its organic ranking is lower. Reported
positions can vary due to personalization, device differences, and
limited visibility of features like “People also ask,” which makes
exact rankings harder to verify.

 GOODBYE TWO BY TWO. HELLO TWO BY NOTHING. A FRAMEWORK FOR BUILDING
DISTINCTIVE BRANDS (4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The “two by nothing” framework shows competitors fighting over
dimensions like price or quality, while the brand creates a new plane
where it stands alone. Airbnb illustrates this by shifting from a
place to stay to a way to experience a destination or travel together,
which enables more distinctive campaigns and positioning. The approach
requires mapping competitors, uncovering brand and audience insights,
and reframing industry assumptions into something people care about.
It works even if the product is similar, since differentiation can
come from values and identity rather than features. 

 THIS REDDIT WATCHDOG CHANGED EVERYTHING BRANDS THOUGHT THEY KNEW
ABOUT THE PLATFORM (5 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 The subreddit r/companiesthatspam appeared in February to call out
brands astroturfing on Reddit, quickly ranking in Google search. It
was removed within a month, but the risk remains. Reddit is the
top-cited domain on major AI platforms at 3.11%, ahead of YouTube and
Wikipedia, so bot-driven backlash can appear in AI searches. Brands
like Mint Mobile that maintain genuine community engagement outperform
those using agency-run manipulation. 

 WHAT HAPPENS WHEN A BRAND FAILS TO DELIVER ON ITS BASIC PROMISE (5
MINUTE READ) [16] 

 FedEx's slogan, "The World on Time," sets high expectations, so any
delivery failure feels like a breach. Despite a 95.3% on-time rate,
PwC found 1 in 3 consumers will leave a brand after one bad
experience. A recent 45-hour delay on a priority shipment shows how
gaps like drivers missing store hours and poor customer service cause
more reputational damage than the delay itself. Brands with high
expectations need recovery processes to match.
