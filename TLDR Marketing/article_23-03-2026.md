NEWS & TRENDS

 YET ANOTHER GLOBAL STUDY SAYS INSTAGRAM AND TIKTOK ARE BAD FOR YOUR
MENTAL HEALTH (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A new global study tied to the World Happiness Report 2026 finds that
algorithm-driven platforms are more strongly associated with poorer
mental health than communication-focused apps like WhatsApp and
Facebook. The distinction is how people use social media rather than
how much, with passive scrolling and curated content linked to lower
life satisfaction, while direct interaction shows neutral or positive
effects. The impact is strongest among younger users, particularly
teenage girls, where heavy daily use correlated with significantly
lower happiness levels. 

 WHAT KEEPS VIEWERS WATCHING FACEBOOK REELS? (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Facebook Reels performance is driven by early hooks and format, not
trends. Videos with speech in the first 3 seconds see +24.7% higher
10-second retention and +5.6% more engagement than music-only content.
Vertical videos boost reach by +20.9% and 30-second retention by
+38.5%. Showing a person early increases short-term retention by
+10.1%. For very short clips, seamless loops lift replays by +18.7%
and reach by +23.6%. Prioritize voice, human presence, and vertical
format to keep viewers watching.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 RETENTION TEAR SHEET (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Start small by focusing on one high-dropoff point, like onboarding or
re-engagement, and build a targeted program around it. Work closely
with customer success to understand real user pain points, and track
behavior that signals progress like activation or feature use, not
just email metrics. Repurpose existing content instead of adding
tools, and formally define lifecycle as a function so it can be owned
and measured. Tie your first program directly to a revenue metric like
activation, churn, or expansion to prove impact before scaling. 

 THREAD WALLETS MARKETING BREAKDOWN (3 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Thread Wallets grew by turning a basic product into a
personality-driven accessory and validating demand before scaling. It
proved demand early through Kickstarter, where its second campaign
raised $35,000 against a $5,000 goal. The company scaled fast by using
micro influencers, campus distribution, and Facebook Marketplace ads,
which drove a 41% increase in return on ad spend. It focused on a
clear audience, which ended up being about 90% female, and used
customer data to guide decisions and avoid wasted effort.

MISCELLANEOUS

 THE FUTURE OF CONTENT BELONGS TO THE TASTEMAKERS (5 MINUTE READ) [15]


 As content becomes a commodity, leading brands treat editorial
judgment as a strategic asset, not a volume play. Publishing more can
mask declining interest until it's too late, as audiences disengage
over time. 74% of consumers abandon purchases when overwhelmed, a
pattern that applies to content. The advantage comes from scaling
taste through curated reference sets and experienced editors who
decide what's worth sharing. 

 THE INTERNET RUINED CUSTOMER SERVICE. AI COULD SAVE IT (9 MINUTE
READ) [16] 

 The internet scaled commerce but weakened customer service, turning
people into tickets. Concierge-level care stayed limited to luxury
brands because it's expensive. AI changes that by making always-on
attention cheap and scalable. Companies can now offer high-touch
service to everyone. Customer service shifts from a cost center to a
driver of loyalty and revenue.
