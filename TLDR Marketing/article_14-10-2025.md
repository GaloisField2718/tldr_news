 GOOGLE SAYS AUSTRALIAN LAW ON TEEN SOCIAL MEDIA USE 'EXTREMELY
DIFFICULT' TO ENFORCE (2 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Google said Australia's new law banning social media for people under
16 will be hard to enforce and won't improve online safety. The law,
effective in December, requires platforms to estimate users' ages with
AI instead of verifying them. Google argued it could cause unintended
effects and said YouTube shouldn't be treated as a social platform.
Australia will be the first country to impose such a ban. 

 HUGE PROPORTION OF YOUNG AMERICANS NOW REPORTING SERIOUS COGNITIVE
ISSUES (3 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Cognitive issues among Americans aged 18-39 have nearly doubled from
5.1% to 9.7% over the past decade. The sharpest increases appeared
among people earning under $35K, those without a high school diploma,
and Indigenous Americans. Researchers point to factors like economic
stress, inequality, and technology use, warning that the trend could
affect long-term health and productivity.

 TIME TO ANSWER BY GO FISH (TOOL) [9] 

 This free Chrome extension measures how long it takes a visitor to
reach an answer on the page they're reading. It's designed to help
spot why visitors bounce before they convert. To use it, open the side
panel, set your question and reading speed, and click the element that
contains the answer. 

 DOES QUORA MARKETING STILL WORK? YES. HERE'S 5 NEW IDEAS (17 MINUTE
READ) [10] 

 Quora ranks for over 191M keywords and is frequently cited in
AI-generated search results. With 410M monthly organic visitors, the
platform aligns user intent with content structure, as visitors
typically look for specific answers during mid-funnel research.
Content that follows a structured format, such as answer, explanation,
example, and takeaway, has a higher likelihood of being extracted by
AI platforms. Quora Spaces serve as thematic repositories for
educational content, and paid tools like Promoted Answers and Lead Gen
Forms support on-platform engagement and data capture.

 FIRST COMES THE INNOVATOR, THEN COMES THE INNOVATIVE MARKETER (3
MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Innovation alone doesn't sustain market leadership. Brands win by
pairing it with marketing innovation. SharkNinja outpaced Dyson by
investing 20-25% of revenue in agile marketing versus Dyson's 2-4%.
Similarly, Alo and Fabletics surpass Lululemon through storytelling
and influencer strategy. The strongest brands blend product and
marketing innovation through collaboration and adaptability. 

 YOU'RE PAYING YOUR SALES AND MARKETING TEAMS TO SABOTAGE EACH OTHER
(6 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Too many orgs reward marketing for quantity and sales for quality,
setting them up to clash and fail. Instead, focus on bringing
high-fit, high-LTV leads that sales can confidently close. Tactics
include passing product usage insights back into content and
campaigns, and syncing on a shared success metric like pipeline
velocity. Real alignment means using sales feedback within 48 hours
and creating evergreen marketing loops powered by real customer data.

pixel, showing how vital accurate tracking is. Shopify integrates the
Meta Pixel and Meta's Conversion API to measure results, identify
target audiences, and refine ad strategies based on real purchase
