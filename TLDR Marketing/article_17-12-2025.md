 HOW TO BE COMPETENT VS FUN ON SOCIALS (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Follower growth depends on whether the audience wants information or
entertainment. When people seek information, balanced content builds
credibility and drives follows, as Yelp accounts with 3 to 4 star
ratings gained up to 105.5% more followers than overly positive ones
and up to 500% more than consistently negative ones. When people seek
entertainment, highly positive content performs best and drives up to
116.3% more followers on average. Brands focused on sales should match
tone to intent, since friendly and genuine content converts better
even when credibility signals like verification are present. 

 6 PIECES OF ADVICE TO BUILDING YOUR PERSONAL BRAND ON LI (2 MINUTE

avoid writing cold, and use tools to simplify content creation. Be
approachable and relatable rather than overly polished. Make sure your
profile reflects your personality and story. Consistency matters more
than perfection.

 COMBATING THE CONSUMER â€˜JOY DEFICIT' THIS SEASON (5 MINUTE READ)
[12] 

 Holiday campaigns need to reflect the reality that most shoppers are
financially stressed and price-conscious. Brands are seeing stronger
results from early sales, resale options, and emotionally resonant
messages instead of pressure-heavy promotions. Highlighting group
activities over individual gifts and bundling affordable luxuries can
help shoppers feel joy without overspending. Consumers are highly
sensitive to pricing tricks, so clear value and trust are more
important than urgency this season. 

 IF YOU QUIT SOCIAL MEDIA, WILL YOU READ MORE BOOKS? (7 MINUTE READ)
[13] 

 Quitting social media may free time, but it won't automatically make
people read more books. Social platforms shape reading habits by
connecting users with tailored content. It fosters focused exploration
and helps discover niche interests. Reddit, TikTok, and Substack can
expose readers to books they wouldn't otherwise find, though they may
also reinforce filter bubbles. While in-person book clubs offer richer
intellectual engagement, social media enables personalized learning,
making the effects on literacy complex and nuanced.
