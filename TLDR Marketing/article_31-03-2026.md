 CONSUMER SENTIMENT FALLS 6%, HIT BY DECLINING STOCKS, HIGHER GAS
PRICES (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Consumer sentiment fell 6% to its lowest level this year as the Iran
war pushed up gas prices and slashed stock values. Short-term economic
outlooks dropped 14%, and personal finance expectations fell 10%.
Gasoline prices and stock market losses hit middle- and upper-income
households hardest. Sentiment could worsen if the conflict persists,
with headline inflation expected to reach 4.2% and growth slowing to
2% this year.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 EMAIL NURTURE SEQUENCE FRAMEWORK (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 This framework builds a B2B email nurture sequence by defining
audience, story, problem, value, action, and post-nurture experience.
Use storytelling and content that solves the problem (templates,
frameworks, or case studies). Segment by problem, job, vertical, or
company size, and send from a real human, responding to replies. Treat
each email as an experiment. Test content, CTAs, format, and
incentives, then iterate. Include exclusive assets and use
post-nurture actions like demos, roundtables, or trials to drive
engagement. 

 MARRIOTT BETS ON REAL-TIME CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE TO INDUSTRIALISE

 Marriott is shifting its loyalty strategy from points to emotional

Marriott as a lifelong travel partner across the full journey, from
inspiration to post stay engagement.

 THE CXO NEWSLETTER PLAYBOOK: WHY 95% FAIL (AND THE 5-PROMPT STRATEGY
SYSTEM) (20 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Most CXO newsletters fail because they skip validation and publish
generic content that drives no pipeline. Traditional channels are
weakening, with cold email reply rates at 3-5% and LinkedIn reach at
1.6%. Niche newsletters can reach 30-45% open rates and build
compounding relationships when they target a clear CXO persona and
deliver useful insights. Even 200-300 subscribers can drive 15-20
qualified meetings a year. This piece includes a 5 prompt system to
test demand, competition, ROI, and fit before investing. 

 BLUESKY BUILT A NEW AI TOOL THAT WANTS TO FREE YOU FROM SOCIAL
ALGORITHMS (2 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Bluesky's Attie is an AI assistant that lets users create custom
social media feeds through conversational commands rather than relying
on platform-controlled algorithms. The app runs on Bluesky's AT
Protocol and pulls data from across the entire ecosystem to build
personalized timelines instantly. Attie represents a deliberate
contrast to how major platforms use AI primarily to increase
engagement and data collection. The long-term vision includes allowing
users to build complete social applications without coding knowledge.

 THE SCIENCE OF WHAT AI ACTUALLY REWARDS (7 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 AI citation behavior varies by industry, not a single formula. Many
SEO tactics don't scale. A clear opening line boosts citations by
about 14%, while hedging hurts performance across all seven verticals.
Including a date and a number consistently helps. Price mentions
reduce citations in most verticals, and Knowledge Graph entities show
negative impact. Heading structure varies widely, from 5 to 9 in
crypto to 20 plus in SaaS. Corporate content drives 94.7% of
citations, while UGC ranges from 0.5% in finance to 9.2% in crypto. 

 WHY YOUR PRICING CHANGES TAKE QUARTERS INSTEAD OF HOURS (14 MINUTE
READ) [15] 

 Pricing changes at most SaaS companies take a full quarter of
engineering time because access rules and billing logic are
hard-coded. AI makes this worse as usage shifts more often. The fix is
a four-layer monetization setup that separates access from
enforcement, so non-engineers can manage plans and experiments.
Superhuman waited six years to raise prices by $10, and even with 4%
churn, saw a 28% overnight revenue lift that compounds over time.
