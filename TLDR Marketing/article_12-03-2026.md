 WHY DOES EVERYONE HATE AI? (8 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Public hostility toward AI stems from a mix of distrust in tech,
economic anxiety, and cultural resistance to synthetic tools. Public
opinion had already turned against tech before AI launched due to
issues like the Cambridge Analytica scandal and concerns about social
media's effect on mental health. Many people fear job loss because AI
arrived during a weak economic moment and threatens knowledge work
rather than physical labor. Creatives shape culture and view AI as a
threat to identity and artistic work, which fuels backlash online.
Adoption will require companies to focus on real benefits like medical
breakthroughs, highlight everyday users instead of executives, and
support worker retraining as AI reshapes labor markets. 

 AI IS MUCH BIGGER THAN YOU THINK (12 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI usage is far larger than commonly reported, with 45B monthly

dominates the market, holding 89% of global AI sessions and 86% in the
US, while search traffic continues to grow alongside AI, proving usage
is additive rather than zero-sum. AI adoption is rapidly expanding,

 HOW TO BUILD INSANELY CREATIVE DIGITAL PR CAMPAIGNS WITH KATY POWELL
(15 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Creative digital PR campaigns require bold ideas, strong validation,
and clear measurement tied to business outcomes. Test ideas through
topic analysis and journalist feedback before committing to them.
Successful campaigns often include multiple story angles and realistic
timelines of 4 to 6 weeks to reduce risk if the main concept fails.

real marketing goals. 

 THE SIMPLE GENIUS BEHIND THIS LONG-FORGOTTEN GOOGLE CHROME AD (2
MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Committing to one clear strength builds more credibility than
competing on range. Research on the goal dilution effect shows that
adding benefits to a product claim actively weakens persuasion, with a
study demonstrating a 12% drop in perceived effectiveness when a
second benefit was listed alongside the first. Chrome ignored its
security features and Gmail integration at launch and led only with
speed. The browser now holds 71% of the global market. Five Guys
followed the same logic in 1986, limiting its menu to burgers and
fries and growing over 700% in six years.

 Visual craft ranks first with 58% of designers and hiring managers

 WHAT SHOULD YOUR COMPANY'S AI SOUND LIKE TO CUSTOMERS? (6 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 As AI voice systems move toward handling 70-80% of customer service
interactions by 2028-2029, most organizations have no framework for
calibrating how confident their AI sounds relative to what it actually
knows. Research in vocal psychology shows confident delivery increases
persuasiveness and triggers authority deference independent of
information quality, meaning a moderately certain AI recommendation
can be heard as definitive when spoken aloud. The fix requires
institutional governance that classifies interactions by risk and
assigns vocal delivery rules accordingly, paired with real-time
systems that adjust tone based on model confidence and user context.
