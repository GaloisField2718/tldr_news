 CONSUMERS SPENT MORE ON MOBILE APPS THAN GAMES IN 2025 (3 MINUTE

 Consumers spent more on non-game apps than games for the first time

 RESULTS FROM UNGATED CONTENT (1 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 After spending $15K to generate just 106 low-quality ebook leads, a
team realized gated content was blocking real engagement. Only 3% of
visitors filled out the form, so they ungated everything and stopped
reporting on leads. They measured cost per engagement and focused on
pipeline and revenue. One year later, demo requests rose 265%,
pipeline grew 242%, and cost per engagement dropped 90%. By letting
people opt in only when they wanted the content, the team showed
ungating drives far more business impact than chasing low-quality
leads. 

 LET PEOPLE KEEP THEIR RETURNS (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Letting customers keep low to moderately-priced items while issuing a
full refund increases repeat purchase intent and brand sentiment,
making customers up to 2x more likely to buy again. Across 9
experiments, returnless returns drove 24.6% higher ratings, up to
30.1% more positive reviews, and 30% higher perceived value compared
to standard return policies. The effect is stronger when it is framed
as customer convenience or an environmental choice and when customers
are encouraged to donate items, but it weakens if proof is required,
causing 20% less positive word of mouth. The approach works best for
frequently repurchased and lower-cost items, while profitability
depends on category, price, and fraud controls. 

 HOW TO MANUALLY TRACK SENTIMENT (1 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Understanding brand sentiment does not require expensive tools.
Collect 100 comments from influencer posts, reposts, and related
social channels like Reddit, Facebook groups, or TikTok. In a
spreadsheet, create columns for positive, neutral, and negative.
Positive includes intent to buy, or tagging friends. Neutral covers
mentions or facts. Negative includes skepticism, complaints, or
comparisons. Count comments in each column and divide by the total to
get a sentiment score.
