 WE STOPPED FORCING THE SUBSCRIPTION MODEL ON OUR USERS (5 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Lovable found that forcing a subscription-only model hurt retention
because user demand was irregular and many users rejected ongoing
commitments. It introduced credit top-ups instead and A/B tested
pricing, and a 20% premium over subscription credit prices performed
best while a 40% premium reduced adoption. The 20% markup increased
engagement and bookings, and paid plan retention rose by 7%, even
though upgrades to higher plans dropped by nearly half. Within one
week, 20%+ of bookings came from top-ups, and subscription revenue
still grew. Flexible pay-as-you-go options matched real usage patterns
better and drove stronger long-term value than subscription-only
billing.

MINUTE READ) [11]

higher risk of targeted attacks. It mitigates prompt injection and
data exfiltration by restricting external interactions, limiting live
web access, and disabling tools that cannot meet strict data safety

 HOW PINTEREST HACKED GOOGLE IMAGES (3 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Pinterest captured massive traffic from Google Images by turning UGC
into an SEO engine. Search crawlers read text rather than images.
Pinterest structured board titles, pin descriptions, and comments into
indexable pages, then built templated pin, board, and programmatic
explore pages designed to match high volume queries. Each user action
created a new searchable entry point, forming a self-reinforcing
growth loop that drove hundreds of millions of organic visits. A login
wall gated full content behind registration, converting search traffic
at scale until Google's 2018 algorithm update penalized the tactic and
erased millions of rankings. 

 THE SCIENCE OF HOW AI PAYS ATTENTION (6 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 AI citation behavior favors front-loaded and entity-rich writing over
long narrative structure. 44.2% of citations come from the first 30%
of a page, while only 24.7% come from the last third. Key facts buried
deep are 2.5X less likely to be cited. Citation-heavy passages use
definitive statements, include questions, and show high-entity density
(using specific names like Salesforce or Pipedrive). Paragraph
analysis shows 53% of cited sentences sit in the middle of a
paragraph, so strong facts can appear after the first sentence if the
paragraph appears early on the page. Citation winners also use a
balanced tone and a readability level near grade 16, similar to
outlets like Harvard Business Review.
