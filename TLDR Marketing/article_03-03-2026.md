 HOW GOOGLE'S UNIVERSAL COMMERCE PROTOCOL CHANGES ECOMMERCE SEO (9
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Product feeds and structured data now determine visibility in
Google's AI shopping. AI agents can discover, compare, and complete
purchases, so optimization targets being chosen, not ranked. Brands
compete in the recommendation layer and need accurate product data.

Center helps teams monitor performance and fix issues. Clear product

 HOW TO THINK ABOUT INFLUENCERS OR: DEATH TO AUTHENTICITY (22 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 Influencer marketing is considered the fourth age of media, after
print, TV, and search. Social platforms now let creators shape culture
in ways traditional media cannot. Long-term ROI from influencer
campaigns is 51% higher than the all-channel average. Brands see a 38%
lift in ROI when working with creators over time rather than in
one-off deals. Yet 45% of marketers still judge influencers by
follower count, and 80% of brands take “baby steps” in creator
strategy.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HOW TO BUILD AN AI SEO STRATEGY THAT OUTLASTS TACTICS (6 MINUTE READ)
[8] 

 Teams often chase visibility in AI tools without connecting it to
revenue or market share. Strong strategies start with research on
where audiences use AI and which queries drive pipeline. Behavior
studies show user patterns often differ from assumptions. Content
structure matters, as 44% of AI citations come from the first 30% of a
page, making front-loaded answers critical. Leaders respond better to
scenario planning than traffic forecasts, so model outcomes and review
the strategy quarterly to stay aligned with fast-moving AI platforms. 

 PARTIFUL: WHAT WE CAN LEARN FROM THE APP THAT KILLED FACEBOOK EVENTS
(8 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Partiful outpaced legacy event platforms by embedding growth into its
product. Every invite created built-in distribution and FOMO.
Serialized content on Substack and TikTok engaged users. Org Profiles
and Group Order fostered communities and added utility, while playful
responses to competitors reinforced confidence. By aligning product
and positioning with Gen Z's desire for low-friction social
experiences, Partiful built a self-sustaining growth loop.

 WHAT REAL MARKETERS ARE BUILDING WITH CLAUDE CODE (16 MINUTE READ)

 This piece breaks down how marketers are building reusable AI skills

MISCELLANEOUS

 THE MINIMUM LOVABLE PRODUCT ERA (7 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Minimal Lovable Products are replacing MVPs because basic
functionality is now cheap and easy to copy. AI has cut build costs
from about $200K to as little as $20 or free, so simple utility no
longer creates an advantage. Most products still sit at the functional
and reliable layers, while lovability drives higher retention, LTV,
and word of mouth. Emotional touches such as personality, delight
moments, and human tone are now the strongest moat since AI can
replicate features quickly. Teams should build delight into roadmaps
and set a clear standard that every release must feel genuinely
enjoyable to use. 

 THE HIDDEN RISK OF A STRONG BRAND HALO (3 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Johnson & Johnson's Tylenol recall built a powerful reputation that
later softened scrutiny around repeated legal settlements across its
pharmaceutical and consumer businesses. That defining moment created
lasting credibility that shaped both public perception and internal
culture. When ethical identity becomes central to a brand, misconduct
is easier to rationalize. Reputation must be reinforced by ongoing
accountability, or it becomes a shield instead of a safeguard.
