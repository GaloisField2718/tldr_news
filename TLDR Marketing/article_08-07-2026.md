NEWS & TRENDS

 HOW TECH WORKERS ARE FEELING IN 2026: A WORKFORCE SPLITTING IN TWO
(13 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 55.7% of tech workers now report significant burnout, up from 44.7% a
year ago, while career optimism has dropped to 48.7% from 54.8%. Only
22% cite losing their job to AI as a top fear, and the correlation
between AI replacing parts of a job and layoff worry sits at
essentially zero. What's actually driving the anxiety is the squeeze,
since AI raised the bar for output and the reward has been more work
for the same pay rather than fewer hours. More than half of
respondents say they would steer a newcomer away from their own field,
producing an average career recommendation score of -39. 

 AI OVERVIEWS ARE EXPANDING ACROSS COMMERCIAL INTENT SEARCH (6 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 AI Overviews appeared on 71% more commercial-intent search results,
expanding well beyond the informational queries where they first took
hold. Finance saw the sharpest jump, up 231% in the period, followed
by computers and electronics at 108% and games at 77%. Transactional
queries moved in the opposite direction, with AI Overview appearance
falling 5% as Google increasingly reserves them for the research phase
of a purchase rather than the moment someone is ready to buy. Google
Ads and AI Overviews are also showing up together roughly twice as
often as a year ago, putting more paid and AI-generated real estate on
the same results page.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 TURN DAILY MOMENTS INTO VIRAL LINKEDIN POSTS (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Content works better when it starts with a relatable moment because
personal stories stand out in a feed full of AI-generated advice.
Start with a real event, build one clear message, and match the post
to one of three goals: personal, proof, or promo. The STORM structure
gives each post a simple flow from hook to takeaway and helps readers
stay engaged. Specific hooks and a closing that connects back to the
opening create stronger engagement and clearer calls to action. 

 BRANDS SEE CONTENT GOLD IN MICRODRAMAS. WILL THEY MUCK UP THE MOMENT?
(7 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Microdramas give brands a low-cost way to build attention through
entertainment instead of traditional ads, but success depends on
strong storytelling before product promotion. Short episodes that open
with a hook, end with a cliffhanger, and include clear business goals
can create stronger engagement and commerce opportunities. Audience
interest is growing fast as time spent with short drama apps increased
by 5.78 billion hours in 2025, while Crocs generated nearly 10 million
views from its first branded microdrama. Start with small tests,
measure repeat viewing and binge behavior instead of only impressions,
and avoid forcing products into the story because audiences quickly
reject content that feels like an infomercial.

SEARCH (1 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Google Search Console now lets marketers measure how social and video
content performs in Google Search and Discover across Instagram,
TikTok, X, and YouTube. The new platform properties show which search

 HOW WE'RE KEEPING REDDIT REAL AND SAFE IN THE AI ERA (3 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Reddit is using AI-driven systems to proactively detect spam, bots,
and harmful content before users see it. These upgrades now block 23M
spam views daily, cut user exposure to spam by 20%, and revoke nearly
2M fake votes per day. Enforcement on hate and violent content has
also dropped to under five seconds, reducing exposure by over 40%.

 IT'S ALL ABOUT FRAMING (2 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Framing can increase customer value as much as product quality
because the right partner changes how people perceive and use an
offer. Robinhood generated more than 50% of Kalshi's trading volume by
presenting prediction markets inside an investing app, which made
larger wagers feel normal to investment-minded users. Partnerships can
also reposition a brand, as Apple used Hermès to present the Apple
Watch as a luxury accessory and New Balance used fashion
collaborations to change its image. Products are easier to copy than
brand perception, so partnerships that expand reach and create
stronger mental associations can create a lasting advantage. 

 NETFLIX INVENTED BINGE-WATCHING. NOW IT MAY HAVE OUTGROWN IT (5
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Viewers are increasingly abandoning popular Netflix shows before a
second season, driven by frequent cancellations, long gaps between
seasons, and algorithm-shaped programming. Netflix's binge model was
built to beat linear TV, a fight streaming already won when it
overtook combined broadcast and cable viewing in 2025, but the
platform now competes with short-form video and microdrama apps
instead. 

 THE RISE OF RAGEBAIT LIT (5 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Buzz-heavy memoirs and novels are sparking intense online debate.
Group chats, Substack, and book clubs are driving the conversation.
These books center on messy personal lives and polarizing choices.
Readers engage for the drama but often feel personally invested in the
authors, which sharpens reactions. Conversations move beyond hot takes
into questions about feminism, relationships, and autonomy. The trend
echoes the confessional era but feels more fractured today. Cultural
regression shapes the tone, turning outrage into a way to process
collective anger.
