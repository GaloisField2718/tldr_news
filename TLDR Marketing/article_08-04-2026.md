NEWS & TRENDS

 HOW META'S AI PUSH IS CHANGING AD CREATION (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Meta is pushing toward fully automated advertising, with a reported
goal of letting businesses simply input a credit card and a goal by
the end of 2026. In practice, media buyers say the platform is far
from ready. The new Andromeda ad retrieval system incentivizes
uploading diverse creative to broad audiences rather than targeting
fixed segments, which has dramatically increased production demands.
Advantage+ campaigns now account for 60-70% of spending at some
agencies, but recurring issues with low-quality placements and
overspend in weak demographics mean significant human oversight is
still necessary. Most large brands remain unwilling to use Meta's
generative AI for ad creative due to legal concerns and brand control.


 GEN Z-ERS ARE BECOMING BOSSES (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Gen Z is reshaping workplace norms. They reject the old model of
loyalty and delayed rewards. Raised with constant access to
information, they want clear reasoning behind decisions and faster
trust. This clashes with older leadership styles built on hierarchy
and slow-earned credibility. Companies that flatten structures and
involve younger employees will retain talent.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 WIN REPORT: ONE WORD, 38% MORE BOOKINGS (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Adding one word to a call-to-action drove large gains because it
closed the intention-action gap. Changing a search button from
“Search” to “Search Now” increased bookings by 38% on a travel
marketplace. Many visitors were browsing and delaying action due to
choice paralysis and present bias. The word “Now” worked because
searching is low risk and requires no commitment. It also added
urgency and gave a clear instruction, which reduced hesitation and
pushed users to act immediately. 

 THE POWER OF A FLYWHEEL (1 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Growth accelerates when channels reinforce each other instead of
operating alone. LinkedIn drives visibility and starts conversations,
while YouTube builds trust and captures high-intent search. The
newsletter deepens relationships and converts more effectively, since
it reaches an owned audience. Content is repurposed across all three
so each idea compounds and stays active longer. This creates a
flywheel where every piece of content feeds the next, driving
consistent leads without ads or outreach.

 THE CLAUDE DTC SECOND BRAIN (6 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 This guide shows how to turn scattered business data into a
self-improving AI knowledge base using Claude. Instead of manually
organizing files, users dump raw inputs like ads, reviews, and notes
into folders. Then the AI compiles them into a structured,
cross-linked wiki that answers strategic questions using only that
data. The system continuously improves by saving outputs and
re-ingesting them, creating a feedback loop where insights compound
over time. It demonstrates a simple but powerful shift from fragmented
tools to a single AI-maintained “brand brain” that makes strategy
grounded in real data. 

 GOOGLE QUIETLY LAUNCHED AN AI DICTATION APP THAT WORKS OFFLINE (2
MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Google AI Edge Eloquent is a free iOS dictation app that turns speech
into polished text using on-device AI. It transcribes in real time,
removes filler words, and can rewrite output into summaries or more
formal versions. Users can choose local-only or cloud processing, add
custom terms, and search past transcripts while tracking stats like
word count and speaking speed. It may expand to Android in the near
future.

 A MASSIVE RANT ABOUT USING LLMS FOR MARKETING TASKS (9 MINUTE READ)
[13] 

 This article catalogues specific examples of LLMs failing at tasks
they are commonly promoted for, including company research, data
analysis, transcription, and file exports. One model fabricated 30
businesses during a prospecting task, then claimed it had verified
them via live web searches it never performed. Another task involving
Search Console data produced invented page names and inconsistent
metrics despite having the actual export attached. The real danger is
not just the occasional errors but confident, repeated fabrication
paired with corporate decisions to replace junior marketers with AI
tools. 

 WHY SAAS GOT PRICED OUT (5 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Public SaaS valuations collapsed despite continued growth, which
shows that the market no longer believes in the traditional model.
Median SaaS multiples fell from 18.6× revenue in 2021 to 5.1× in
2025, even as HubSpot grew revenue 141% while its stock fell 71%. The
core issue is the input model, since software built on manual data
entry is being replaced by AI that understands unstructured input like
voice and context. Speed gains from AI are quickly commoditized, while
quality problems like model collapse and error scaling make judgment
more valuable than output. Future products will win by integrating
into AI-driven workflows and improving quality. Users now expect tools
to work with how they already use AI rather than forcing new systems. 

 COULD GAMING'S 'EPIC' STRUGGLES OPEN THE DOOR FOR MORE ADVERTISERS?
(4 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Epic Games' recent layoff of a thousand employees and Fortnite's
declining audience highlight a wider downturn in gaming, but industry
insiders say advertising alone would not have solved Epic's core
challenges around engagement and mobile access. Large publishers
remain reluctant to adopt scalable ad formats, often stuck in a cycle
of licensing brand IP and then awkwardly asking those same brands to
buy ad placements. Roblox is the furthest along in testing in-game
ads, but revenue from advertising is still modest, and many of its
game developers do not see the value in running them.
