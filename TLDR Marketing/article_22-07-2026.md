NEWS & TRENDS

 AI CONTENT IS EVERYWHERE ON SOCIAL MEDIA, ESPECIALLY LINKEDIN (4
MINUTE READ) [7]

 AI-generated content is spreading across social media, with longform
posts hit the hardest. Pangram data from over 1M posts shows 13.8%
flagged as AI, rising to 25% for posts over 250 words. LinkedIn drives
nearly two-thirds of flagged content, with over 40% of longform posts
fully AI-generated. X shows the highest combined AI use when including
mixed content. Reddit replies remain mostly human, but top-level posts
show higher AI rates, revealing gaps in moderation.

 OPENAI APPEARS TO BE MISSING ITS SALES GOALS BY A VAST MARGIN (2
MINUTE READ) [8]

 OpenAI's $100 billion ad-revenue target for 2030 requires three
things to happen at once. Advertisers moving decades of search and
social budgets into chatbots, OpenAI out-competing Google and Meta for
that spend, and the entire AI ad market growing roughly 100 times from
where it sits today. The category's entire addressable market
currently tops out around $5.4B, and OpenAI itself is on pace to miss
its own ad revenue forecast by about 90% this year.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 COPYWRITING BREAKDOWN: HOW TO SELL SOMETHING ORDINARY (4 MINUTE READ)
[9]

 Strong marketing often comes from better research instead of better
copy. Claude Hopkins won customers in a crowded category by asking
hundreds of consumers what they wanted, measuring how Palmolive
performed against those needs, and turning the results into specific
proof points. The ad replaced vague claims with numbers such as 250
times more lather, 15% water absorption in one minute, and lather that
lasted 10 minutes. Research, measurable evidence, and clear messaging
can make an ordinary product stand out even when competitors offer
similar features.

 DON'T BLOCK PERSONAL EMAILS (1 MINUTE READ) [10]

 Blocking personal emails in B2B forms can hurt conversion rates.
Instead, detect domains like Gmail or iCloud and require a company URL
field. With name and company, you can still find or enrich work
emails. This keeps friction low while preserving lead quality. Many
high-intent leads still submit personal emails, often due to autofill
behavior.

 SWAG THAT WORKS: THE NEW RULES FOR TRADE SHOW GIVEAWAYS (5 MINUTE
READ) [11]

 47% of marketers already treat branded merchandise as a core
marketing channel, and 87% of recipients keep and use what they
receive rather than tossing it. Middesk built a tiered swag strategy
around that shift at Money20/20, gating higher-value items behind
booth actions like booking a sales meeting or agreeing to a case
study, then generated 10 times its activation spend in new pipeline
and 26 times more in influenced pipeline. The gifting also produced a
surprise: a steep jump in LinkedIn followers driven entirely by pins
and luggage tags that were never meant to be the campaign's main goal.

 HOW MUCH CREATIVE DEBT ARE YOU CARRYING? (6 MINUTE READ) [15]

 Creative work needs recurring time on the calendar or it will always
lose to urgent tasks. "Creative debt" is the cost of repeatedly
delaying creative projects until avoiding them becomes part of your
identity instead of a temporary choice. Treat creative projects like
recurring commitments by scheduling dedicated time, reduce project
scope, and keep momentum through small, consistent actions instead of
waiting for the perfect moment. You should also remove pressure to
prove ROI so creative work can exist without immediate business value.
The goal is to stop delaying meaningful projects before postponement
blocks future creative output.

 WHY YOUR TOP KEYWORDS ARE QUIETLY LOSING VOLUME (11 MINUTE READ) [16]


 Head term queries are what fund Google's ad business, since two-word
category terms draw 5x to 15x the CPC of longtail equivalents, and
those same head terms are now shrinking in 7 of 9 consumer categories.
Entertainment's head term volume fell 16% year over year, the sharpest
decline in the dataset, in a category where AI tools already give
users a credible substitute for a Google search entirely. The bigger
risk for Google isn't cannibalized clicks on searches that still
happen, it's users skipping the search box altogether for tools like
ChatGPT on exactly the head term queries the ad auction depends on.

 HOW I (ACCIDENTALLY?) WENT FROM LIFECYCLE MARKETER TO PRODUCT MANAGER
(5 MINUTE READ) [17]

 A lifecycle marketer transitioned into a product manager role through
embedded product work, not a planned switch. Leading iterative in-app
projects and using AI to prototype solutions shifted ownership from
execution to problem definition. Internal support helped formalize the
move. The role change requires giving up solo execution and focusing
on defining problems and aligning teams. Lifecycle skills translate
well, especially prioritization and building user experiences.
