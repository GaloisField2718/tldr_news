 HOW AI-GENERATED CONTENT PERFORMS IN GOOGLE SEARCH: A 16-MONTH
EXPERIMENT (6 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 AI content can get indexed and gain early traffic fast, but it'll
fail to sustain the rankings without SEO fundamentals. In this
experiment, 71% of pages were indexed in 36 days and generated
122,000+ impressions, then grew to 526,000+ impressions by month 3,
but only 3% of pages remained in the top 100 after that point. Around
70% of total traffic came in the first 2.5 months, then growth slowed,
and visibility dropped sharply due to weak authority, lack of unique
insights, and missing trust signals. Adding new AI content later
increased impressions by up to 17x to 19x for older pages, but gains
were short-term and did not fix the long-term ranking issues. 

 UX WORKS THROUGH SOCIAL RELATIONSHIPS. AI TOOLS ARE ERASING THEM (4
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI is eroding the social relationships that make design valuable,
which leaves designers stuck cleaning up stakeholder-generated output
instead of shaping strategy. Design has always balanced complexity and
regularization, but AI pushes teams toward over-reliance on templates
and patterns, which reduces original thinking and turns design into
repetitive output. This shift replaces collective вЂњmeaning-makingвЂќ
with isolated production, which increases noise and slows work. The
core problem is not tooling but the breakdown of relationships, since
real value comes from sharing ideas, getting feedback, and discovering
what is wrong early. Improving AI skills does not fix this issue
because it strengthens a system that avoids listening, which leads to
generic solutions and weaker outcomes.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 THE EXACT TITLE TAG FORMULAS BEHIND TOP-RANKING PAGES (2 MINUTE READ)
[7] 

 Title tags now impact visibility across search, AI tools, and link
previews, not just Google. Unclear or overly creative titles reduce
clicks and limit how content is surfaced by AI. Clear, intent-driven
titles perform best. Focus on one keyword, stay under 60 characters,
and match the page's H1. 

 STACKED (VS SINGLE) DISCOUNTS WORK BETTER (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Stacked discounts drive higher purchase intent and engagement than a
single discount of the same total value. Splitting a 25% discount into
multiple parts increased shopping intent by up to 15.8% and drove
66.3% more views, 52.7% more upvotes, and 52.3% more comments across
9,000+ deals. People see stacked offers as 33.1% more unique and feel
20.7% smarter when using them. The effect is stronger when the total
discount is smaller and weaker when stacking feels complex or requires
too much effort. Clear and simple stacking improves results and makes
customers more likely to act. 

 SURREAL: A MASTERCLASS IN UNHINGED MARKETING (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Surreal broke into the saturated cereal market by positioning itself
between sugary kids' cereals and bland health options, offering
high-protein, low-sugar products wrapped in chaotic humor. The brand
built a cult following on LinkedIn by targeting marketers as an
amplification channel, knowing they'd share clever campaigns
organically. Its paid media leans on fake celebrity billboards, absurd
brand collaborations like "Dild-Os" with Lovehoney, and anti-marketing
aesthetics that interrupt the patterns consumers expect from
advertising.

 YOUR WRITER DOESN'T WANT TO BE YOUR CONTENT STRATEGIST (4 MINUTE
READ) [12] 

 Content strategy often fails when companies ask writers to own it.
Writing and strategy require different skills. Writers focus on
execution, while strategy defines audience and ties content to
revenue. Confusing job titles lead to underqualified hires. Teams
often expect one person to do everything, which creates output without
direction. Companies should separate roles. Hire a strategist to build
the foundation, then bring in marketers or managers to execute. 

 CHATGPT ADS PILOT LEAVES ADVERTISERS WITHOUT PROOF OF ROI (2 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Early advertisers testing ChatGPT ads say the product isn't ready for
performance marketing. There is no programmatic buying, minimal
targeting, and almost no data to evaluate outcomes. Two agency
executives confirmed they couldn't demonstrate measurable business
results from their spend. OpenAI plans to roll out ads to all free and
low-cost US users soon, but without proper measurement tools, the
channel remains a blind bet for now.
