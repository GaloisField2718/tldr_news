NEWS & TRENDS

 BOT TRAFFIC NOW EXCEEDS TRAFFIC FROM HUMAN USERS (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Bot traffic has surpassed human traffic online for the first time. It
accounts for about 57% of web page requests on Cloudflare's network.
Agent and agentic browser traffic is up nearly 8,000% in 2025.
Automated traffic is growing about 8X faster than human traffic. AI
agents are now a primary audience layer. Measurement now requires
citation rates, AI share of voice, and referral traffic from AI
platforms. 

 THERE'S A BIG DIFFERENCE BETWEEN USING AI AND BEING AI-FIRST (6
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Marketing teams are becoming leaner, more generalist, and more like
creative studios than production pipelines. AI handles execution while
humans focus on strategy, narrative, and taste. Teams are moving
beyond using AI to speed up workflows toward rethinking systems.
Differentiation comes from doing less and prioritizing curated signals
over volume. AI search is reshaping discovery through structured data,
proof, and cross-channel presence. 

 THE PRINCIPLES OF BUYABILITY: WHY STRONG DEALS STALL AND WHAT
SEPARATES THE VENDORS WHO GET CHOSEN (3 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 B2B deals are more likely to close when the purchase feels safe and
defensible, not just superior. About 40% of deals stall because buying
groups cannot agree, and 81% go to vendors already familiar to most
stakeholders. Finance, legal, and procurement teams control roughly
50% of decision influence, making cross-functional trust critical.
Buyers are 3X more likely to choose a heavily recommended vendor than
one with a better product or lower price.

 I BEGGED THIS EMAIL EXPERT FOR HIS SECRETS (59 MINUTE VIDEO) [11] 

 Daniel Bustamante explains how a dormant 50,000-person email list
generated $150K in a 7-day launch using a simple three-email weekly
system. The cadence includes a Monday curation email, a Wednesday deep
dive, and a Friday story or case study to build trust. He covers how

balancing nurture with sales in welcome flows. He also shows how AI
tools like Claude help streamline launch campaigns. 

 INSIDE THE LINKEDIN CONTENT SYSTEM I BUILT IN A WEEKEND (7 MINUTE
READ) [12] 

 A creator built a local LinkedIn content system in a weekend that
replaces multiple SaaS tools and cuts daily workflow from 3 hours to
about 20 minutes. The tool uses engagement data to surface trending
topics, track content pillars, suggest reposts, and identify audience
signals for outreach. It also generates adjacent content angles from
past high performers and ensures balanced coverage across themes. By
replacing feed scrolling with signal-based inputs, it streamlines
ideation and distribution.

 IF I WERE STARTING FROM ZERO TODAY, I'D BUILD MY AI BRAIN ON GITHUB
FIRST (7 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 A GitHub repository acts as a living brain, using markdown files to
store priorities and decision logs. A background file holds key
context while a journal file forces regular prioritization. A Claude
Code routine runs in the cloud each night, scanning Slack, email,
calendar, and meeting transcripts. It then drafts replies, updates
tasks, surfaces summaries, and writes changes back to GitHub. Notion
is used for drafting and editing, Slack is used for notifications
through a custom bot, and the whole setup runs continuously without
manual prompts.

 FEEDBACK LOOPS REQUIRE REAL FEEDBACK. AI DRIVES IT UNDERGROUND (4
MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Feedback loops rely on real critique and experimentation. These
systems break when feedback is unstructured or detached from
decisions. AI increases noise by producing abundant low-value feedback
at scale. Teams also move away from direct cross-functional
conversations. As speed becomes the priority, learning slows and
signals get suppressed. Without strong feedback systems, companies
drift and corrections become slower and more expensive. 

 WHY TIKTOK IS EXPANDING ITS PREMIUM ADS PUSH AND WHAT THAT MEANS FOR
YOU (5 MINUTE READ) [19] 

 TikTok's 2026 NewFronts framed the platform as a sequential
storytelling environment that mirrors television with new ad formats.
Prime Time lets brands serve up to three ads within a 15-minute
high-engagement window, while TopReach bundles the first app-open and
in-feed placement to capture attention during cultural moments. With
only 26% of marketers using TikTok campaigns, the platform argues for
greater budget reallocation from linear and streaming TV. 

 ACKNOWLEDGEMENT IS THE CX STRATEGY MOST ORGANIZATIONS ARE OVERLOOKING
(6 MINUTE READ) [20] 

 Negativity bias makes difficult customer experiences more memorable
than positive ones. Unacknowledged friction therefore shapes long-term
brand perception. Acknowledging complexity, even without fixing it,
reduces resistance and builds psychological safety. Validation changes
how friction is remembered by the customer. Embedding this across
onboarding, digital flows, and automated communications turns
acknowledgement into a journey-wide loyalty strategy rather than a
support tactic.
