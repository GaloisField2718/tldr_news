NEWS & TRENDS

 WHEN ALGORITHMS CURATE CULTURE, WHAT DO WE LOSE? (5 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 As AI curates what we watch, listen to, and read, pop culture will
become predictable. Algorithms favor familiarity, ignoring the context
and creative accidents that drive breakthroughs. Iconic moments in
music, film, and fashion come from risk and experimentation, which AI
cannot replicate. Human curation preserves history, connecting art and
innovation across generations. AI cannot replace the insight and
storytelling that keep culture rich and transformative. 

 HOW BRANDS CAN BE OLD AND NEW SIMULTANEOUSLY (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 KFC successfully uses The Colonel to connect traditional values with
modern relevance, while Cracker Barrel misstepped by removing its Old
Timer mascot. Research from KFC revealed that customers viewed its
longevity and consistency as proof of credibility and quality. The
Colonel's image communicates brand identity instantly, similar to
symbols like the Nike Swoosh or Golden Arches. Brands do not need to
abandon history to stay current, they need to show how established
values fit into today's lifestyles.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 THE SURVEY EMAIL PLAYBOOK: HOW TO ACTUALLY GET PEOPLE TO TELL YOU
WHAT THEY THINK (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Survey emails are powerful for understanding customers, but they must
be simple and valuable to get responses. Effective tactics include
stating how long the survey takes, explaining why feedback matters,
embedding questions in the email, and offering small incentives.
Personalization, such as using a real sender or asking targeted
questions in welcome and post-purchase emails, increases completion.
Interactive formats like ratings or checkboxes reduce friction.
Collected data should feed directly into audience segmentation and
automation to tailor future messaging. 

 MAKE YOUR BRAND FAMOUS (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 To build brand recognition, appear where the target audience already
spends time and use existing platforms instead of focusing only on
owned channels. Survey trends, popular content, and creators to find
culturally relevant ideas. Create content that entertains or educates
rather than feeling like a sales pitch. Schedule time to explore
magazines and social feeds for inspiration and adapt insights to the
brand story. Invest in collaborations, podcasts, or co-branded content
to reach audiences where they already pay attention.

 FIXING THE DISCONNECT BETWEEN MARKETING AND REVENUE (5 MINUTE READ)
[14] 

 Many companies do plenty of marketing but fail to drive revenue
because their efforts aren't tied to real business outcomes. Teams
chase vanity metrics like clicks and impressions instead of focusing
on pipeline and sales. Misalignment between marketing, sales, and
finance creates confusion, while short-term thinking prevents
learning. Real progress comes when marketing is accountable for
revenue, shares goals with sales, and functions as core business
infrastructure that turns activity into measurable growth.
