 WE'VE LOST PREVIEW TEXT FOR EMAIL. ARE SUBJECT LINES NEXT? (7 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 Apple, Google, and Yahoo are replacing subject lines and preview text
with AI-generated summaries, deal cards, and groupings that override
brand messaging. These features often misstate discounts, dates, or
expirations, eroding trust and damaging customer relationships while
removing opportunities for brands to convey personality. Since neither
users nor brands can reliably opt out, the changes risk financial
loss, legal disputes, and frustration across industries.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 IS RELATIONSHIP MARKETING THE NEW MOAT? (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 With AI tools making automation and personalization widely available,
the real differentiator is how brands invest in human connection.
Relationship marketing tactics such as handwritten notes, personalized
thank you videos, and participation in virtual events help build trust
and reciprocity. Combining these with AI-driven analytics allows
marketers to tailor follow-ups that mention previous conversations and
deliver curated recommendations. These moments of authenticity promote
retention and create a durable moat in a commoditized landscape. 

 5 STEPS TO BUILDING A CONTENT CLUSTER FROM EXISTING CONTENT (2 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Start by identifying 2-5 core parent terms that are high-volume,
competitive, and tied to conversion. Audit your content so each URL
has one clear target keyword, even if it's not the one you rank for
yet. Group related pieces around the parent terms using your ICP
knowledge, then validate with SEO tools. Link each page to its parent
within the first 100 words and to sibling pages to share authority.
Continuously refine clusters as search intent and priorities shift.

 IDEOGRAM (TOOL) [10]

renderings. The freemium model allows users to generate up to 25 sets
of 4 images per day.

 WHY THE BEST PRODUCTS TELL MOST PEOPLE TO F*** OFF (4 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Products that polarize users often perform better than those that try
to appeal to everyone, as shown by Elden Ring, Linear, Owner, and
Superposition, which each deliberately restrict features to serve
their core audience. A dedicated 5% of users who love a product is
more valuable than 50% who find it acceptable. Founders and marketers
can employ this strategy by defining trade-offs, making products act
as filters, using complaints as marketing, measuring depth of loyalty
rather than width of reach, and doubling down on what makes a product
unique. 

 DO YOU NEED A GTM ENGINEER? (5 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 GTM engineers have been (over)hyped as the hottest job of 2025, but
hiring is far from widespread. Job postings remain scarce compared to
RevOps or SalesOps roles, with many companies instead outsourcing GTM
engineering work to agencies or consultants. The role blends system
design, automation, and growth experimentation, but sourcing top
talent is difficult and often limited to ex-founders or internal
transfers. As AI tools become more accessible, companies are training
existing teams to handle automation rather than relying on unicorn
hires, suggesting GTM engineering may ultimately fold into RevOps and
RevTech. 

 WILL THE ANTI-MONOPOLY RULING CRACK OPEN GOOGLE'S DATA? (7 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Google's antitrust penalties stop short of breaking up its empire but
still chip away at its dominance. The company must end exclusive
contracts, share portions of its search index and user-interaction
data with qualified competitors, and provide syndicated access to
search results across verticals. However, it avoided harsher remedies
such as divesting Chrome or Android, reinstating Exact Match in ads,
or opening up Knowledge Graph data. This article details the
implications of each of these decisions in the ruling.
