NEWS & TRENDS

 GARTNER IS TELLING CMOS TO DOUBLE THEIR PR BUDGETS BY 2027 (2 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 AI search engines cite non-paid sources far more than ads. About 94%
of AI citations come from non-paid sources and 82% from earned media,
with journalism accounting for 20-30%. Gartner predicts PR and earned
media budgets will double by 2027 as LLMs replace traditional search
discovery. Fresh content matters as over half of citations come from
material published in the last 12 months, peaking within the first
week. Brands with strong editorial presence and third-party
credibility gain the most visibility. 

 A $1,000 DOG GROOMING SESSION? THE PET WELLNESS INDUSTRY IS BOOMING
(7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Pet grooming is now treated as a wellness practice. Owners are
spending $500 - $11,000 per pet yearly on specialized trims,
treatments, and custom meals. Rising costs are also leading some to
travel for cheaper services. Pets are seen as family, and care focuses
on prevention and routine. The industry is booming alongside human
wellness trends and growing recognition of pets as sources of
emotional support.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 TIME-SENSITIVE OFFERS IN GOOGLE ADS (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Time-sensitive promotions in Google Ads need specific tactics because
the platform decides which ad assets appear. Countdown customizers
show the remaining time until an offer ends and create urgency. The
countdown can display days and switch to hours and minutes on the
final day. Promotion assets add a 25 character line that highlights a
discount and can include start and end dates. Callout and sitelink
assets can also highlight offers, though too many assets can reduce
how often the promotion message appears. 

 AUTOMATING LIFECYCLE EMAIL QA WITH CLAUDE (1 MINUTE READ) [9]

 REDDIT'S IMPACT ON B2B SEARCH (18 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Reddit now outranks many B2B SaaS vendors in Google results across
major keyword categories. It beats competing vendors on 50-66% of
shared keywords in three of four SaaS verticals and captures about
950K+ monthly searches before any vendor appears. Most wins come from
generic category searches, which drive 77% of its search volume.
Reddit also dominates longer queries, winning 73-100% of searches with
six or more words. Just five subreddits generate 3,709 keyword
rankings and over 1.1M monthly searches.

MISCELLANEOUS

 ALWAYS WINNING: WHY COMPETITION IS ABOUT ENDURING BRAND RELEVANCE (4
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Brands often react to market shifts by updating their identity or
messaging, which can weaken recognition. Burberry illustrates this
risk. Years of reinvention diluted its core identity. Uniqlo shows the
opposite approach by evolving around a clear idea of LifeWear while
maintaining a consistent role in the market. Leaders often default to
visible change during uncertainty because it signals action. The
strongest brands stay competitive by holding firm to a clear core and
allowing thoughtful evolution around it. 

 LINKEDIN ADS HAVE A REPUTATION PROBLEM (3 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Most lead generation campaigns fail because they treat LinkedIn like
search. Buyers on LinkedIn are not actively evaluating vendors. They
are scrolling through industry content and orienting themselves around
problems, which makes demo-first offers a poor match for their
mindset. Assets like industry benchmarks and teardown style analyses
consistently outperform direct demo requests with cold audiences. Demo
offers only perform well after prospects have already interacted with
the brand.
