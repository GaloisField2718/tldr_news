NEWS & TRENDS

 WHY LOCAL MEDIA ADVERTISING GOT HARDER TO SELL (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Local media advertising is harder to sell because the mix of local
businesses has shifted. New advertisers favor targeted digital tactics
over traditional media. The market added 1.7M net new businesses after
the pandemic, and many of these buy search ads and targeted banners
that sales reps often miss. There are now 3X more in-house marketers
than ad sales reps, and these marketers focus on quick results and
bottom-funnel tools rather than brand building. This makes advertisers
more skeptical and more focused on proof over reach. 

 YOUR 2026 MARKETING BUDGET HAS A HOLE IN IT — AND IT'S CALLED
TOKENS (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI token costs are a fast-growing gap in 2026 marketing budgets
because usage scales quickly and is hard to predict. Traditional B2B
budget splits of about 45% headcount, 45% programs, and 10% technology
are shifting as tech spend rises by 2% to 3% due to AI tools and data
needs. Headcount is flat at many SaaS companies, but expectations are
higher. AI-native companies are hiring as revenue grows faster than
staff. Program spend is steady but faces pressure to lower
cost-per-outcome with the help of AI. The biggest challenge is
forecasting token usage for both internal AI work and vendor pricing,
since even providers lack clear benchmarks.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 EMAIL ANIMATED GIFS: BEST PRACTICES FOR DRIVING ROI WHILE AVOIDING
ANNOYING SUBSCRIBERS (5 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Brands using purposeful animated GIFs in email see a 37:1 ROI, a 105%
improvement over those relying on static images alone. Interactive
elements like gamified reveals and video-GIFs drive 73% higher
click-to-open rates by creating a curiosity gap that pulls subscribers
into engagement. However, 31% of subscribers label animated emails as
annoying when the motion feels random, so every animation needs to
serve a clear function. Gmail clips emails over 102KB, and GIFs should
stay under 500KB to render properly on mobile. 

 AUGUST PERIOD CARE'S VIRAL COMMUNITY-FIRST MARKETING STRATEGY (4
MINUTE READ) [10] 

 August built rapid growth by openly normalizing periods and using a

 WHY AI SUCKS AT FRONT END (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 AI tools excel at scaffolding generic UI components, migrating design
tokens, and outlining features. However, they fall apart when
front-end work requires pixel precision or bespoke interactions. The
core issue is that LLMs trained on common web patterns cannot see
rendered output, struggle with math-heavy layout calculations, and
have no control over the chaotic browser environment where HTML and
CSS actually execute. Scroll-driven animations and complex component
states are particularly weak spots. The more complex a component
becomes, the slower and less reliable AI assistance gets.

 COPYWRITING FRIDAY: ONE OF THE FEW THINGS ON THE SPACE SHUTTLE THAT
DIDN'T HAVE A BACKUP SYSTEM (3 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Nikon's ad proves reliability by placing its camera in a high-stakes
context instead of making generic claims. Using NASA as an authority
signal, it shows the camera was trusted on a Space Shuttle without a
backup, signaling extreme dependability. Specific details like 1971
and the admission of modifications add credibility. The ad then
connects this extreme use case to everyday users, implying the camera
will handle normal situations with ease. It focuses on customer
outcomes, making the message feel like a logical conclusion rather
than a sales claim. 

 AEO IS NOT SEO 2.0 (5 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 Treating AEO as an upgrade to SEO misunderstands how LLMs work. There
are no keywords to rank for and no page one. Responses are
personalized based on prompts, history, preferences, and platform.
Visibility depends on brand authority and trust signals, not technical
optimization alone, though crawlability and structured data still
matter for ingestion. The takeaway is to measure brand recognition and
third-party mentions, not prompt visibility like a ranking report.
