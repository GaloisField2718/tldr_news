 WHY YOUR LATEST ROMANCE FIND MIGHT COME FROM A BRAND (4 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Romance-driven marketing is gaining momentum as consumers seek more
uplifting content amid a heavier news cycle. Campaigns range from
Audible's immersive fan events to Neutrogena's influencer partnerships
and Doritos' humor-infused romance parodies. The approach allows
brands to test creative boundaries while connecting with consumers
looking for entertainment and positivity.

 Across the US, UK, and Australia, average recognition of brand

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 A HACK TO GET 5% MORE LEADS (1 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Verify emails before form submission to prevent silent lead loss.
Most missed leads come not from bounces but from mistyped addresses.
This wastes ad spend, disrupts onboarding, creates support issues, and
blocks re-engagement. One enterprise client discovered 5% of signups
had typos, resulting in thousands of lost contacts and millions in
potential LTV. Real-time verification keeps your funnel clean and
conversion-ready. 

 DON'T CALL YOUR BLOG A BLOG (1 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Avoid calling your blog a blog, as the term now suggests low-value
content. Instead, frame your content hub with names like Content Hub,
Research Center, Insights, or Idea Hub, depending on your business.
Audiences are less interested in generic listicles and more drawn to
unique data and insights relevant to their field.

 HOW ANTHROPIC TEAMS USE CLAUDE CODE (31 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Page 15 of Anthropic's internal guide explains how its team uses
Claude Code for growth marketing. They generate hundreds of new Google
Ads variations using specialized sub-agents, produce up to 100 social
ad creatives at once via a Figma plugin, and analyze Meta Ads campaign
performance directly within Claude Desktop. A memory system tracks
previous tests and hypotheses, enabling self-improving ad
experimentation that would be impossible to manage manually. 

 THE STATE OF MARKETING DATA QUALITY 2025 (11 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 CMOs estimate that 45% of the data their teams use is incomplete,
inaccurate, or outdated. Poor-quality data costs organizations an
average of $12.9M annually. 30% of CMOs view improving data quality as
the biggest lever for marketing performance, ahead of automating data
workflows and improving data democratization. The most common data
quality problems are completeness and consistency.
