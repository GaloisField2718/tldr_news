NEWS & TRENDS

 IT IS TRIVIALLY EASY TO USE REDDIT TO MANIPULATE AI SEARCH (7 MINUTE
READ) [4] 

 AI search and deep research tools can be influenced by extremely
small pieces of UGC on sites like Reddit, Wikipedia, and Quora. As
little as a 13-word snippet can shift AI outputs and cause models to
repeat or cite spam-like claims. These systems rely heavily on
user-generated sources for retrieval, which makes them vulnerable to
targeted manipulation through lightly edited or promotional posts.
Well-placed comments can also distort what AI tools surface as
answers. 

 NEW RESEARCH SHOWS HONESTY ABOUT AI USE AT WORK IS BACKFIRING (4
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 AI adoption at work is near universal, with 94% of knowledge workers
using it and most openly discussing it with managers and peers. New
research shows disclosure carries a cost even when outputs are
identical. In a controlled experiment, participants rated AI users as
10X lazier and were 24 percentage points less likely to recommend them
for high-visibility work when told AI was used. Framing AI as helping
the team improves ratings but still ranks below workers who do not
mention AI.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 TOPICS MATTER FOR THIRD-PARTY AUTHORITY SIGNALS (6 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI pulls from a different set of trusted sources for every topic,
making broad off-site authority building inefficient. Instead of
spreading mentions across generic high-authority sites, brands need
depth within the specific sources models already trust in their niche.
AI reuses existing trust from those entities, meaning citations depend
more on topical reputation and credible third-party mentions than
overall backlink volume. Concentrated placements in a few top-tier,
in-topic sources drive more visibility than scattered coverage. 

 WE ANALYZED 137K SITES: 97% OF LLMS.TXT FILES NEVER GET READ (12
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 97% of llms.txt files got zero readers in May, despite growing
adoption driven by speculation about AI benefits. Of the limited
traffic, 77% came from non-AI bots, with most AI access coming from
coding agents like Claude Code, not search tools. Perplexity and
ChatGPT together made up just 1.1% of requests, and no AI system
actively looked for the file when it wasn't present.

 QUANTIFYING YOUTUBE KEYWORD OPPORTUNITIES (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 YouTube keyword research uses different metrics than traditional SEO
since most views come from discovery, not search. Instead of search
volume, use Median Monthly View Velocity (MMVV) to estimate demand
across top videos. Competition is measured by median subscribers of
ranking channels, while median video age shows how long content stays
relevant. Keywords with strong view velocity, lower subscriber
competition, and longer lifespan signal more potential. 

 HOW G2 REVIEWS ARE BUILDING THE TRUST LAYER OF AI SEARCH IN B2B (6
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 ChatGPT and Perplexity are increasingly citing G2 to recommend
software, even over traditional search results. G2 jumped to ~23,700
citations in Perplexity and 12,000 in ChatGPT as 71% of B2B buyers now
use AI for research. Models rely on fresh reviews, ratings, and
comparisons to justify answers. This makes recent, high-quality
customer feedback more influential than search rankings. 

 ELECTRONIC ARTS LAUNCHES EA ADVERTISING (2 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 EA Advertising is a new unit that lets brands appear directly inside
gameplay through real-time placements like stadium signage,
scoreboards, overlays, and in-game challenges. The system runs across
EA's 120M+ monthly active users, with EA SPORTS FC alone generating
over 1B matches per month. A new ad server enables privacy-safe
targeting, alongside partnerships for live events and community
activations.

 YOU WILL BE REPLACED BY A 19-YEAR-OLD (4 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI isn't replacing marketers, it's flattening skill gaps so
experience matters less. What took a decade to learn is now cheap and
accessible, making younger creators instantly competitive. Their edge
isn't tools, it's instinct and audience fluency from growing up
online. They take more risks and ship without overthinking.
Experienced marketers get slowed down by caution. The takeaway is to
unlearn that hesitation and prioritize hiring for hunger and native
internet instincts over experience.
