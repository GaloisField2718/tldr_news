 OPENAI HAS TRIPLED THEIR CRAWL OF THE WEB: AN ANALYSIS OF 7B+ LOG
FILES (6 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 OpenAI is scaling crawl activity, so brands need to treat AI bots as
a discovery channel alongside search. Total crawl volume has tripled
since August 2025, while direct user-triggered visits dropped 28%.
This signals greater reliance on cached indexes and automated
retrieval. Post GPT-5, OpenAI has leaned more on search than training,
making real-time visibility more important than static training data
inclusion. Crawl behavior varies by industry, requiring
vertical-specific optimization. Google still dominates at 18.2B
monthly crawl events versus OpenAI's 887M, but OpenAI has grown from
1.38% to 4% of Google's crawl share in a year. 

 AI'S IMPACT ON EARLY-CAREER MARKETERS IS REACHING A CRISIS POINT (5
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Entry-level marketing roles focused on execution tasks like

handle much of this work. Employment among workers aged 22-25 has
fallen 16% relative. The broader job market is also cooling, with only
181K jobs added in 2025. Companies are hiring more cautiously and rely
on AI to increase productivity instead of expanding teams.

 HOW MUSIC MARKETING WORKS (8 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Modern music marketing is heavily engineered, with teams running fan
pages, seeding content, and using algorithm tactics to simulate
organic buzz. TikTok and Meta now control distribution more than
traditional media, amplifying only select artists. Labels use
streaming and engagement data to spot talent, so existing momentum is
required before signing. Awareness can be manufactured through these
systems, but fandom still depends on an authentic connection with
audiences.
