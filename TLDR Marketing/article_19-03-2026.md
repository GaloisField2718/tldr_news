 THE BRAND TAX: HOW GOOGLE PROFITS FROM DEMAND YOU ALREADY OWN (5
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Paid search performance looks strong because branded search takes
credit for demand that already exists. An analysis of 99B sessions
finds that ad costs rose 30%, cost-per-visit rose 9.4%, and conversion
rates fell 5.1%, while paid search bounce rates hit 59% and paid
social reached 65%. Branded campaigns claim 1,299% ROAS versus 68% for
non-branded, yet branded keywords rarely drive new revenue. Most
conversions come from repeat visitors, while paid channels bring low
quality traffic that leaves quickly. As search shifts beyond Google
and AI answers reduce clicks, investing in brand visibility and AI SEO
can lower wasted spend and improve overall returns. 

 PINTEREST SPRING TREND REPORT 2026: PERSONALIZATION IS IN BLOOM (5
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Pinterest trends show a shift from perfection to feel-good living.
People are embracing self-expression at home with bold colors and
small upgrades. Food trends favor comfort and creativity over dieting.
Social moments are more casual with low-effort gatherings. Activities
are more local with small outdoor spaces and simple escapes. Cleaning
and organizing are now framed as self-care. Overall, the focus is on
improving daily life, not reinventing it.

 YOUTUBE SOCIAL LISTENING: HOW TO FIND ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS IN A
VIDEO-FIRST WORLD (8 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 YouTube social listening requires a different approach than
text-based platforms because API restrictions limit comment data
collection at scale. Instead of chasing volume, marketers can focus on
comments across their own videos, competitor channels, and creator
content to surface product feedback, content requests, and sentiment
shifts. A structured strategy involves categorizing comments into
themes like pain points, purchase-intent signals, and competitor
comparisons, then translating those patterns into content and
positioning decisions. 

 WHY GMAIL IS CLIPPING YOUR EMAILS — AND WHAT TO DO ABOUT IT (2
MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Gmail automatically hides any part of an email that exceeds 102 KB,
showing a "Message clipped" link that most readers never click. This
quietly damages open rates because the tracking pixel typically sits
at the bottom of the email and gets cut along with the hidden content.
Common culprits include uncompressed images, heavy inline CSS, and
long newsletters with multiple formatted sections.

 ‘AI-DESIGNED' HURTS SALES (4 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Research shows that labeling products as “AI-designed” can hurt
sales, with purchase intent dropping by up to 29% compared to
human-designed items and people perceiving 75% less human involvement.
This happens because consumers struggle to connect with products that
lack a human creator, especially when identity plays a role in the
purchase. However, framing AI as a tool used by humans improves
outcomes, with human-AI collaboration increasing purchase intent by
3.5% over fully human designs and 12.8% over AI-only ones. 

 WHY CMOS AND ICS SEE AI SO DIFFERENTLY (4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 While 83% of marketers say leadership is committed to AI, 62% of CMOs
report being able to prove ROI compared to just 12% of ICs. Leaders
focus on efficiency and outcomes, while ICs deal with workflow
challenges, governance delays, and untracked effort. This divide is
driven by gaps in visibility and ownership, which leads leaders to
push scale while teams grow cautious. 

 AMAZON EXPECTED TO MOVE PRIME DAY SALE TO JUNE (2 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Amazon is expected to move Prime Day from July to late June, shifting
the sales surge into the second quarter and changing revenue timing.
The timing is critical for third-party sellers and competing brands.
With Amazon accounting for about 40% of US ecommerce spend, the timing
shift could impact the broader retail calendar, especially around
back-to-school and holiday planning.
