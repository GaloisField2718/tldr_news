NEWS & TRENDS

 WHY THE BEST MARKETING TODAY IS BUILT TO BE FORWARDED (3 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Content today is built around a simple design question: what would
make someone want to share this with their network? Emotional
specificity drives performance, with high arousal content generating
34% more shares than passive content. Discovery is also shifting, with
nearly 1 in 3 consumers now starting searches on TikTok, Instagram, or
YouTube instead of Google, rising to over 50% for Gen Z. Content that
delivers a single clear insight and fits shrinking attention spans is
more likely to circulate than sit idle. 

 THE APP STORE IS BOOMING AGAIN, AND AI MAY BE WHY (3 MINUTE READ) [6]


 Contrary to predictions that AI would render apps obsolete, new app
launches jumped 60% globally in Q1 2026 and are up 104% in April alone
compared to 2025. AI coding tools appear to be the primary driver,
enabling creators without technical backgrounds to build and ship
mobile software for the first time. The categories gaining the most
ground are productivity, utilities, and lifestyle, pointing to a more
utility-driven app economy.

 HOW TO OPTIMIZE YOUR PRICING PAGE FOR AGENTS (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 SaaS companies are optimizing pricing pages for AI agents as well as
humans. Buffer added a machine-readable /pricing.md file that
structures pricing and packaging in markdown. This allows AI tools to
parse pricing directly instead of scraping pages or relying on
outdated data. It builds on the llms.txt movement and treats pricing
as structured data. The approach enables fast comparison across tools
and reduces ambiguity in pricing pages.

 LINKEDIN'S ALGORITHM KILLED COMPANY PAGES. EMPLOYEES ARE THE
DISTRIBUTION NOW (3 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 LinkedIn has shifted distribution from company pages to employees,
making personal profiles the main driver of B2B reach. Its 360Brew
algorithm prioritizes semantic signals of expertise over engagement,
favoring content that shows real professional insight. Only about 3%
of employees post, but they generate roughly 30% of brand engagement.
This aligns with buyer behavior, where 64% of hidden decision makers
prefer thought leadership content over marketing materials, and with
LinkedIn becoming a major source for AI search systems.

 HOW TO TRANSITION FROM LINK BUILDING TO DIGITAL PR (11 MINUTE READ)
[12] 

 A top ranking page can have hundreds of links but only a few high
quality ones. Strong campaigns often send 20-50% fewer pitches while
still earning 3-10 pieces of coverage. It relies on targeted outreach,
clear story driven pitches, and timely ideas tied to news or trends,
since journalists prioritize relevance over link requests. Results
typically appear within 3-6 months, with faster impact from high
authority coverage and improved indexing. Pricing is shifting toward
retainers and data driven work. Metrics should expand beyond backlinks
to include nofollow links and mentions, as about 75% of clients now
value AI visibility. 

 THE CONTENT CULTURES THAT LAST HAVE ONE THING IN COMMON (4 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Most content programs stall around the 18-month mark. 22% of B2B
marketers rate their content marketing as very or extremely
successful, while 42% cite a lack of clear goals as the root cause of
underperformance. The difference between programs that fade and those
that endure comes down to culture, built on a documented editorial
mission, cross-functional ownership across product and sales teams,
and sustainable workflows that prevent burnout.
