HEADLINES & LAUNCHES

 CLAUDE OPUS 4.7 (8 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Anthropic has released Claude Opus 4.7, offering improved performance
on difficult engineering tasks, stronger vision capabilities, and more
reliable long-running task execution compared to its predecessor. 

 OPENAI'S GPT ROSALIND (8 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 OpenAI introduced GPT‑Rosalind, a specialized model designed to
support drug discovery and biological research through improved
reasoning, tool use, and integration with scientific data sources. 

 CODEX EXPANDS INTO FULL COMPUTER AUTOMATION (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 OpenAI updated Codex with background computer control, multi-agent
workflows, and deeper integration with developer tools, extending its
role across the full software development lifecycle. 

 THE COMPUTER IS PERSONAL (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Perplexity launched "Personal Computer," an AI platform that shifts
the operating system model from manual instruction execution to
probabilistic goal completion. The agent utilizes deep web research as
its core foundation to autonomously evaluate reasoning paths and drive
multi-step workflows. This architecture transforms the computer into
an active orchestrator, eliminating the administrative friction of
managing fragmented software tools.

DEEP DIVES & ANALYSIS

 JENSEN HUANG ON ANTHROPIC, OPENAI, CHINA, AND DEMAND FOR INFERENCE
TOKENS (7 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Jensen Huang recently appeared on an interview where he discussed
mostly familiar topics. However, there were three exchanges where
Jensen said more than he probably intended. Jensen even lost his
composure when discussing China and whether sales of chips should be
restricted in the region. This post details each of the exchanges and
notes other observations about the interview. 

 WHAT I LEARNED THIS WEEK (20 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 This post contains rough notes on pretraining parallelisms, whether
distillation can be stopped, Mythos and the cybersecurity equilibrium,
Pipeline RL, and why pretraining runs fail. 

 THE PR YOU WOULD HAVE OPENED YOURSELF (12 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 A new Skill and Test Harness help port transformer models to mlx-lm,
streamlining contributions and reviews. This tool assists contributors
by managing model conversion tasks and provides reviewers with
agent-assisted PRs complete with comprehensive reports. It aims to
maintain code quality and improve efficiency in an open-source
environment where manual review cannot scale.

 INTRODUCING TERNARY BONSAI: TOP INTELLIGENCE AT 1.58 BITS (4 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 Ternary Bonsai is a 1.58-bit language model family offering improved
performance with minimal memory usage. It outperforms 1-bit
counterparts, scoring 75.5 on average benchmarks and achieving 3-4x
better energy efficiency. The models, available in 8B, 4B, and 1.7B
versions, provide flexible deployment across devices like Macs and
iPhones under the Apache 2.0 License. 

 SANDBOXED AGENTS FOR CODEBASE MIGRATION (19 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 This guide outlines a structured approach to modernizing large
codebases using sandboxed agents that operate on isolated tasks,
validate changes, and return auditable patches while orchestration
remains outside the execution environment.

MISCELLANEOUS

 ANTHROPIC EXEC LEAVES FIGMA BOARD AMID PRODUCT TENSIONS (2 MINUTE
READ) [16] 

 Anthropic's chief product officer stepped down from Figma's board
following reports that upcoming models may include design tools
competing with Figma's core product. 

 OPENAI TO SPEND MORE THAN $20 BILLION ON CEREBRAS CHIPS, RECEIVE
STAKE, THE INFORMATION REPORTS (2 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 OpenAI will pay Cerebras more than $20 billion over the next three
years to use servers powered by Cerebras' chips. The deal may also
give OpenAI an equity stake in Cerebras. OpenAI has also agreed to
provide Cerebras with about $1 billion to help fund the development of
data centers. Cerebras is targeting an IPO in the second quarter of
this year.
