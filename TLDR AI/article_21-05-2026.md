HEADLINES & LAUNCHES

 ANTHROPIC TO PAY SPACEX NEARLY $45 BILLION FOR COMPUTING DEAL (2
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Anthropic has agreed to pay SpaceX nearly $45 billion over the next
three years for compute resources. It will pay $1.25 billion per month
until May 2029, with either party being able to end the agreement with
90 days' notice. The companies had earlier this month inked a deal
that gave Anthropic 300 megawatts of computing capacity from a large
SpaceX data center in Memphis known as Colossus 1. This partnership
has expanded to include capacity at a second SpaceX data center. 

 OPENAI REPORTEDLY MOVES TOWARD IPO (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 OpenAI is reportedly preparing for a potential IPO as early as
September following the dismissal of Elon Musk's lawsuit challenging
the company's structure. 

 STABLE AUDIO 3.0 (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Stability AI released a new family of Stable Audio 3.0 models,
including open-weight versions capable of generating music and sound
effects up to more than six minutes long.

DEEP DIVES & ANALYSIS

 AI SOLVES A LONGSTANDING GEOMETRY CONJECTURE (14 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 An OpenAI reasoning model autonomously disproved a major conjecture
tied to the planar unit distance problem, an open question in
combinatorial geometry that dates back to 1946. The proof introduced
techniques from algebraic number theory and was independently verified
by external mathematicians, marking one of the first cases where an AI
system resolved a prominent unsolved mathematics problem. 

 ON BUILDING AGENTS FROM FIRST PRINCIPLES (15 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Mishra strips away the TRL, Unsloth, and PRIME-RL framework
abstractions to show that every agent-training system reduces to the
same loop: prompt to model action to environment to reward to gradient
update. He builds a toy tldraw-style text-to-diagram agent in pure
Python where the model emits JSON create_shape and connect actions
against a validating canvas, then layers a reward function combining
JSON validity, schema compliance, layout quality, and semantic
coverage of prompt keywords. 

 A BITTER LESSON FOR DATA FILTERING (1 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 New scaling studies reveal that using no data filtering may be
optimal for large model pretraining in high compute, data-scarce
settings. Large parameter models not only tolerate but benefit from
including low-quality and distractor data. Contrary to prevailing
beliefs, filtering out low-quality data may not be necessary with
ample compute resources.

 INTRODUCING AGENT EXECUTOR, GOOGLE'S DISTRIBUTED AGENT RUNTIME (6
MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Google Agent Executor is an open-source runtime standard for reliable
and efficient execution of long-running agent workflows. Key features
like durable execution, secure isolation, and connection recovery
enhance workflow management, while session consistency and trajectory
branching support distributed agent environments. It also collaborates
with Kubernetes Engine on Agent Substrate to optimize compute
efficiency for massive agent deployments.

 MIND-BLOWING GROWTH IS ABOUT TO PROPEL ANTHROPIC INTO ITS FIRST
PROFITABLE QUARTER (5 MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Anthropic's revenue is set to more than double in the second quarter
to $10.9 billion. The projections, disclosed to Anthropic's investors
as part of an ongoing funding round, show how the company's sales have
exploded since the start of the year. Its quarterly revenue is now
growing faster than Google's and Facebook's in the run-up to their
initial public offerings. The company might not remain profitable for
the full year as it plans to increase spending due to its vast need
for compute. 

 CHEAP AI COULD DERAIL OPENAI AND ANTHROPIC'S IPOS (7 MINUTE READ)
[19] 

 OpenAI and Anthropic's expected IPOs face challenges due to
decreasing AI costs and increased competition. American and Chinese
labs are producing cheaper and efficient AI models, threatening their
market share and pricing power. Enterprises are increasingly adopting
cost-saving measures like "advisor models" and seeking affordable
alternatives, complicating the valuation for anticipated IPOs.
