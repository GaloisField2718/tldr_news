 GOOGLE PLANS TO RELEASE GEMINI 3 PRO PREVIEW IN NOVEMBER (1 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 Google's Gemini 3 Pro preview model has appeared in VertexAI code,
labeled '11-2025', suggesting a November launch. The timeline matches
prior testing activity observed in AI Studio. Google has not issued
any official statements about the launch. 

 OPENAI ISN'T YET WORKING TOWARD AN IPO, CFO SAYS (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 OpenAI's Chief Financial Officer, Sarah Friar, said the company is
not planning to IPO in the short term. It will instead prioritize
growth and R&D over profitability. OpenAI is hoping the government
will support its offers to increase data center capacity by helping to
guarantee the financing for the chips behind its deals. The
depreciation rates of AI chips are uncertain, making it more expensive
for companies to raise the debt needed to buy them. Friar claims
OpenAI could reach profitability on very healthy gross margins if it
weren't seeking to invest so aggressively. 

 GOOGLE GEMINI'S DEEP RESEARCH CAN LOOK INTO YOUR EMAILS, DRIVE, AND
CHATS (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Google Gemini's "Deep Research" now uses Gmail, Drive, and Chat data
for AI-generated reports. The feature can create detailed research
plans and reports, integrating personal workspace data with web
searches. Currently available on desktop, Google plans to roll out
mobile access soon.

 ENABLING TRILLION-PARAMETER MODELS ON AWS EFA (12 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Perplexity published code enabling trillion-parameter
mixture-of-experts models to run efficiently across multiple AWS
servers for the first time, opening up less advanced data centers to
AI inference. The challenge is that large models, even many open
weights models, are too large to fit on single servers, but AWS'
networking hardware was too slow for the constant inter-server
communication these models require.
