 IMPROVING DEEP AGENTS WITH HARNESS ENGINEERING (8 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 LangChain's coding agent went from Top 30 to Top 5 on Terminal Bench
2.0 with just a harness change. This post discusses the team's
approach to harness engineering. The goal of a harness is to mold the
intelligence of a model for tasks you care about. Self-verification
and tracing help a lot. 

 PROMPT CACHING 201 (10 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 OpenAI published a detailed guide explaining how prompt caching
reuses repeated prompt prefixes to skip prefill compute, reducing
latency and input token costs. It outlines cache mechanics, routing
considerations, KV reuse, and practical strategies for increasing
cache hit rates in production workloads.

 INTRODUCING EVMBENCH (6 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 EVMbench is a benchmark that evaluates the ability of AI agents to
detect, patch, and exploit high-severity smart contract
vulnerabilities. Smart contracts routinely secure hundreds of billions
of dollars in crypto assets. As AI agents improve, it becomes
increasingly important to measure their capabilities in economically
meaningful environments. This also means using AI systems to
defensively audit and strengthen deployed contracts. 

 GLM-5 TECHNICAL REPORT (42 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 GLM-5 technical report outlines the details of the model development,
combining DSA-based efficiency improvements with asynchronous
reinforcement learning to decouple generation from training. 

 GOOGLE PROPOSES RANDOM MASKING FOR LLM OPTIMIZATION (19 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 Google researchers showed that randomly masking parameter updates can
outperform dense adaptive optimizers, with a masked RMSProp variant
exceeding recent state-of-the-art methods. Their proposed Magma
optimizer uses momentum-aligned gradient masking to improve
pre-training efficiency.
