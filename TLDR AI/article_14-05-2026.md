 NOTABLE RESEARCHERS JOIN $4 BILLION EFFORT TO BUILD SELF-IMPROVING AI
(5 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Recursive Superintelligence has raised more than $650 million at a
valuation of more than $4 billion to build AI that can improve itself
with little or no help from human developers. Its seven co-founders
include notable researchers from many of the industry's leading AI
companies. Many of these researchers specialize in AI systems that can
run for long periods in pursuit of goals. While the researchers are
bullish on the idea of AIs recursively improving themselves, the
current technology is a long way from the point where humans can be
removed from the loop.

 Cerebras raised $5.5 billion in its US IPO at a market valuation of

drew orders for more than 20 times the number of shares available. The

 ANTHROPIC BEATS OPENAI ON BUSINESS ADOPTION (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Anthropic has passed OpenAI in business adoption. More businesses
used Anthropic than OpenAI in April. Anthropic has quadrupled business

only 0.3%. The pace of development in the AI industry is overriding
the typical forces of vendor stickiness.

 THE ECONOMICS OF SUPERSTAR AI RESEARCHERS (12 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Superstar researchers at frontier labs can earn over a hundred times
more than the average AI postdoc. Researcher quantity doesn't easily
make up for quality in the field of AI. Even a 2x researcher can earn
far more than the median because their contributions easily scale to
billions of users. If they can add something that multiple 1x
researchers can't, then it's worth paying a lot to capture it.

 CLINE RELEASES OPEN-SOURCE AGENT RUNTIME SDK FOR CODING AGENTS (3

products.

 MICROSOFT'S MULTI-AGENT AI SYSTEM TOPS ANTHROPIC'S MYTHOS ON
CYBERSECURITY BENCHMARK (3 MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Microsoft's MDASH AI system uses more than 100 specialized AI agents
to work together across multiple AI models to find real-world software
vulnerabilities. A set of agents scans code for potential
vulnerabilities, and then a separate group of agents debates whether
each finding is real and exploitable. A final stage constructs
proof-of-concept attacks to confirm the bugs exist. MDASH surpassed
Anthropic's Mythos model on the CyberGym benchmark, a test that
measures how well AI systems can reproduce real-world vulnerabilities.
