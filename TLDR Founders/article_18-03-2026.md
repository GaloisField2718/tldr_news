HEADLINES & TRENDS

 THE MARGINAL COST OF ENTREPRENEURSHIP IS TRENDING TOWARDS ZERO (5
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Shopify is increasingly becoming the core infrastructure for modern
commerce. The platform dramatically reduces the barriers to starting a
business by unifying payments, logistics, and software. The company is
embedding AI directly into its entrepreneur stack to lower the time
and labor required to build and run a company. Shopify is making
entrepreneurship increasingly frictionless, and the data reflects it. 

 HOW MUCH OF THE SOFTWARE SLOWDOWN IS JUST BUDGETS FLOWING TO
ANTHROPIC AND OPENAI? MAYBE AS MUCH AS 70% (14 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Almost every major public software company has printed lower growth
rates every single year since the 2021 peak. The conventional
explanation is that vibe coding is eating software, but what's
actually happening is simpler and more damaging: companies have finite
budgets, and they're pouring an unprecedented share of those budgets
into AI infrastructure and foundation model providers. The software
budget pool is now shared with a new category that went from zero to
over $40 billion in two years. This structural rebalancing will take
several years to sort itself out, and it is going to hurt a lot of
software companies before it does. 

 WHY THE WORLD STILL RUNS ON SAP (7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Upgrading from SAP ECC to S4HANA costs $700M, takes 3 years, and
requires a team of 50 from Accenture. Lidl scrapped its transition
after blowing $500M. Workers toggle between apps ~1,200 times per day,
and 47% can't find the information they need. Startups like Axiamatic,
Conduct, and Tessera are wrapping legacy systems in AI instead of
replacing them, targeting the $380B system integration market.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 WHY FOUNDERS CAN'T DELEGATE STORYTELLING (2 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Storytelling is strategy, and the founder is the only person who
holds the full context: why the company started, which experiments
failed, what those failures taught, and where the company is heading.
When storytelling gets delegated, marketing, sales, and product each
fill in the gaps with their best guesses, creating subtle misalignment
that compounds across every function until customers are closed on the
wrong story and the product builds toward the wrong vision. 

 THE MOST UNDERRATED HIRE RIGHT NOW IS A GREAT PRODUCT PERSON (2
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 When building was hard, engineering was the bottleneck, and the
status hierarchy reflected that. Now that building is easy, the
variance in outcomes has shifted almost entirely to judgment on what
to build, how to sequence it, and how to talk about it. The rarest
version of this hire sits at the intersection of culture and deep
technology, genuinely bilingual between what is technically possible
and which cultural currents are real versus ephemeral. 

 SHOW CUSTOMERS THEIR WINS (5 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Retention is half a perception problem - customers don't churn
because they stopped getting value, they churn because they stopped
noticing it. The fix starts on day one by cementing their "before"
state so every improvement has a visible benchmark. Rilla went from $0
to $70M ARR in 7 years as the 7th fastest-growing SaaS company, built
entirely on consumer tactics like streaks and goals that most B2B
companies ignore.

MISCELLANEOUS

 SPENDING OTHER PEOPLE'S TIME (7 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Asking for someone's time is virtually free for the asker, yet
enormously expensive for the person being asked. The trade is
asymmetric, with the askee having to rebuild whatever mental state
they were in before the interruption, while the asker just gets on
with their day. The problem compounds with AI as people are
effectively shifting their 'productivity' onto other people's
shoulders. Organizations need to make these costs visible and put
friction in the right places so that the default path protects
attention instead of consuming it. 

 THE COLLAPSE OF TERMINAL VALUE - WHAT HAPPENS IF AI MAKES EVERY MOAT
TEMPORARY? (6 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 $58 trillion in S&P 500 market cap repriced at 5x free cash flow
instead of today's 20x means a 75% drawdown - roughly $44 trillion
evaporating. That's the math if AI makes every competitive moat
temporary instead of durable. Newspapers went from 12-15x EBITDA to
2-4x in a decade, retail followed, and taxi medallions went to zero.
The $300-500B/year in AI capex only pencils out if returns are
durable, but AI itself is the thing making returns short-lived.
