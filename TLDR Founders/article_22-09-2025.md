 NAVAN FILES TO IPO (5 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Navan is heading to the public markets with $688M ARR, 30% growth,
and gross margins up to 73% after major AI-driven efficiency gains.
Still, net revenue retention barely clears 110%, free cash flow

 Robots still struggle with tasks like picking up objects due to the

breakthroughs akin to a "ChatGPT moment" in robotics requires blending
advanced AI models with traditional engineering. The future of
robotics depends on combining simulation data with real-world
applications to overcome current limitations.

 HOW TO GET CITED IN AI ANSWERS (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Did you know AI models cite brands mentioned at industry conferences?
This SEO expert discovered it after ChatGPT kept recommending his
competitors despite his better domain authority and more backlinks.
Turns out LLMs scan transcribed conference talks, YouTube
presentations, and LinkedIn recaps - places his competitors were
getting namedropped while he bought Reddit mentions. The models also
track user corrections. A pizza shop vanished from AI suggestions
after enough people typed "don't show me that again" in follow-up
prompts. His competitors weren't trying to game AI at all. They were
presenting case studies at conferences, getting discussed in customer
Slack channels, and showing up in YouTube tutorials. These mentions
trained the AI to trust them.

 HOW TO FIND THE PERFECT NAME (9 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 David Placek and his team at Lexicon Branding have named nearly 4,000
brands, including Swiffer, Sonos, and Pentium, by using a structured
naming process. In today's competitive and AI-driven market, an
effective name can create trust, communicate originality, and be
globally accessible. The Lexicon Framework advises generating
thousands of names, considering sound symbolism, and emphasizing trust
and uniqueness over descriptiveness.

 THE SELF IS THE PLATFORM (5 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 AI has inverted the fundamental equation of technology - individuals
can now rival entire teams, and private tools are beating public
platforms. One person with ChatGPT and Cursor can ship what used to
require 50 people. The last tech boom was about coordinating people at
scale, but AI flipped it - personal leverage beats social leverage
now. These aren't social networks, they're supertools that multiply
individual capacity without needing anyone else. The individual is now
the base unit of scale.
