HEADLINES & TRENDS

 THE AGE OF VERTICAL MODELS IS HERE (6 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Intercom's Fin agent is objectively the highest performing, fastest,
and cheapest model for customer service. The model resolves almost two
million issues per week, a number that is growing exponentially. It
resolves customer issues at a higher rate than any other model
available. The model has already grown to nearly $100 million in
recurring revenue in its short life. 

 AI COMPANIES ARE BUILDING FOR THE WRONG USERS (9 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 The gap between the early adoption and maturation phases is closing
fast. The early adopters of AI have gotten the industry to where it is
now. The next phase of growth will come from users who are curious but
skeptical, willing to try but not interested enough to figure out how
to work around the rough edges of the technology. This audience will
determine whether AI delivers on its productivity promises.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 AI FEATURES ARE ERODING YOUR SUBSCRIPTION APP'S MARGINS — HERE'S
HOW TO FIX IT (13 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 AI features introduce variable costs that scale with user engagement.
This disrupts the near-zero marginal soft model of traditional
subscription apps. The most successful businesses are designing their
entire systems around the economics of usage. Teams need to manage AI
the same way they manage acquisition spend or infrastructure. Consider
whether the AI feature is worth the cost - the improvements from
implementing AI need to be measurable. 

 THE IMAGINATION CHASM: WHY 99% OF USEFUL AGENTS HAVEN'T BEEN BUILT
YET (10 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Most industries still can't define what an "agent" actually is. You
can't build what you can't picture. This post lays out the six things
still missing before agent-to-agent economies work: a communication
layer (MCP/A2A), vendor-agnostic infra, dynamic auth, a payments
layer, a marketplace, and real benchmarks. OpenClaw expanded
imagination, but it's still the first 1%. 

 AVOIDING THE EYE OF SAURON (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Founders in AI face heightened competition from incumbents, startups,
foundation models, and empowered customers, risking obsolescence if
their products remain at the workflow level. Companies building
horizontal applications for high-agency, high-capability customers are
particularly vulnerable, as these customers can easily replicate such
tools. Founders must prioritize deep domain mastery, addressing
human-centric problems, and developing chassis products to maintain a
competitive edge.

MISCELLANEOUS

 SAAS WAS ABOUT UNBUNDLING. AI IS ABOUT REBUNDLING (4 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 The SaaS playbook was simple - own one workflow, expand from there.
AI companies are doing the opposite - they're expanding into adjacent
categories almost immediately because the cost of building is near
zero. Models change every 42 days now, and buyers would rather trust
one platform for 3-5 years than stitch together a best-of-breed stack
that's outdated by next quarter. 

 2026 STATE OF GTM: THE BENCHMARKS THAT ACTUALLY MATTER (7 MINUTE
READ) [16] 

 Companies under $100M ARR are accelerating again, likely driven by
AI-native companies growing abnormally fast. CAC payback period is the
metric to watch - revenue durability is uncertain, and shorter
contract lengths are becoming the norm. GTM efficiency on AI products
with 30% gross margins looks very different from that of traditional
software with 75% margins.
