 ASSESSING A STARTUP'S DEFENSIBILITY (6 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Assessing a startup's defensibility focuses on evaluating its
economic moat, product-market fit, and competitive advantage. Key
factors include cost advantages, intangible assets, network effects,
switching costs, and efficient scale for economic moat; strong
retention rates, NPS, and usage metrics for product-market fit; and
intellectual property, team capability, and scalable business models
for competitive advantage. Investors use these criteria to determine
if a startup can sustain market share and scale effectively against
competition.

 THE DISCOMFORT OF FOCUS (5 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 What if focus isn't about discipline but about trust, as in trusting
that saying no won't close doors, it'll open the right ones faster.
Most founders chase breadth to feel safe, but this team learned real
momentum came from committing to one painfully specific ICP. The
paradox was that narrowing created freedom. Once they stopped hedging
and chose, everything else (clarity, conviction, growth) finally
followed. 

 THE EPIDEMIC OF WASTED TALENT (5 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 Most jobs don't need you, they just need someone. Alex McCann argues
that high salaries often mask a deeper cost: companies pay you to
ignore your own potential. He contrasts corporate comfort with a
Colombian birdwatcher who found purpose in work only he could do. The
point isn't to quit tomorrow, but to start creating something that
couldn't exist without you. Being replaceable pays well. Being
irreplaceable matters more.
