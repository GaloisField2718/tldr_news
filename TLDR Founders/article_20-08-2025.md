 DON'T SHIP YOUR PITCH DECK TO YOUR WEBSITE. HERE'S HOW TO TRANSLATE
YOUR STORY FOR CUSTOMERS (14 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Founders often mistakenly use their investor pitch deck for customer
marketing, ignoring that investors and customers have different
priorities. Effective early B2B marketing requires tailored messaging
that focuses on a specific audience's immediate needs, not the broad
vision in a pitch deck. Translate pitch deck messages into clear
customer-focused website content by addressing specific pain points,
differentiating the product, and building trust with social proof.
Ensure communication is relevant, precise, and customer-centric.

 THE CASE FOR STAYING PUT (5 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 After three years in one apartment, this author discovered what
digital nomads miss: real connection only comes from dealing with a
place's boring parts. He spent years hopping between cities, thinking
each Instagram-worthy location would make him happy. However, constant
movement meant never belonging anywhere. The case for staying put is
simple - you can't build meaningful relationships or impact when
you're always leaving. The daily annoyances, the negotiations with
neighbors, the commitment to local problems - that's what creates
actual community. 

 RAG IS DEAD, CONTEXT ENGINEERING IS KING (8 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Chroma's founder says RAG is dead because it bundles three concepts
poorly - the real job is context engineering, figuring out what
belongs in the context window for each LLM generation. The best teams
now use a two-stage approach: first-stage retrieval (vector search,
text search, and metadata) culls 10,000 candidates down to 300, then
LLMs re-rank those to the final 20-30. This matters because context
rot is real - despite perfect needle-in-haystack marketing, models
degrade as you add tokens. The future looks radically different:
models will stay in latent space without converting back to text and
retrieve continuously during generation instead of once at the start.
What we call "memory" is just context engineering with better
branding.
