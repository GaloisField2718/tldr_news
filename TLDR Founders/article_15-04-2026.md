HEADLINES & TRENDS

 THE AI-PILLED COMPOUNDING STARTUP (4 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Flatiron spent a month visiting AI-native startups in SF, and the org
charts look nothing like a normal company. One PM covers five
companies. Engineers talk to customers and own product decisions.
Claude Code has replaced Cursor in most shops. Non-engineers shipped
production tools, an account manager built a Slack agent to automate
uploads, and accounting teams wrote database queries through MCP.
Everyone's iterating 3-5x faster, which sounds great until you realize
the real danger is saying yes to every feature request just because
you finally can. 

 MORE LIES OF STOCK-BASED COMPENSATION (5 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Stock comp looks the same on a GAAP line no matter what your share
price does. The dilution doesn't. When Amplitude traded at $83, a
$250K package cost 301K shares. At $5.90, that same package costs 4.2M
shares. Same expense, 14x the dilution. That math gets ugly fast as
software multiples keep compressing. 

 SURVIVING AI PRICE WARS WITHOUT DESTROYING YOUR BUSINESS (12 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 AI startups face pricing wars due to market saturation, leading to
destructive underpricing. Enterprises have significant AI budgets and
often use multiple tools, prioritizing value over cost. Successful
companies focus on premium perception, creative pricing models, and
strong customer integration to avoid competing solely on price.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HOW WE BUILD PRODUCT TEAMS AT OWNER (25 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Building a small, focused team requires discipline at every layer.
You need to have relentless standards in hiring and constantly work to
maintain alignment. The team needs to be genuinely obsessed with
customers and have zero tolerance for mediocrity in craft. When done
correctly, the team becomes a force that builds products that feel
different. 

 HOW TO BRAND YOUR COMPANY: THE COMPLETE PLAYBOOK (9 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Branding is a strategic system, not just design, built on
positioning, messaging, visual identity, and scalable assets. Strong
brands reduce sales friction, enable premium pricing, and compound
over time. Most companies fail by starting with visuals instead of
defining clear positioning and messaging first. 

 HOW CORGI HIT $40M ARR IN 6 MONTHS (6 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Corgi grew fast by fixing the root problem in insurance: slow
underwriting and complex workflows. Instead of acting as a broker, it
became the insurer and used AI to compress processes from weeks to
minutes. Growth came from strong product-market fit and a unique
strategy of building community (café, events, and media) instead of
relying on traditional marketing. 

 HOW WE BUILT A $7M FUNNEL WITH 4 LAYERS (5 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Most B2B companies have two funnel layers. Generate leads, close
them. Then they hit $1-3M and wonder why nothing's moving. ColdIQ
built two layers in between and bootstrapped to $7M ARR. One is
capture, mini-tools made with Claude Code that grab emails,
de-anonymization that identifies anonymous visitors, and video sales
letters that do the selling before the call. The other is management,
a 10K subscriber newsletter, behavior-triggered email sequences, and
150+ LinkedIn posts a month across 24 team accounts. Prospects who see
your content 3+ times before getting a cold email convert at 2-3x the
rate.

MISCELLANEOUS

 MOATS DON'T WORK WHEN BRIDGES ARE FREE (14 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 Software that only sells code will not survive the AI wave. There's
no moat when anyone can use AI to recreate the software. Only
companies with a two-sided market, a proprietary dataset, a hardware
component, or a messy real-world integration layer have a real moat.
The software industry is about to be re-sorted, and a lot of companies
that look like category leaders won't make it. 

 NUCLEUS NOUNS (2 MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Most apps revolve around one or two nouns, like 'Channel' and
'Message' in Slack and 'Project' and 'Issue' in Linear. These are
nucleus nouns, and every other noun is a 'satellite'. Thinking about
apps in terms of their nucleus nouns allows developers to cut to the
core of what an app is supposed to do. Customers feel it when a
company loses sight of its nucleus nouns. Companies with a firm
understanding of their nucleus nouns can use them as a weapon by
making them part of their brand. 

 YOUTUBE IS THE NEXT NEOBANK (5 MINUTE READ) [19] 

 The next wave of neobanks probably won't look like banks at all.
Every past winner found the spot where incumbents overcharged, SoFi on
income-based underwriting, and Nubank on no-fee cards in Brazil. Now,
stablecoins have made the deposit layer so cheap that accounts alone
don't get you anywhere. What matters is owning the income
relationship. YouTube has paid creators $100B+ since 2021 and just
turned on stablecoin payouts.
