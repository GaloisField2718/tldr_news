 PSIQUANTUM RAISES $1 BILLION TO BUILD MILLION-QUBIT SCALE,
FAULT-TOLERANT QUANTUM COMPUTERS (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 PsiQuantum secured $1 billion in Series E funding to develop
million-qubit fault-tolerant quantum computers. The funding will
enable the company to establish quantum computing sites in Brisbane
and Chicago and validate its systems architecture. New investors
include BlackRock, Temasek, and NVIDIA's venture arm, valuing
PsiQuantum at $7 billion.

also discusses the underestimated potential in AI startups and
highlights the importance of embracing challenges to avoid failure.

 WHY YOUR AI PRODUCT NEEDS A DIFFERENT DEVELOPMENT LIFECYCLE (22
MINUTE READ) [10] 

 AI products require a different development lifecycle compared to
traditional software due to their non-deterministic nature and the
balance between agency and control. The Continuous
Calibration/Continuous Development (CC/CD) framework helps address
these challenges by continuously refining system behavior and
gradually granting more autonomy. This approach ensures stability,
user trust, and the successful scaling of AI products.

 WHY MORE STARTUPS ARE BUYING OTHER STARTUPS IN 2025 (6 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 Startups are increasingly acquiring other startups due to challenging
fundraising environments and affordable valuations. In the first half
of 2025, M&A deals rose 18% year-on-year, driven by companies looking
to access new technologies or consolidate market positions. Notable
transactions include OpenAI's $6.5 billion acquisition of Io and
Ripple's $1.25 billion purchase of Hidden Road. 

 HOW TOP VCS ARE REALLY INVESTING IN AI APPLICATIONS (AND WHAT IT
MEANS FOR YOUR SAAS STRATEGY) (10 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Jacob Efron of Redpoint Ventures emphasizes that AI companies scale
faster than traditional SaaS due to plummeting model costs and advises
focusing on four AI use cases: chat, document processing, speech, and
coding. Redpoint's investment strategy evaluates AI by examining
market wedge potential, industry expansion capability, and market
quality demands. The rapid evolution of AI necessitates a swift
adaptation and integration into SaaS to maintain competitiveness, with
Redpoint highlighting marketing AI as a promising yet underdeveloped
area.
