HEADLINES & TRENDS

 LET IT CRASH: HOW TO STEER WHAT COMES AFTER (14 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 An AI crash could still leave behind power plants, data centers, open
models, and a workforce trained to work with machines. Overbuilding is
how new infrastructure usually gets installed. The crash only opens
the door - the rebuild depends on who already owns the compute,
models, and tools when prices reset. Spread the means, not the
proceeds. 

 ONTOLOGY EVERYWHERE! (13 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 An ontology is a formal, explicit specification of a shared
conceptualization. It is an agreement about what things exist in a
domain, how they relate, and what we call them. As AI agents become
the primary consumers of data, we need a layer that explicitly
describes what things mean, how they relate, and what rules govern
them. Structure knowledge and make it accessible, and the market will
sort out the labels.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HIRING WITH AI (3 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Recruiting feels perfectly aligned with AI capabilities because, at
its core, it's mostly a data problem. This article looks at the state
of the AI recruiting industry. Every AI recruiting company seems to
run into the same problems: recruiting isn't very frequent, causing
natural user churn; companies can use multiple platforms at once, so
there's no loyalty or lock-in; a data moat is hard; and the long-term
winner needs to become part of the broader recruiting window to scale.
The space is still early, and markets can be unlocked with new
capabilities. 

 WHAT IS TALENT AND TALENT ENGINEERING (20 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 'Talent' and 'Talent engineering' are two new roles that have
recently emerged. Frontier labs, VCs, and startups are hiring for both
roles. A 'talent' is a person who is great at attracting smart people
and has deep relationships with all of them. A 'talent engineer' is
someone who loves to build people tools and is also a great engineer.
The highest talent density companies now hire based on a blend of
talent and talent engineering rather than using the traditional model
of recruitment. 

 HOW TO KILL CHURN (10 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 You can't out-onboard a customer who only needed you once. 10%
monthly churn means rebuilding 70% of your user base every year. The
biggest lever is category choice, because a daily marketer workflow
retains nothing like a one-time birthday-card job. Blended churn hides
the split between sticky cohorts and tourists, so the work is steering
ICP, onboarding, and acquisition toward the people who come back.
Annual plans, dunning, and win-back flows help at the margin, but
choosing a repeat-usage customer is the whole game. 

 WHAT GAME ARE YOU PLAYING? (12 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 A lot of founders are describing their company with the wrong game
board. PLG, enterprise trust, marketplace liquidity, and liquidity
financing each have different rules, clocks, and ways to die. Name the
win condition first, then the feedback loops and constraints that
follow from it. Strategy gets sharp the moment you can name the game
everyone else is misplaying.

 X NOW OFFERS AN MCP SERVER TO MAKE ITS PLATFORM EASIER FOR AI TOOLS
TO USE (3 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 X has released a Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that lets AI
tools communicate with the X API. The new MCP doesn't add new
capabilities beyond what X's API already offers - it just makes them
easier to use for AI applications. The integration turns X into an
information network filled with real-time data to retrieve and
analyze. The MCP tool is not compatible with X's Write API endpoints,
so it can't be used to post autonomously. 

 INTRODUCING CLAUDE SONNET 5 (9 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Claude Sonnet 5 can make plans, use tools, and run autonomously for
long periods. Its performance is close to that of Opus 4.8 but at
lower prices. Safety assessments show that Sonnet 5 shows a lower rate
of undesirable behaviors than Sonnet 4.6 and is generally safer to use
in agentic contexts. It has a much lower ability to perform
cybersecurity tasks than current Opus models. Claude Sonnet 5 is now
available across all plans.

 THE PRIMITIVE IS THE PRODUCT (11 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 AI has created a new type of user that is rapidly consuming an
expanding share of software. Agents intuit and compose software
instead of navigating it. They care purely about capabilities and
whether they can be invoked and chained reliably. The best product for
an agent is just the best product for a developer - software companies
need to start thinking like developer tools companies. 

 THE WORLD AS MODEL (11 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 High-frequency trading firms succeeded by developing systems that
effectively extracted predictive signals from market data at
millisecond timescales. Companies are now applying similar approaches
to the physical world, creating digital representations of industrial
processes to optimize financing and trading of physical assets. The
focus is on developing the "reality stack" to turn physical signals
into actionable financial intelligence, reshaping markets and
investment strategies.
