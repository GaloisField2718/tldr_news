 THE STORY OF A CAP TABLE: KLARNA (3 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Sequoia Capital emerged as the big winner in Klarna's IPO, securing a
7x return on its investment. Klarna's journey from a loan provider to
a neobank involved major shifts in its cap table, including founder
exits, a boardroom battle, and an 85% down-round. Sequoia's strategic
moves, including early investment and avoiding 2021's inflated
fundraising rounds, were key to its success. 

 GROQ RAISES $750 MILLION AS INFERENCE DEMAND SURGES (2 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Groq has secured $750 million at a post-money valuation of $6.9
billion in a funding round led by Disruptive. The investment aims to
enhance Groq's global inference infrastructure, crucial for AI
deployment. Groq's technology supports over two million developers and
Fortune 500 companies with efficient compute solutions. 

 STUBHUB FALLS A BIT IN FIRST-DAY TRADING (2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 StubHub's shares fell 6% on its NYSE debut, closing at $22 after
pricing at $23.50. The IPO, which raised $800 million at an $8.5
billion valuation, marks a long-anticipated public entry for the
ticket marketplace, now owned by Viagogo and backed by key VCs holding
major stakes. Despite achieving $828 million in revenue in H1 2023,
StubHub reported a $76 million net loss, highlighting challenges amid
its recent market debut.

 WHY THE FUTURE OF SAAS IS EAT OR BE EATEN (4 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI disruption is forcing SaaS companies to innovate or risk
obsolescence. Traditional giants like Microsoft may pivot to
acquisitions, while smaller SaaS startups face pressure to quickly
secure large enterprise deals or risk being acquired. As AI redefines
industry workflows, companies in sectors like healthcare and finance
must harness data in transformative ways to stay competitive. 

 MY #1 TIP WHEN YOU ARE PITCHING VCS: JUST ASK THEM WHAT THE ODDS ARE
THEY'D INVEST (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Ask VCs about the odds of their investment decision at the end of
your pitch meeting to save time and avoid confusion. This direct
approach helps prevent feeling "ghosted" by providing clarity on their
interest level. Experienced VCs usually know their investment
likelihood by the end of the first meeting, so just ask to gain
immediate insight. 

 BUILDING MY FIRST AI PRODUCT: 6 LESSONS FROM MY 90-DAY DEEP DIVE (31
MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Developing an AI product revealed key lessons: identify suitable AI
problems, prototype with existing tools, and select the right
architecture without defaulting to chat interfaces. Address
inconsistencies across user inputs with evals, ensuring continuous
improvement through evaluations and experimentation. Ethical data
practices are crucial. Be transparent about data usage and consider
partnerships for compliance infrastructure.

 A PRIMER ON THE CREATOR CAPITAL MARKETS (4 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Creator financing went from fringe experiment to full capital market
in three years, with multiple paths beyond bootstrapping.
Revenue-based financing from companies like Spotter lets creators get
cash today for a percentage of future YouTube earnings - great when
income is steady, but risky when algorithms change. Some investors buy
into specific creator businesses like MrBeast's Feastables, while
others invest in everything a creator does by backing their parent
company. A creator who needs $50K for new equipment might take revenue
financing and pay it back from their next six months of ad revenue,
while someone launching multiple businesses might sell 20% of their
company to an investor who can help them grow faster. 

 VENTURE ATLANTA ANNOUNCES 2025 LINEUP OF BREAKTHROUGH TECH COMPANIES
(5 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Venture Atlanta 2025 will showcase 86 breakthrough tech companies,
drawing over 1,600 attendees and 450 investors to its event on October
15-16. The conference will introduce new features like Procurement
Palooza to enhance networking and access to procurement leaders.
Notable past successes include Flock Safety's $275M funding, which
demonstrates the conference's impact on the Southeast's tech economy.
