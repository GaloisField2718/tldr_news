 WHY AI CANNOT SIMULATE YOUR CUSTOMERS (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A new study claims AI can mimic customer purchase intent with 90
percent accuracy. Impressive on paper, but founders should be
cautious. Models can mirror how people talk about buying, not how they
actually buy. Real decisions happen inside context: risk, politics,
switching costs, and emotion. AI can help generate hypotheses, but
strategy still starts in messy, human conversations. You don't find
differentiators in data, you find them in people.

 WHAT EARLY STAGE FOUNDERS SHOULD KNOW ABOUT COMP (5 MINUTE READ) [14]


 Early-stage founders don't need Google-style compensation systems.
What you need is something simple enough to explain in five minutes
and fair enough that no one questions it. Start by defining your
philosophy: how much cash versus equity, how transparent you'll be,
and what principles guide every decision. This foundation matters
because you'll use it to justify every offer you make. From there, the
math is straightforward. Pay people fairly but not lavishly, since the
real upside is equity. Keep your first ten hires under 10% of the
total pool so you have room to grow, then calibrate by role: sales
earns on revenue, customer success on retention, product on outcomes.
When someone proves their impact, reward them immediately.
