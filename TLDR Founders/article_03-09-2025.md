 ANTHROPIC RAISES $13B SERIES F AT $183B POST-MONEY VALUATION (3
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Anthropic raised $13 billion in a Series F round, valuing the company
at $183 billion. Significant investors include ICONIQ, Fidelity, and
Lightspeed. This funding will support growing enterprise demand,
safety research, and international expansion. 

 VIJAYE RAJI TO BECOME CTO OF APPLICATIONS WITH ACQUISITION OF STATSIG
(3 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 OpenAI has acquired Statsig and named its founder, Vijaye Raji, as
CTO of Applications. Raji will oversee ChatGPT and Codex engineering.
The acquisition strengthens OpenAI's experimentation capabilities,
enhancing product development and responsiveness. Statsig will operate
independently in Seattle, with continuity for current customers. 

 WHEN SHOULD FOUNDERS SELL THEIR SHARES? (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Secondary sales hit $162 billion in 2024, up 45% from the previous
year, with founders increasingly cashing out small portions during
funding rounds - Revolut's CEO just sold $200-350 million while
companies like Stripe, Databricks, and OpenAI run regular employee
liquidity programs. The old taboo against founder secondaries has died
because IPOs now take 10+ years, and founders need basic financial
stability, but the key is moderation.

 FROM ZERO TO $25B IN 17 YEARS: THE INSIDE STORY OF HUBSPOT'S
DOMINATION STRATEGY (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 HubSpot's success stems from its focus on predictable, compounding
growth through inbound marketing and platform development. Expanding
from a marketing automation tool to a comprehensive system of record,
HubSpot increased customer retention and ARPU. Strategic acquisitions
and significant R&D investments further solidified its position,
creating sustainable competitive advantages and continual growth. 

 YOUR COMPLETE GUIDE TO REVENUE LEAKAGE (5 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Revenue leakage, the gap between potential and collected revenue,
often occurs due to system misalignments across sales, finance, and
customer service. Common causes include missed invoices, unintended
discounts, churned customers still receiving service, and untracked
credit memos. Identifying and addressing these leaks is crucial for
maintaining revenue integrity and ensuring organizational efficiency. 

 WHY TODAY'S BEST CFOS THINK LIKE FOUNDERS (3 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Modern CFOs act as business operators, focusing on turning capital
into outcomes rather than just financial reporting. They prioritize
strategic judgment, guide growth decisions, and align business metrics
with execution goals. A CFO who thinks like a founder can provide
clarity amidst startup chaos, enabling faster, more effective
decision-making.

 12 BOOKS FOR THE BUSY FOUNDER (28 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 This post recommends 12 essential books for startup founders tackling
initial challenges that emphasize customer development and achieving
product-market fit. They include "Running Lean" by Ash Maurya for Lean
Startup methodologies and "Effectual Entrepreneurship" by Saras
Sarasvathy on venture growth. It is important to focus on
discontinuous innovation, building relationships, understanding
customer perspectives, and iterating based on real offers to refine
products and business strategies. 

 BUILDING A 60% AI TEAM: EFFICIENT, PROFITABLE, AND SURPRISINGLY
LONELY (4 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 SaaStr has transitioned to a workforce comprising 60% AI,
significantly boosting efficiency and output at a reduced cost. Key
functions handled by AI have tripled content production, managed over
100,000 chat interactions, and built substantial sales pipelines. The
shift raises questions about maintaining company culture, as AI teams
bring productivity but lack the human energy and interaction that
traditional teams offer. 

 THE ONE NICHE PROGRAMMING SKILL THAT VCS ARE POURING MILLIONS INTO
(AND IT'S NOT AI) (5 MINUTE READ) [17] 

 VCs are heavily investing in formal verification, a niche programming
skill that uses mathematical models to ensure software reliability
beyond traditional testing. Companies like Certik and Quantstamp
leverage formal verification for blockchain security, while AWS
employs it for cloud infrastructure stability. This critical skill,
integral to high-stakes applications like AI safety, attracts
significant funding as it promises unbreakable software and a future
built on trust.
