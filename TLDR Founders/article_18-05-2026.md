HEADLINES & TRENDS

 PRODUCTS ARE OUT, BRAINS ARE IN (13 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Founders need to lead equally with the product and company
philosophy. They need to build a public track record of specific,
falsifiable claims about their domain and make their teams visible. A
lot of value is migrating now as things commoditize. Companies are now
competing on the quality of their predictions about their own
roadmaps. 

 THE REAL APP STORE OPPORTUNITY (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A skill is a markdown file Claude reads when a task calls for it.
Anyone in a company can write one in an afternoon. Inside companies,
people are writing them faster than they can think of new ones,
complaining "I could build ten times more of these, I just can't think
where to start." A public marketplace doesn't exist yet but it's
coming, with security review and governance built in. Twelve months
from now, "we have a skill for that" will be a buying question the way
"we integrate with X" was a decade ago.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HOW I BUILT AI AGENTS TO CLOSE THE BOOKS (7 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 This developer turned a two-hour-long task for their accounting team
into a five-minute-long task for an AI agent. This dramatically
impacted how their team operated as the same flow was used across many
similar processes. This article presents their six-step process for
building an accounting AI agent. By iterating and improving the agent,
the team eventually developed some major time savings. 

 GET TO THE CORE OF THE THING (3 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Your VPs are debating whether to add multi-region support or go
deeper in one industry. Forty minutes later they've quoted Andy Grove,
mentioned Stripe twice, and shipped nothing. The conversation that
would actually matter is which specific feature would have closed the
customer who ghosted you Friday. If your team can't name that feature
in one sentence, no framework or whiteboard session will conjure it. 

 AEO AND GEO FOR AI OVERVIEWS, CHATGPT, CLAUDE, GEMINI, AND PERPLEXITY
(13 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Search is now a lot different from just two years ago. Two new terms
have shown up to describe the work of being visible inside those
answers: Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), the work of being the
source an answer engine uses, and Generative Engine Optimization
(GEO), the work of appearing inside answers a model produces. Each
pulls from a different web index, but most of these indexes are the
result of the same crawl, rendering, and quality work. This post walks
through how to optimize for this new type of search. It provides
sources for every recommendation.

MISCELLANEOUS

 EVERY AI SUBSCRIPTION IS A TICKING TIME BOMB FOR ENTERPRISE (11
MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Every AI lab is currently running at a loss. There is a large gulf
between what companies pay for AI subscriptions and what it actually
costs to serve these services. Every organization that has built
workflows, products, or entire business units on top of these
subsidized prices will find itself one day looking at a bill it did
not budget for. The subsidy creates dependency, and that dependency
will trap companies when the inevitable price increase occurs. 

 ON HUMANITY AND HUMAN BEINGS (8 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Dario buried "work and meaning" at the very back of Machines of
Loving Grace and admitted the section was short because he didn't have
answers. Demis says we'll need new philosophers. Every AI roadmap
right now describes capex and scaling laws in tight numbers and the
human side in fuzzy gestures about meaning and adaptation. If you
can't describe what one specific person does on a normal Tuesday in
the world you're building, you don't have a future, you have an
aesthetic.
