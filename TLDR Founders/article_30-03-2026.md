 YC W26 DEMO DAY: 14 COMPANIES HIT $1M ARR BEFORE PRESENTING (5 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 190 companies presented this week. 85% were AI-first. 14 hit $1M ARR
before demo day, the highest ever for any YC batch. The bigger signal
here is that the bar for what counts as "early stage" keeps moving.
$1M ARR used to be a Series A milestone. Now, it's table stakes for a
demo day presentation. 

 REFLECTIONS ON THE STATE OF THE SOFTWARE AND AI MARKET (9 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 The AI market isn't the same as the Dotcom bubble of the past. The
technology is still early, and demand is pulling spending forward. The
software selloff is real, but it won't hit every layer equally, as
each has its own moats. Across the last few technological revolutions,
the companies that became durable winners were predominantly founded
in years four and five of each platform shift. We are now in that
window for AI.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 SELLING TO AI AGENTS (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Every major tech shift creates a new growth channel. AI is becoming a
decision-maker - they're starting to choose products. This new growth
channel is embedding products directly into the AI tools that people
use. This impacts strategy, product, and engineering. The companies
that will be marketing to the interface layers that make decisions on
users' behalf. 

 PER TOKEN PRICING IS COMING FOR EVERY AI COMPANY (8 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 The hardest problem in AI right now isn't the model, it's the
pricing. In traditional SaaS, you could underprice a seat, and it
didn't matter because the marginal cost of serving another user is
basically zero. Now every agent action, every "magic" feature triggers
real inference costs on the backend. Price too low and you're
literally paying customers to use your product. Price too high and you
lose to the competitor who figured it out first. Per-token and
credit-based pricing models are about to become the default because
they're the only ones that align cost structure with value delivered.

MISCELLANEOUS

 WHO TO HIRE AT STARTUPS: ANTIFRAGILE PEOPLE (12 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Antifragility is the premise that some things, including people,
improve from stressful situations. Disorder and uncertainty are the
rule at startups. Over time, antifragile people will consistently
improve their value/skills due to the continued exposure to a
startup's disorder. 

 THE ONLY PATH LEFT IN SOFTWARE: ACCELERATE OR DIE (6 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 Software companies have three paths right now: accelerate revenue
10%+ YoY through AI-native products, get stuck in the messy middle, or
rebuild to 40-50% operating margins. Even the profitability path is
deceptive. Most companies that look profitable on FCF margins are
still in the messy middle once you account for stock-based
compensation. The only real option is to accelerate. If you're not
rewriting your product around AI, you're choosing a slow death.
