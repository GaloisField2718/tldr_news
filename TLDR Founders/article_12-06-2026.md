HEADLINES & TRENDS

 ASSUME EVERYTHING YOU SAY AT WORK IS NOW RECORDED (5 MINUTE READ) [5]


 AI notetakers quietly made recording the default, and the smart play
is to treat it as permanent and onboard your AI like a new hire
instead of a document. You don't hand a new employee the wiki and walk
off - you put them in meetings, where the culture and the real
judgment calls actually live. AI learns the same way, except it sits
in every meeting and never gets bored. Talk-heavy companies like
Shopify and OpenAI get a compounding edge because the context that
used to evaporate in conversation finally gets captured. 

 VENTURE'S PAPER WEALTH IS AT A RECORD, ITS BANK ACCOUNTS ARE DRY (11
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Demo days are packed and $500M funds keep getting announced, but the
cash actually flowing back to investors has slowed to a trickle,
because the IPO and acquisition exits have been welded shut for years.
There are now more than 1,500 unicorns worth around $6 trillion with
nowhere to go, and at the current pace, one estimate says it would
take 49 years to clear the backlog. Look at the secondary market,
where stakes change hands at 40 to 60% below their official marks, the
real write-down happening where the quarterly letters won't show it.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 HOW THE FASTEST AI COMPANIES REBUILT CUSTOMER SUCCESS (15 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 CSM hiring flatlined for four years while Forward Deployed
Engineering grew 1,000%+, and Assembly AI's Ryan Seams confirmed it
filled a TAM pipeline overnight by switching the title to FDE,
Lovable's Monica Perez rebuilt CS on Lovable replacing Gainsight and
Harvey's Tom Ronen stayed traditional with on-site EBRs because
selling AI to law partners is change management not software. The
panel killed NPS unanimously, agreed 50%+ of CSM activities have no
correlation with retention, and Bobby Cooper's Weave case study showed
moving closed-won to an implementation success threshold dropped churn
from 4% to ~0.5% monthly while scaling $8M to $200M ARR. 

 WHY YOUR AI BILL KEEPS EXPLODING (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Most AI bills balloon for a dumb reason: companies run frontier
models for everything, when open-source models are now nearly as good
and often 90% cheaper. One team swapped off Opus and cut costs by 93%
with nobody noticing. The fastest win is routing: send only the
genuinely hard work to the expensive model and push the rest to a
cheap one, and the bill drops the same day. The other money pit is
rewarding raw usage, because the minute you post a most-tokens-used
leaderboard people burn cash to top it, so track spend per person and
judge everyone on impact instead. 

 IN A CONFUSING MARKET, BUYERS BUY YOUR POINT OF VIEW (5 MINUTE READ)
[9] 

 When a market is changing fast, buyers stall because they're scared
of picking something they'll regret in five years, and what reassures
them is hearing your clear view of where the whole market is going.
This is your belief about where things are heading, the assumption
your roadmap rests on, and it should be rooted in what you do better
than anyone. Notice how the giants each push a future that
conveniently centers their own strength: OpenAI says AI is a metered
utility, Microsoft says models are commodities and context is king,
and ServiceNow says it's all workflows and governance. Stake out yours
just as deliberately.

 HAVE YOU EARNED YOUR OPINION ON AI? (8 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Your opinion on AI is only worth something if you earned it by
actually using the tools, rather than absorbing a position by
proximity and defending it like a team you root for. Ask yourself what
would change your mind, because if your honest answer is nothing,
you've stopped holding a view and started defending an identity,
whichever side you're on. Being early counts for less than staying
clear-headed and updating as the evidence changes.
