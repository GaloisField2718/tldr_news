 REVENUE DURABILITY IS FAILING IN THE AGE OF AI (7 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Every VC, PE firm, and public market investor is circling the same
question in 2026: Is this company's revenue actually durable? The
answer is crushing valuations, freezing M&A, and making fundraising
brutal. Bill.com dropped from 90% growth to 12%. Snowflake fell from
73% to 26%. The uncomfortable truth is that nobody has real confidence
that software revenue will hold up over the next decade, and that
uncertainty alone is enough to reshape how every founder thinks about
pricing, retention, and defensibility. 

 WHY FREE TRIALS DON'T MAKE SENSE ANYMORE (AND WHAT USER ACQUISITION
TACTIC TO TRY INSTEAD) (11 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 AI-driven costs, shorter payback expectations, and web funnels are
causing free trials to decline as companies push for faster
monetization. Short trials now dominate despite lower conversion. Use
paid trials and trial qualifier events to improve signal quality. They
filter for high-intent users, enabling more effective UA optimization
while maintaining sufficient event volume for ad networks.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 I WANTED TO BUILD A VERTICAL SAAS FOR PEST CONTROL. I TOOK A
TECHNICIAN JOB INSTEAD (7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Taking a job in an industry you want to get into is a good way to
figure out what problems the industry faces. Terry Clarke wanted to
build a vertical SaaS for pest control, so he applied for jobs, got
licensed, and started documenting all the issues he faced in his role.
He moved about the company he worked in to learn more about how it
worked, and eventually left the company to start his own. The
experience helped him learn what tooling to build for his platform. 

 HOW TO BUILD A PMF MACHINE (9 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Start with a metric, then build the feature. Rank your ideas
according to which of them has the highest probability of moving the
metric. Estimate how much effort each idea would take to realize. You
can then see which tasks are high-impact and low effort. Constantly
revise roadmaps to avoid wasting engineering time, and measure
everything to calibrate better.

 SOFTWARE ENGINEERING CALORIES: WHAT WOULD CAL.AI SAY ABOUT YOUR
PRODUCT? (7 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Founders should treat their products like their bodies. We gain
weight if we eat more calories than we burn, and lose weight if we
burn more calories than we eat. For software, the input is code, and
the output is feature adoption. Some products would genuinely be
better if half their features were deleted. More weight should mean
more strength. 

 THERE ARE ONLY TWO PATHS LEFT FOR SOFTWARE (11 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Software companies face two paths to survive market pressure:
accelerate growth through new AI-native products or achieve over 40%
operating margins, including stock compensation. CEOs need to
decisively select a path, restructure leadership, and either innovate
with high-growth AI products or refocus on profitability through
higher efficiency and pricing. Choosing neither and staying in the
middle will lead to growth pressure and diminished market value.
