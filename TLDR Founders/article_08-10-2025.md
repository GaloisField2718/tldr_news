 EVERYTHING OPENAI ANNOUNCED AT DEVDAY 2025 (1 MINUTE READ) [6]

 NYSE OWNER ICE COMMITS UP TO $2B INVESTMENT IN BETTING PLATFORM
POLYMARKET (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 The NYSE owner, Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), plans to invest up
to $2 billion in the prediction marketplace Polymarket, valuing it at
around $8 billion. This move seeks to provide legitimacy and expand
Polymarket's reach, potentially aiding its return to the US market.
ICE will also distribute Polymarket's event-driven data and
collaborate on tokenization projects.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 7 QUESTIONS EVERY DATA TEAM SHOULD ASK THE BUSINESS (5 MINUTE READ)
[9] 

 Most data teams struggle with the same problem: nobody knows what to
work on. The business asks for dashboards, the analysts build them,
and a month later, everyone quietly stops using them. This piece
argues it's a conversation problem. The author's team at Facebook used
to sit down with every department twice a year and ask questions that
got to the real issues: What's keeping you from hitting your targets?
When was the last time data changed your mind? Where are you putting
in effort without results? These questions don't sound technical, but
they surface where data can actually help. If you stop asking what
people â€œneed from dataâ€ and start asking how they make decisions,
you'll finally find work that matters. 

 ELAD GIL'S FRAMEWORK FOR SPOTTING BILLION-DOLLAR MARKETS BEFORE THEY
LOOK BIG (5 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Markets beat teams beat ideas - great teams can't escape bad markets,
but great markets give you multiple shots on goal. Wedding invitations
seemed too niche, payroll too boring, file storage too late, then
became Minted, Gusto, and Dropbox. Four principles for finding
overlooked opportunities: use first principles to ask what's changed
in cost or infrastructure, scratch your own itch with real pain, not
vague ideas, build product-first because "would you use this" is weak
but "would you pay now" is real, and look where smart people tinker
for fun before mainstream notices.

 A founder decided to measure what makes great writing tick,

 BUILD A BILLION-DOLLAR STARTUP WITH A MINIMAL TEAM THANKS TO AI (4
MINUTE READ) [17] 

 Instagram sold for $1B with 13 people. WhatsApp had 55. PostHog ships
with pods of two to six, and Basecamp runs profitable software with
roughly 30 and no managers. The observable pattern is to design for
systems over headcount. In practice, that means your engineer answers
the support ticket instead of waiting for â€œcustomer success,â€ your
designer pushes copy tweaks straight to production instead of filing a
