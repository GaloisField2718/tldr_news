HEADLINES & TRENDS

 THE GREAT SOFTWARE CORRECTION OF 2026 (8 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 IGV dropped 28% from its September peak. Microsoft cratered 12% in a
single day despite beating earnings. Oracle is down 56%. It's not a
temporary panic - it's the market finally pricing in a structural
shift where AI eats into the application layer and seat-based pricing
models start to break down. Whether you agree or not, every founder
selling software should understand the argument. 

 WHAT FOLKS ARE REALLY VIBE CODING TODAY (18 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 The top use cases for vibe coding in businesses right now are rapid
prototyping without waiting on engineering, building internal tools
that match processes and needs, turning slide decks into real,
interactive presentations, and replacing simple SaaS with custom-built
solutions. Vibe coding is eliminating the gap between having an idea
and seeing it work. People who need tools are now the ones building
them.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 YOUR PRODUCT SHOULD PROVE ITS VALUE IN UNDER 60 SECONDS (10 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 Most products lose users because nothing meaningful happens early
enough. Users are looking for a reason to believe. Products must earn
confidence before asking for effort. Otherwise, users simply
disengage. 

 THE GROWTH META: EVENTS AS A GROWTH CHANNEL (10 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Events should be a central part of a launch strategy. They are wildly
underutilized by early-stage founders. Events are one of the highest
leverage go-to-market activities. In person and live is a different
type of magic - there's no way to automate a handshake.

MISCELLANEOUS

 THE CASE FOR SCALING VENTURE (21 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Top venture firms argue that scaling VC to raise massive funds is
essential as more startups require significant capital to compete in
today's market. The abundance of larger companies and increasing
private valuations mean VCs must adapt, focusing on supporting
founders with resources beyond just financial backing. 

 RENTED VIRTUE (20 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 A Quaker lit a blast furnace in 1709 and accidentally started the
Industrial Revolution. The businesses that endure across generations
have something at their center that can't be justified in purely
secular terms. The AI megacycle will produce more overcapitalized
companies seeking an identity than any boom in history. The ones that
last will look, to outside eyes, irrational.
