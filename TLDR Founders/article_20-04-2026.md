HEADLINES & TRENDS

 IT'S TIME TO EXPOSE A HUGE SCAM IN AI STARTUPS: CONTRACTED ARR (1
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Many AI startups are crushing revenue records because they are using
a dishonest metric. Companies are reporting inflated ARR due to
long-term enterprise deals they can report now but also opt out of
after 12 months. On top of this, some companies are bundling full-time
forward-deployed engineers into deals that massively reduce margins
and sometimes produce negative margins. A wave of enterprise AI
companies will collapse if customers start triggering their opt-out
clauses or aggressively negotiate down long-term pricing. 

 THE MOST IN-DEMAND STARTUP ROLES RIGHT NOW (6 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 The types of roles at startups are changing. Keeping up with the
industry now requires adapting your skillset and evolving. This post
puts together a list of some of the hottest roles in tech right now.
They include internal automations and efficiency, founding
salesperson, labs teams, forward-deployed engineering, creative
direction/content, go-to-market engineering, and chief of staff.

STRATEGIES & TACTICS

 YOUR COMPANY NEEDS A BRAIN, NOT MORE CONNECTORS (13 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Companies need to have a continuously updated, source-grounded,
conflict-resolved understanding of who they are, what they're doing,
and how they work. This should be delivered as files that any agent
can read. This understanding will compound and create value that no
amount of money can buy later. It will create a new moat that's
proprietary and grows over time. 

 HOW TO HIRE PEOPLE BETTER THAN YOU (10 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 The hiring problem nobody talks about is that you can't evaluate
domain expertise you don't have. You know you need someone sharper
than you in a given area, and that's exactly why you can't tell which
candidate actually is. The test that works is whether they generate
ideas that reshape how you think about the company and whether you'd
learn from them in meetings. The sharpest gut check is simpler, which
is that if you feel it's easier to just do the work yourself, you
hired the wrong person. 

 HOW TO COMPETE WITH LEGACY INCUMBENTS (7 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Vertical AI startups can compete with legacy incumbents by building
customer-centric "systems of love," ensuring standalone product paths,
and partnering strategically with non-dominant ERPs. Founders should
avoid costly litigation and consider giving up margins if necessary to
maintain growth momentum. Structural inertia and poor responsiveness
of incumbents create opportunities for new platforms.

MISCELLANEOUS

 NUMBER GO DOWN: INNOVATION, CAPITAL, AND DEFLATION FROM FIRST
PRINCIPLES (30 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The 2% inflation target shapes the cost of capital for every founder
raising a round, so it's worth knowing where the number came from.
You'd assume rigorous economic research. Nope. A New Zealand finance
minister pulled it out of the air during a 1980s TV interview to
influence public expectations, and every central bank on earth just
copied it. The broader argument is that good deflation (prices falling
because entrepreneurs found cheaper ways to produce) is actually the
definition of economic progress, and Moore's Law is the cleanest proof
since nobody waited until 2024 to buy a computer despite knowing
prices would halve every 18 months. 

 THERE IS NO PIVOT (9 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 When software was expensive to build, commitment was cheap, and
changing direction was the crisis that demanded a name. Now that
building is cheap, the economics invert. Commitment becomes the
expensive thing, and iteration is the default mode. The companies
winning now look less like ships changing course and more like test
kitchens running permanent R&D, which is why calling any given change
a pivot has started to sound like a word from a different era. 

 HOW TO ANGEL INVEST (EVEN IF YOU HAVE NO MONEY) (3 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Founders in the AI sector currently prioritize investors who offer
specialized operational expertise and industry connections rather than
simple capital deployment. Emerging professionals can bypass personal
capital constraints by joining venture capital scout programs, which
provide institutional funding allocations in exchange for a percentage
of future returns. Successful angels build their portfolios by
focusing exclusively on domains where they can provide unique
strategic value and by restricting their investments to intimately
known peers or exceptionally proven founders.
