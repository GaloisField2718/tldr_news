 HOW GOOD ENGINEERS WRITE BAD CODE AT BIG COMPANIES (10 MINUTE READ)
[11] 

 Surprisingly, sloppy code at big tech companies isn't due to
incompetent engineers, but rather the common practice of assigning
engineers to unfamiliar codebases. Engineers often work outside their
expertise, rotate frequently, and are incentivized to change jobs,
resulting in a lack of long-term ownership and expertise in specific
systems. 

 IT'S ALWAYS THE PROCESS, STUPID! (3 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Implementing AI on top of broken business processes will only
accelerate the generation of inefficiencies. Instead of viewing AI as
a magical solution, enterprises should focus on optimizing their own
processes first,Â  especially when dealing with unstructured data. 

 WHY YOUR BEST ENGINEERS ARE INTERVIEWING ELSEWHERE (23 MINUTE READ)
[13] 

 Many companies struggle with retaining engineers because of poor
information flow within their hierarchical structure, leading to
executives being unaware of critical issues until it's too late.
Companies need to prioritize open communication channels like
skip-level meetings and act on the feedback they receive to show that
engineers' input is valued.

MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Devs should resist the pressure to integrate AI into every feature,
and instead use deterministic code for simple, well-defined tasks.

complex problems involving unstructured data, natural language
understanding, or creative generation.
