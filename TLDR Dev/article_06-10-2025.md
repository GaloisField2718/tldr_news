ARTICLES & TUTORIALS

 CONSISTENT HASHING (12 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Consistent hashing is an algorithm that minimizes key recomputation
when a hash table's size changes, specifically in distributed caching
scenarios. The traditional hashing approach suffers from complete
cache invalidation upon node additions or removals. Consistent hashing
maps both nodes and items onto a circle, assigning items to the
nearest node clockwise, limiting the impact of node changes. 

 WHICH TABLE FORMAT DO LLMS UNDERSTAND BEST? (RESULTS FOR 11 FORMATS)
(9 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 This study tested 11 different data formats for LLM understanding by
feeding GPT-4.1-nano 1,000 employee records and assessing its ability
to answer questions accurately. Markdown-KV had the highest accuracy,
while CSV and JSONL performed poorly, suggesting that format impacts
LLM comprehension a lot. However, Markdown-KV also used more tokens
than the most token-efficient format of CSV. 

 LORA WITHOUT REGRET (33 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 The Thinking Machines Lab published research on Low-Rank Adaptation
(LoRA), a more efficient fine-tuning method for LLMs. Their findings
show that LoRA can achieve similar performance to full fine-tuning
when applied to all layers, especially MLP layers, and when not
constrained by capacity limitations relative to dataset size. The
study also found that optimal learning rates for LoRA are consistently
10x higher than those for full fine-tuning, and offers insights into
setting LoRA hyperparameters.

 WHAT GPT-OSS LEAKS ABOUT OPENAI'S TRAINING DATA (11 MINUTE READ) [20]


 A recent analysis of OpenAI's open-weights model, GPT-oss, revealed
details about the training data used for GPT-5. By examining the
model's token embeddings, researchers identified "glitch tokens" with
high L2 norms, including phrases from adult websites and other spam
content, showing that these sources were part of the training data.
The study also found weak evidence suggesting that GitHub may have
been scraped as part of the training corpus.
