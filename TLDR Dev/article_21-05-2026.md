 HOW TO WRITE "GARBAGE CODE" (BY LINUS TORVALDS) (7 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Linus Torvalds, the creator of Git and Linux, says that overly
complex or abstracted code, like unnecessary helper functions, makes
code maintainability and understanding worse. It increases cognitive
burden and, in some cases, it makes sense to repeat code to reduce
human and LLM context switching. Code should be optimized for
readability and ease of iteration. 

 THE TACIT DIMENSION: WHY YOUR BEST ENGINEERS CAN'T TELL YOU WHAT THEY
KNOW (12 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Software engineering expertise relies on tacit knowledge, an
intuitive understanding that AI cannot grasp since it is only trained
on explicit text and code. Over-reliance on AI risks losing such
important architectural knowledge, making senior engineers and
apprenticeship irreplaceable for transmitting these complex, unwritten
insights.
