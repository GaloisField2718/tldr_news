 KAFKA IS FAST -- I'LL USE POSTGRES (34 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Developers often prematurely adopt complex technologies like Kafka
when Postgres would suffice. Benchmarks show Postgres' impressive
scalability for both pub/sub messaging and queueing, often meeting the
needs of most organizations. Despite concerns about scaling, the
article shows Postgres' scalability and suggests that many projects
have ample time to migrate if necessary. 

 I BUILT THE SAME APP 10 TIMES: EVALUATING FRAMEWORKS FOR MOBILE
PERFORMANCE (45 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 A developer built the same Kanban app 10 times to evaluate mobile
performance, finding that next-gen frameworks (Marko, SolidStart, and
SvelteKit) delivered 35-39ms load times with bundles 4-6x smaller than
Next.js, which shipped 176.3 kB compressed and loaded in 467ms. It was
found that React has an unavoidable performance ceiling, since
TanStack Start with React delivered 118.2 kB compressed versus 60.4 kB
with Solid using identical infrastructure, proving React's runtime
adds 2x overhead. 

 AWS TO BARE METAL TWO YEARS LATER: ANSWERING YOUR TOUGHEST QUESTIONS
ABOUT LEAVING AWS (9 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 OneUptime migrated from AWS to bare-metal two years ago, and they are
now saving $1.2M/year. The migration involved running MicroK8s + Ceph
with high availability, improving latency, and reinvesting savings
into AI servers. They found bare metal cheaper than AWS after
factoring in all costs, including bandwidth and control plane fees,
and despite initial concerns, reliability improved.

 A BRIEF HISTORY OF DOMAINS (12 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Paul Mockapetris invented DNS to map domain names to IP addresses,
creating a centralization point in the Internet. There was a domain
gold rush where early investors registered domains for free and later
sold them for millions (like sex.com for $12 million). This article
covers ICANN's 1998 creation to govern TLDs, how registries bid for
TLD rights in auctions (Google paid $25 million for .app), and
dramatic geopolitical domain stories, like one about Yugoslavia's .yu
domain, which was literally stolen when scientists cut network lines
with scissors during the country's breakup. 

 DISASTERS I'VE SEEN IN A MICROSERVICES WORLD, PART II (8 MINUTE READ)
[15] 

 Recurring disasters in microservice architectures are usually due to
four specific problems: having more services than engineers, poorly
designed gateway layers, unconstrained technology sprawl, and
architectures mirroring organizational charts rather than domains.
Distributed systems, including emerging AI agents, still suffer from
fundamental challenges like latency and observability. 

 IS LLAMA REALLY AS BAD AS PEOPLE SAY? I PUT META'S AI TO THE TEST (11
MINUTE READ) [16] 

 Meta's Llama 3.2 1B model is a useful, lightweight coding assistant
for development work. It's not as bad as critics claim. However, it
can't handle agentic coding and produces functional but imperfect
code, constantly requiring manual fixes. Llama is best for simple
projects like todo apps.
