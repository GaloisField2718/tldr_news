ARTICLES & TUTORIALS

 HOW KERNEL ANTI-CHEATS WORK: A DEEP DIVE INTO MODERN GAME PROTECTION
(54 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Modern kernel anti-cheat systems, like BattlEye and Vanguard, operate
at the highest privilege level in Windows, necessitated by the failure
of user-mode protections against advanced kernel-level cheats. They
have a three-component architecture, using kernel callbacks for
real-time system monitoring, memory scanning, and advanced
anti-injection and hook detection methods. 

 WHAT WE LEARNED FROM A 22-DAY STORAGE BUG (AND HOW WE FIXED IT) (16
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Mux experienced a 22-day storage bug between January 8 and February
4, during which 0.33% of audio and video segments were served in a
corrupted state. An optimization to use fewer, larger storage nodes
created bottlenecks that amplified pre-existing issues like remote
read cancellations, a race condition in file deletions, and a bug
where partially written segments were treated as complete. To fix
this, Mux addressed the deletion purge path, resolved remote read
context cancellation, and increased storage node counts, then
regenerated all affected segments and purged CDN caches. 

 FRONTEND MEMORY LEAKS: A 500-REPOSITORY STATIC ANALYSIS AND
FIVE-SCENARIO BENCHMARK STUDY (32 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A study analyzed 500 public React, Vue, and Angular repositories and
conducted five benchmark scenarios to understand the prevalence and
impact of frontend memory leaks caused by missing cleanup patterns.
The static analysis found that 86% of repositories had at least one
missing-cleanup pattern, with timers and event listeners being the
most common offenders. Controlled benchmarks consistently showed that
each unhandled pattern resulted in approximately 8 KB of retained heap
growth per component mount/unmount cycle.

OPINIONS & ADVICE

 GRIEF AND THE AI SPLIT (8 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI-assisted coding has revealed a pre-existing split among
developers: one group mourns the loss of the craft and artistry of
writing code, while the other prioritizes making computers achieve
desired outcomes. AI tools are a natural progression of software
engineering though, shifting the puzzle-solving aspect of it to a more
abstract, architectural level. 

 18 MONTHS OF CODE, GONE. HERE'S WHAT WE LEARNED. (12 MINUTE READ) [9]


 After 18 months of development, acquiring clients, and raising
funding, Autonoma is completely rewriting its product. This decision
was driven by the regret of an initial "no-tests" culture that led to
unmanageable tech debt and lost clients as the team expanded. It also
abandoned Next.js Server Actions in favor of React with tRPC and a
Hono backend. 

 OPTIMIZING CONTENT FOR AGENTS (5 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Content should be optimized for AI agents, similar to how it's
optimized for human users. This is necessary because AI agents,
particularly frontier models, have specific behaviors and constraints,
such as only reading parts of files or responding differently to
direct information versus self-discovery. The recommended
implementation involves content negotiation, where an `Accept:
text/markdown` header signals an agent request, prompting the server
to deliver specialized content.

MISCELLANEOUS

 MCP IS DEAD; LONG LIVE MCP! (18 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 When comparing CLIs vs MCPs for coding agents, MCPs are better for
enterprise and organizational adoption. MCP over streamable HTTP
provides advantages such as centralization, robust security,
comprehensive telemetry, and standardized, dynamic delivery of tools,
prompts, and resources. For well-known tools, CLIs do provide more
token savings. 

 TWENTY YEARS OF AMAZON S3 AND BUILDING WHAT'S NEXT (7 MINUTE READ)
[17] 

 Amazon S3 launched in 2006, unexpectedly shaping the internet
infrastructure by offering developers highly scalable, reliable, and
inexpensive web-scale storage. Over two decades, S3 has grown from
petabytes to hundreds of exabytes and trillions of objects. Looking
forward, S3 is evolving beyond simple storage to become a universal
foundation for AI and data workloads, introducing capabilities like S3
Tables, Vectors, and Metadata to directly support complex applications
on stored data. 

 THE 49MB WEB PAGE (15 MINUTE READ) [18] 

 Modern news websites are bloated, slow, and usually have a lot of ads
covering their UIs. Publishers prioritize short-term ad revenue (CPMs)
over reader retention, resulting in massive data downloads, constant
surveillance, and a high cognitive burden on users. This also shows up
in the download size of web pages, some being slow to load because of
all the data being downloaded.
