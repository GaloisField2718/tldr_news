 GOOGLE PHOTOS ADDS A QUICK TOGGLE BETWEEN AI AND CLASSIC SEARCH (2
MINUTE READ) [4]

 Google Photos introduced a toggle letting users switch instantly
between Gemini-powered Ask Photos and classic keyword search, without
navigating through settings. The addition responds to user complaints
about irrelevant AI results and the previous absence of a quick way
back to classic search, following a mid-2025 rollout pause. Users can
now choose their preferred search mode per query, though Google still
applies its own judgment about which system fits, and non-adopters
must complete onboarding before accessing the AI option.

 WEBFLOW MCP 2.0 IS A BIG UPGRADE. HERE'S WHAT IT CAN DO (6 MINUTE
READ) [5]

 Webflow's MCP 2.0 upgrade lets AI agents build components from
screenshots, edit over 50 CSS-variable design tokens per conversation,
and query analytics conversationally, without needing the previous
bridge app for most tasks. New "Instructions" featuresвЂ”rules and
skillsвЂ”let teams embed always-on guardrails and on-demand workflows
directly into Webflow for agent use. Additional capabilities include
managing forms, assets, page branches, custom code, and
Enterprise-level site history tracking.

 HOW TO APPLY NIR EYAL'S HOOK MODEL TO AI PRODUCTS, SAFELY (16 MINUTE
READ) [7]

 Nir Eyal's Hook ModelвЂ”trigger, action, reward, investmentвЂ”maps
onto AI products almost perfectly, with variable rewards and
frictionless chat boxes arriving nearly pre-built. Eyal's own
"Manipulation Matrix" and regret test offer a check: products that
improve users' lives and that builders would use themselves qualify as
facilitators, while those failing both tests risk becoming
exploitative "dealers." The loop measures consumption, not benefit, so
real success requires tracking outcomes like time saved rather than
engagement metrics like messages sent.

 HOW TO TURN YOUR COMPETITOR'S WORST REVIEWS INTO YOUR STRONGEST
DESIGN ARGUMENT (4 MINUTE READ) [8]

 Designers can turn competitors' negative reviews into persuasive
evidence, since scattered complaint patterns reveal gaps stakeholders
can't dismiss as opinion. Mining sources like Reddit, G2, and
Trustpilot for recurring 1-2 star complaintsвЂ”time-boxed and counted,
not curatedвЂ”surfaces real user language and frustration clusters.
Charting these clusters' frequency converts qualitative gripes into
data-backed arguments that move stakeholder conversations from opinion
to evidence.

 AI CAN FAKE YOUR PORTFOLIO. IT CAN'T FAKE THE SECOND QUESTION (12
MINUTE READ) [9]

 AI has made polished portfolios, case studies, and take-home
assignments easy to generate, shifting the real proof of a designer's
ability from artifacts to live conversations where they must explain,
defend, and reflect on their decisions under pressure. Future
interviews will increasingly focus on these "room tests," which reveal
judgment, trade-off thinking, and experience through unscripted
follow-up questions rather than polished presentations. The solution
isn't to perfect portfolios but to regularly practice articulating
decisions, discussing failures, and defending work in real time,
because those skills are becoming the strongest signal of genuine
expertise.

 WHAT IF DESIGN COULD MAKE SCIENCE IRRESISTIBLE TO KIDS? (8 MINUTE
READ) [13]

 Scintillab, an educational kit co-designed by Fondazione OpenDot with
Fondazione Agnelli, Ferrari, and CNR, brings hands-on STEM
experimentation into Italian primary school classrooms. In its first
year, 2025/2026, it has reached 59 schools and about 4,700 students
across six provinces, with plans to expand nationally to 15,000
students. Built on inquiry-based learning, it uses simple tools like
shadow theatres and ramps, encouraging children to experiment and
learn from mistakes rather than lectures.

 AI MANIA IS EVISCERATING GLOBAL DECISION-MAKING (25 MINUTE READ) [14]


 Companies overwhelmingly fail at AI adoption, yet fear and internal
politics prevent executives, employees, and vendors from admitting
these failures publicly or privately. This dynamic forces
organizations to fake AI success, punish honest skeptics, and
prioritize "AI-washing" projects over genuinely useful work. The
result is widespread paralysis in institutional decision-making, with
practical survival advice offered for professionals stuck navigating
this manufactured mania.

 HOW DID STUDIO CHAPEAUX BUILD A TYPEFACE SPECIFICALLY FOR PACKAGING?
(3 MINUTE READ) [15]

 Studio Chapeaux argues that typography has become the most important
element of packaging design, with bold, distinctive typefaces
outperforming logos and illustrations by remaining legible,
expressive, and impactful on crowded shelves. To demonstrate this
approach, the studio created SC Ostrea, a custom typeface designed
specifically for its oyster and beer brand, showing how typography can
become an integral part of a product's identity rather than a
supporting element. The project also marks the beginning of a type
foundry focused on packaging, addressing what the studio sees as a
lack of typefaces optimized for high-impact commercial products.
