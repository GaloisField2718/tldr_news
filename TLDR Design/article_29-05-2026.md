 IOS 27 LEAK REVEALS NEW SIRI DESIGN, CAMERA APP, MORE (4 MINUTE READ)
[4] 

 Bloomberg's new leaked illustrations of iOS 27 reveal Apple's biggest
Siri redesign yet, with the assistant moving into the Dynamic Island
and gaining a new “Search or Ask” interface that combines voice
commands, typed requests, web search, and chatbot-style conversations.
The update will reportedly let users launch apps, send messages,
search personal data, and access AI-powered results through rich
interactive cards, while also supporting third-party AI services like
ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude. 

 ROKU CHANGES HOMESCREEN WITH MAJOR OVERHAUL (3 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Roku is overhauling its homescreen for the first time in over a
decade to drive more engagement through personalized content
recommendations and improved app discovery. The new interface will
prominently feature users' most-used apps, include "top picks for you"
recommendations, and adapt to different household members' viewing
habits. The update aims to maintain Roku's simplicity while making the
platform smarter and more useful for its 100 million-plus users. 

 GOOGLE WALLET AND ITS REDESIGN WERE SUPPOSED TO IMPROVE THE USER
EXPERIENCE, BUT A LOT OF PEOPLE ARE FURIOUS. WHY? (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Google redesigned its Wallet app to improve user experience by adding
colors, new buttons, and better organization of ID cards like
passports and driver's licenses. Many users are frustrated with the
changes, complaining about poor readability due to smaller fonts, a
confusing layout with two cards displayed side by side, and a hidden
search feature.

 I'M MAKING A SPEC FOR DESIGN SYSTEM DOCUMENTATION (6 MINUTE READ) [7]


 The author is creating a Design System Documentation Spec to
standardize how design system knowledge is documented and stored. The
spec aims to solve issues like vendor lock-in, data portability, and
fragmented documentation by providing a structured format with two
main sections: entities and document blocks. This standardized
approach would create a true single source of truth, enable easier
tool building, and support AI integration through structured data. 

 WHY I DON'T USE ICON FONTS IN FIGMA (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Icon fonts in Figma require a cumbersome 7-step process that involves
leaving the application to browse icons on external websites and
copying tiny renderings. Users must memorize exact icon names without
visual previews, creating a poor experience compared to SVG components
with fuzzy search and instance-swapping capabilities. Icon fonts also
make comparing different glyphs tedious since icons can't be easily
placed side by side for visual comparison. 

 THE MCDONALD'S CHARITY LOGO FINALLY GOT THE REDESIGN IT DESPERATELY
NEEDED (4 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Ronald McDonald House Charities has introduced a long-overdue rebrand
that replaces its outdated logo featuring Ronald McDonald's hand with
a warmer, more modern and versatile identity better suited to its
global mission of supporting families with ill children. The redesign
keeps the charity's emotional warmth and connection to McDonald's
through color and hand-drawn elements, while improving clarity, donor
appeal, and independence as the organization expands toward its goal
of doubling the number of families it serves by 2030.

 DESIGNING WHERE THE PIXELS ACTUALLY LIVE (6 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 A senior product designer at Slack describes how AI tooling helped
her bridge the longstanding gap between static design mockups and
real, interactive product experiences. Working primarily in Cursor,
she now builds and deploys functional prototypes that teammates can
actually test, enabling faster alignment and richer design
conversations. The transition came with trade-offs — AI occasionally
breaks existing work and demands smaller, more deliberate iteration
steps — but the ability to design closer to the real experience
feels like a meaningful shift. 

 WHY UX SHOULD OWN RETENTION (4 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 UX teams should own customer retention since acquiring new customers
costs five times more than keeping existing ones, yet most
organizations focus on acquisition while neglecting retention
strategy. Retention currently falls between departments with no clear
ownership, creating an opportunity for UX to claim this strategic
responsibility and tie their work directly to revenue metrics. By
owning retention, UX can shift from being perceived as making screens
look nice to being seen as strategically important for business
outcomes like lifetime customer value and churn reduction. 

 THE REVOLUTION WILL NOT BE OPTIMISED: ON FRICTIONMAXXING AND THE
CREATIVE VIRTUES OF INCONVENIENCE (10 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Modern life is increasingly designed to eliminate friction and
inconvenience, but in doing so it may also be removing the pauses,
boredom, and small inefficiencies that foster creativity, intimacy,
reflection, and human connection. The growing “frictionmaxxing”
trend argues that some forms of slowness and inconvenience — like
walking to a shop, cooking, waiting, or doing things manually — are
not obstacles to life but essential parts of experiencing it fully,
pushing back against a culture obsessed with constant optimisation and
productivity.
