 GEMINI PREPS MARKUP EDITING FOR NANO BANANA IMAGES (4 MINUTE READ)
[4] 

 Google is testing a built-in markup tool in Gemini that lets users
highlight parts of AI-generated images and request targeted edits
directly inside the chat, removing the need for external editors. The
feature would enable faster, more precise region-based edits and
support Google's goal of making Gemini a more seamless multimodal
assistant.

 CANVAS IN AI MODE LAUNCHES FOR EVERYONE IN THE US (1 MINUTE READ) [6]


 Canvas in AI Mode is now available for everyone in the US in English,
providing a dedicated space to organize plans and projects with
support for creative writing and coding tasks. Users can create custom
interactive tools and dashboards by selecting Canvas from the tool

working prototypes that can be refined through conversational

 DESIGN SYSTEM CONTRIBUTIONS WORK BETTER WHEN EVERYONE KNOWS YOUR NAME
(5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Design system contributions work better in small organizations where
team members know each other personally, whereas in larger companies,
complex processes make contributions ineffective. Contribution models
should be simple enough to describe in a few paragraphs, relying on
established relationships and trust rather than elaborate procedures.
In small teams, a basic two-step process works best: create a recipe
for the shared library, then optionally review it with the design
system team for potential inclusion. 

 THE JOY OF BUILDING SLOW (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Five years ago, the author left a cushy job to start Not Boring
Software, aiming to replace boring daily software with apps that make
users feel something and want to spend time using them. Rather than
following the typical tech industry script of rapid growth and
scaling, the company chose to build slowly and sustainably, creating
weather apps, timers, calculators, and other tools. This approach
trades an infinitesimal chance at massive success for a real
opportunity at meaningful, lasting success. 

 WHY THE CREATIVE INDUSTRY FEELS BROKEN RIGHT NOW (AND WHY IT ISN'T)
(7 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 The creative design industry has shifted from a vibrant, creator-led
field in the 1980s to a corporatized system where holding companies,
weakened design education, austerity, and cost-cutting have devalued
creativity and worsened working conditions. Despite this decline,
growing dissatisfaction is fueling a new wave of independent,
creator-led agencies and a potential renaissance in creative work.

 DESIGN SYSTEMS CAN'T AUTOMATE AWAY ALL OF YOUR ACCESSIBILITY
CONSIDERATIONS (11 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Accessible design systems dramatically reduce remediation work, but
automated checkers alone can't catch every issue. Critical gaps
include misused components, unmanaged keyboard focus, color-only
status indicators, poor text labels, and illogical heading structures.
Real accessibility requires human judgment, disabled user testing, and
attention to the full end-user experience beyond component-level
concerns. 

 CONFIGURATION COLLAPSE (6 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Figma components with excessive props create a scrollable
"configuration collapse" where too many visibility and layout options
make components fragile and hard to maintain. Native Figma slots will
replace many props with a composable design, allowing designers to
build components through creative composition rather than endless
switches and variants. This shift requires dropping complex props in
favor of leaner component APIs that retain only essential behavioral
properties, such as state, appearance, and size. 

 TRAINING DESIGN JUDGMENT, HOW TO READ PRODUCTS LIKE A SENIOR DESIGNER
(13 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 As AI makes design execution fast and accessible to anyone, the real
competitive advantage for designers is no longer producing UI, but
exercising judgment—understanding context, trade-offs, and user
behavior behind design decisions. Strong design judgment, built
through deliberate practice and analyzing products at multiple layers
(surface, structure, and intent), is what allows designers to use AI
effectively and remain valuable.
