 DESIGN SYSTEMS TEAM STRUCTURE IN 2026 (8 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Design systems team structures have changed dramatically due to AI
capabilities, with tasks that previously required three people in 2023
now often manageable by a single person with the right AI toolkit.
Teams are struggling not with headcount but with leverage, as AI can
now handle non-judgment work like documentation, auditing, and
scaffolding while humans focus on strategic decisions and relationship
building. The key is implementing systems that create scale before
adding headcount, as teams that understand what to automate versus
what to own can outperform much larger teams stuck in inefficient
workflows.

 USING DESIGN TOKENS FOR A PROPORTIONAL TYPOGRAPHIC SCALE (10 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 A proportional typographic scale creates a font size hierarchy using
consistent mathematical ratios, starting with a base size and
multiplying by a fixed ratio to generate larger and smaller sizes.
Design tokens are ideal for creating these scales because they can
reference other tokens' values and contain mathematical calculations,
making it easy to build sophisticated typographic systems with few
tokens. This approach ensures visual harmony and consistency across
designs while making the system scalable and maintainable for both
design and development teams. 

 DESIGNING WITH DATA: A UX RESEARCH GUIDE FOR DESIGNERS (7 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 UX research involves understanding user needs through methods such as
interviews, surveys, personas, and user testing to inform data-driven
design decisions. Key research methods include surveys for
quantitative data from many users, one-on-one interviews for deep
insights, observation of users in natural environments, and diary
studies to track experiences over time. Effective user interviews
require thorough preparation, relevant questions, and a focused yet
flexible approach to uncover insights that other methods might miss.
