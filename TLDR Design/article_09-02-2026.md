 DESIGNING BEYOND THE HAPPY PATH IN DESIGN SYSTEMS (9 MINUTE READ) [7]


 Designers typically focus on ideal "happy path" scenarios, but design
systems must account for real-world conditions such as loading delays,
errors, missing content, and diverse user needs. Design systems should
document technical states, build flexible components using container
queries, design inclusive interactions for various input methods, and
accommodate user preferences like zoom and offline modes. Early
collaboration between designers and developers, browser-based reviews,
and user research in their actual environments help create resilient
components.

 ERIC BAILEY'S PREDICTIONS FOR THE FUTURE OF ACCESSIBILITY (5 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Organizations will adopt "performative accessibility" solutions that
focus on compliance rather than on removing barriers for users,
similar to how GDPR led to widespread cookie consent banners rather
than better privacy practices. The European Accessibility Act deadline
will drive demand for quick-fix accessibility tools, including
AI-powered solutions trained on largely inaccessible web code. This
approach prioritizes contractual compliance over genuine care for
users with disabilities, potentially widening the gap between
accessibility work and its real human impact.
