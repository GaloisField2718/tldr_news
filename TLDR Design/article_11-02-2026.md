 SOME THOUGHTS ABOUT TOOL DESIGN AND AI (9 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Current AI tools poorly align with good tool design principles: they
exhibit high latency with multi-second waits, require text
descriptions rather than hands-on control, and impose usage costs or
limits. Emerging tools like real-time video manipulation models offer
a contrast by allowing immediate interaction, being open-source, and
enabling users to shape outputs directly. AI tooling should evolve
toward faster, more directly manipulable systems that reduce the gap
between user intent and execution, particularly as local models
improve. 

 HOW GENERATIVE AI IS REDEFINING BRAND IDENTITY SYSTEMS (5 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Generative AI is transforming brand identity from rigid, fixed assets
into flexible systems that adapt across contexts. Designers are
shifting from crafting individual assets to becoming system architects
who define constraints, behaviors, and brand logic. Successful
implementation requires establishing a clear core brand DNA, defining
what stays fixed and what is flexible, building governance early, and
addressing ethical considerations around bias and inclusivity.

 HOW TYPOGRAPHY HIERARCHY BOOSTS UI USABILITY (4 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Typography hierarchy organizes text by importance through size,
weight, color, spacing, and position, enabling users to scan
interfaces efficiently. An effective hierarchy reduces cognitive load,
improves scannability, supports faster decision-making, and enhances
accessibility. Common mistakes include using too many font sizes,
overusing emphasis, inconsistent styling, poor contrast, and
inadequate spacing.
