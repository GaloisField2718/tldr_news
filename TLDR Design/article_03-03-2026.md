 GETTY GETS A PUNCHY MODERN REBRAND ANCHORED BY GEOMETRIC "G" MOTIF (3
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 The Getty has unveiled a modern rebrand developed with Fred & Farid
New York that features a geometric "G" icon anchored by the tagline
"ALL FOR ART." The flexible "G" forms a square block referencing the
Getty Center's travertine architecture, with four mosaic-inspired
pieces representing Getty's core programs. The new visual identity
emphasizes Getty's commitment to expanding global access to art and
cultural heritage through a unified, adaptable design system. 

 A NEW PLATFORM "BY CREATORS, FOR CREATORS" WILL ROOT OUT AI DEEPFAKES
(2 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 FanLock is a new platform co-founded by sketch comedian Zander Small
to help creators identify and remove AI deepfakes across over four
million websites. The service costs $49+ per month and specifically
targets platforms like Telegram where deepfakes are commonly shared,
addressing a problem that existing DMCA services haven't fully solved.
With AI fraud losses predicted to reach $40 billion by 2027, the
platform aims to provide creators with better tools to protect their
likeness from unauthorized AI-generated content.

 BREAKING THE ECHO CHAMBER IN YOUR INTERFACE (7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Chatbots trained through reinforcement learning from human feedback
tend to validate users' ideas regardless of merit, creating
conversational echo chambers where agreement replaces genuine
analysis. Designing interfaces with productive friction can counteract
this sycophancy. The level of friction should scale with stakes:
casual tasks warrant smooth interactions, while high-stakes decisions
call for deliberate cognitive speed bumps.

 HOW TO PROTECT YOUR CREATIVE PROCESS WHEN REAL LIFE GETS DISTRACTING
(3 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 When life gets distracting, protect your creative process by taking
breaks when emotionally overwhelmed, working in short, focused bursts,
and collaborating with others for fresh perspectives. Say no to
draining projects and use templates when your brain feels fried to
maintain productivity without burnout. Handle non-creative tasks like
legal matters early so they don't interfere with your creative flow
later. 

 HOW TO WRITE A GOOD SPEC FOR AI AGENTS (37 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Writing effective AI agent specs requires clear, structured documents
covering six core areas вЂ” commands, testing, project structure, code
style, git workflow, and boundaries вЂ” while keeping them focused
enough to avoid overwhelming the model. Large specs should be broken
into modular, task-specific prompts rather than delivered all at once.
The process works best as a continuous loop: plan first in read-only
mode, execute incrementally, enforce three-tier constraints, and
refine the spec alongside the code as the project evolves.
