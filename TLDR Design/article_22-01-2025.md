NEWS & TRENDS

 TRUMP'S PATRIOTIC NEW WHITE HOUSE LOGO IS PAINFULLY UNSUBTLE (1
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Donald Trump's new White House logo for his second term is a bold
departure from traditional designs that emphasizes intricate details
and overt patriotism. Unlike the minimalism of previous
administrations, this design features vibrant American flag accents
and a stately appearance, symbolizing authority and reclaiming power.
The branding shift reflects a darker, more assertive tone for Trump's
leadership, marking a new era centered on patriotism and institutional
strength. 

 THE SWITCH 2 HAS A NEW TRIGGER FOR DISCONNECTING JOY-CON CONTROLLERS
(1 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Nintendo's Switch 2 introduces a new trigger mechanism for
disconnecting Joy-Con controllers, demonstrated in a video on its
website. While the detachment design is more transparent, the method
for keeping the controllers attached, which potentially involves
magnets, remains unconfirmed. 

 META IS BUILDING OAKLEY SMART GLASSES FOR ATHLETES (1 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Meta is reportedly developing Oakley-branded smart glasses for
athletes based on Oakley's Sphaera design, which features a centrally
positioned camera. The move aims to broaden the appeal of smart
glasses by leveraging Oakley's popularity among athletic groups like
cyclists and runners. Meta is also working on various AI-powered
devices, including camera-equipped earbuds, a smartwatch, and premium
AR glasses with a display in the right lens.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 JUST GROW UP: WHY DESIGN MATURITY MODELS MIGHT BE HARMING OUR
INDUSTRY! (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Design maturity models often mislabel strategic design choices as
“immature,” overlooking how businesses tailor design to their
market needs. Instead of chasing an unrealistic ideal, companies
should focus on aligning design with their strategic goals and
recognize that different industries and business models require
different approaches. 

 WHAT DOES 'SUCCESS' MEAN FOR CREATIVES IN 2025? (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Success in the creative world is often tied to external validation,
but true success is internal, driven by personal fulfillment, creative
joy, and meaningful work. For many creatives, success includes
financial stability, artistic purpose, and a balanced lifestyle.
Redefining success means focusing on personal values and the intrinsic
joy of creation. 

 DURABLE PRODUCTS (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Brad Frost emphasizes the value of durable, high-quality products,
using his Olukai slippers as an example. He advocates for creating
lasting digital products, similar to resilient web design by Jeremy
Keith. Frost applies this philosophy throughout his work, including
his book Atomic Design, courses, and consulting services, promoting
longevity in design systems.

MISCELLANEOUS

 NEW YORK CITY FC DEBUTS NEW IDENTITY BY GRETEL (6 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 New York City FC has revealed a refreshed identity designed by Gretel
that aims to capture the city's eclectic spirit and the club's passion
for soccer. The new visual identity features a refined badge, a custom
typeface inspired by the New York City subway, and an electric color
palette. 

 HOW DESIGNERS AND BRANDS ARE MORPHING THE NEXT ERA OF MOTION DESIGN
(7 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Our consumption of culture is driven by constant motion across
various screens, and motion design reflects this behavior. Once
meditative, motion design now defines brand identity through dynamic
elements. The rise in motion design demands scalable, efficient
solutions that balance creativity and guidelines. Designers and
clients navigate this evolving field with mutual respect for creative
processes. 

 WHY GOOD DESIGN COMES FROM BAD DESIGN (7 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Discovering his passion for design at Carnegie Mellon, a former
Computer Science student realized the value of iteration. Initially
embarrassed by bad ideas, he learned that exploring alternatives leads
to better solutions. Emphasizing process and revisions, he highlights
that even renowned creators like Hemingway and Einstein embraced
imperfect drafts to achieve excellence.
