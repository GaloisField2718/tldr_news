 GOOGLE'S AI MODE GETS BETTER AT UNDERSTANDING VISUAL PROMPTS (2
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Google's AI Mode now better understands visual search prompts through
an enhanced "query fan-out" technique, which runs multiple background
searches to interpret complex requests, such as "moody but maximalist"
bedrooms. The update makes the chatbot multimodal, allowing users to
initiate conversations with images or videos and receive more visual
responses rather than text-heavy answers. These improvements
particularly benefit shopping searches, enabling AI Mode to handle
nuanced queries such as "barrel jeans that aren't too baggy" with
refinable follow-up questions.

 WHERE'S THE AI DESIGN RENAISSANCE? (14 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Despite 2.5 years of intense AI hype, there's no evidence that
current AI tools are making design faster, eliminating designer jobs,
or enabling the promised software renaissanceâ€”metrics like new app
releases and domain registrations remain flat. AI struggles with
designs that require tight constraints, novelty, or complexity because
its architecture is based on training data, making it best suited for
average designs rather than bold, opinionated work that stands out.
Designers should focus on out-of-training-data skills, such as
pixel-perfect UI and novel interactions, while using AI primarily for
small personal projects, internal prototypes, and simple public tools
where security and complexity are minimal. 

 BORING IS GOOD (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI language models should focus on "boring" tasks, like proofreading
and summarizing, rather than attempting high-level thinking, where
hallucinations and faulty reasoning can lead to failures. Small
language models (SLMs) are sufficient for these modest language tasks,
offering benefits such as easier ethical training and lower costs. The
industry has been using LLMs backwards by trying to automate complex
tasks from the top down instead of augmenting simpler tasks from the
bottom up. 

 HOW DO GOOD DESIGNERS GET STUCK? THEY WASTE TIME TRYING TO MIND-READ
THEIR TEAM (2 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Good designers often get stuck by attempting to mind-read their
team's intentions when designs are rejected, creating false narratives
like "they don't value UX" instead of addressing the real issue. This
"LoserThink" pattern prevents designers from asking whether they're
solving the actual problem their stakeholders need solved. The
solution is to replace assumptions with direct questions about success
criteria and understanding team pressures through brief conversations.

 HOW DOUBLE DIAMOND REVIVED A RETRO BRAND WITHOUT IT FEELING DATED (4
MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The revival of the classic British beer brand Double Diamond brings
nostalgic charm with a modern twist. Originally brewed in 1876 and
once a UK best-seller, it faded with changing tastes but has now been
relaunched by Jamie Allsopp and Steve Holt using original recipes.
Designer Alec Tear and illustrator Satoshi Hashimoto refreshed its
iconic logo, typeface, and mascot, blending heritage with contemporary
appeal. The result evokes traditional pub culture while feeling bold
and relevant to today's drinkers.
