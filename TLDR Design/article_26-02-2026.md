 MY FAVORITE TYPE OF DESIGNERS (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 There are five standout types of designers, each valuable in
different contexts: the commercial chameleon, who adapts seamlessly to
any style or client; the execution star, who delivers precise,
reliable work when given clear direction; the beautiful visionary, who
generates bold, original concepts but struggles with consistency and
structure; the system architect, who brings order and functional
clarity through strong structural thinking; and the anthropologist
storyteller, who excels at cultural insight and early-stage research
but not technical execution. Most designers embody a mix of these
traits, and their strengths may shift over the course of a career.

 WHY AI IS EXPOSING DESIGN'S CRAFT CRISIS (12 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 AI design tools are revealing a craft crisis in design by exposing
the technical literacy gap that has been undermining designers'
strategic influence for over a decade. While tools like Figma's AI can
generate designs from text prompts, they produce technically flawed
outputs with serious accessibility violations and poor code quality
that only experienced designers can identify and fix. The paradox is
that AI democratization tools don't make design accessible to
everyone, they only help people who already understand what
constitutes good design and technical implementation.
