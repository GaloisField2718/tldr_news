IMAGES, AUDIO, AND VIDEO (3 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 ByteDance has launched Seedance 2.0, an advanced AI video model that
can generate 15-second clips with audio from text, images, and videos
while accounting for camera movement and visual effects. The model
represents ByteDance's entry into the competitive AI video generation
market, joining Google's Veo 3, OpenAI's Sora 2, and Runway's latest

the Doubao AI assistant, the tool has already sparked concerns about
copyright infringement and the future of the creative industries.

 HOW DESIGN SYSTEMS OFFER CREATIVE SAFETY FOR PRODUCT TEAMS (8 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 Design systems offer "creative safety" by providing clear definitions
that distinguish between healthy cohesion (when similar things look
the same) and unhealthy sameness (when different things look the
same). Clear terminology helps product teams make better decisions
about when to standardize versus when to innovate, leading to more
productive critiques and accountability. This clarity reduces reliance
on institutional knowledge and enables design system teams to act as
enablers rather than gatekeepers.

 HOW ADOBE LOST THE WEB AND UX DESIGN MARKET (6 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Adobe lost its dominance in web and UX design by discontinuing
popular tools like Fireworks and forcing designers to use inadequate
alternatives like Photoshop. While competitors like Sketch and Figma
gained market share through better-suited tools and collaboration
features, Adobe's XD failed to compete and is now in maintenance mode.
Adobe now faces new threats from Canva's acquisition of Serif and the
free release of professional Affinity tools, challenging Adobe's
expensive subscription model.

 THE PRD ISN'T DEAD, IT'S EVOLVING FOR THE AI ERA (7 MINUTE READ) [12]


 The PRD (Product Requirements Document) has evolved from
waterfall-era 20-30 page instruction manuals in the 90s-2000s, to
agile user stories in the 2010s, to living collaboration hubs during
the pandemic. While AI can now generate PRDs quickly, it lacks a deep
understanding of customers, product history, and business context,
making the real competitive advantage knowing what's worth building
rather than just building fast. As AI compresses the entire product
development lifecycle, the advantage moves upstream to choosing the
right problems to solve and maintaining team alignment.
