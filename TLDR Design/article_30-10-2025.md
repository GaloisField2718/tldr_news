 WHY STEALING DESIGN WORKS (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Perplexity's AI browser Comet succeeded by deliberately copying
Chrome's familiar interface rather than innovating with radical new
designs like Arc and Dia, which struggled with user adoption. The
strategy follows Jakob's Law (users prefer products that work like
ones they already know), allowing people to switch browsers without
learning new interactions while quietly integrating AI features.
Instead of pursuing original design for novelty's sake, Comet
prioritized invisible ease and useful functionality, proving that
copying familiar patterns and then innovating subtly often beats
flashy redesigns that create friction.

 TACKLING COMMON UX HURDLES WITH AI (2 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 AI capabilities enable solutions to longstanding UX problems by
leveraging models' strengths rather than just adding chat interfaces
to existing applications. Instead of empty states and tutorials, AI
can generate working content that immediately engages users and
supports learning through editing. Natural language understanding
enables conversational search to replace complex filtering systems,
while AI's ability to process unstructured inputs, such as images or
PDFs, eliminates traditional form-filling requirements by
automatically extracting and structuring data. 

 DESIGNING SOFTWARE FOR THINGS THAT ROT (10 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Fermento is a fermentation-tracking app that applies HACCP (Hazard
Analysis and Critical Control Points) food safety frameworks to home
fermentation projects, treating the process as monitored
infrastructure rather than simple cooking. The app uses phase-based
state machines to evaluate measurements such as pH and humidity
against context-specific constraints, automatically flagging
deviations and generating compliance documentation for high-risk
ferments like cured meats while remaining useful as a simple diary for
forgiving projects like sauerkraut. Unlike typical software optimized
for uptime and consistency, the system is designed around controlled
drift and variance, tracking trends over time to help fermenters
distinguish between harmless fluctuations and dangerous deviations.
