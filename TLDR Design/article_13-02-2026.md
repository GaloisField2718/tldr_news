 USEFUL GUIDELINES AND CALCULATORS FOR UX RESEARCH (11 MINUTE READ)

 UX research should challenge assumptions and reduce risk rather than
validate pre-made decisions. Start with small sample sizes (5

 DESIGN IS DEAD, IT'S ALL EVOLUTION NOW (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Traditional product design with intentional structure and hierarchy
has been replaced by evolutionary development, where teams make
hundreds of random changes and keep only the successful ones.
Instagram exemplifies this shift, evolving from a simple photo
timeline into a chaotic mix of features like Stories, Reels, and IGTV
that lack logical organization. This approach eliminates the need for
human designers since the process mirrors natural evolution, where
variants are generated variants and survivors are selected without
conscious planning.

 HOW AI LITERACY SHAPES GENAI SSE (13 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 AI literacy involves two distinct skills: prompt fluency (the ability
to communicate effectively with AI) and output literacy (the ability
to evaluate and verify AI responses). Research identified four user
types based on these skills: AI novices (low in both), naive power
users (good at prompting but poor at evaluation), skeptical abstainers
(good at evaluation but avoid using AI), and AI experts (strong in
both areas). Understanding these different literacy levels is crucial
for designing inclusive AI experiences that support users across all
skill levels.

 FIGMA VARIABLES STILL DON'T UNDERSTAND SYSTEMS (7 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Figma variables are good at storing values, but fail to show how
those values relate to each other or are derived from base values.
Most design systems use a few base values that are modified across
different states, but Figma variables don't support this composition
model. This post proposes a lightweight CMS-like system within Figma
that would allow variables to reference shared structures and
automatically generate predictable token sets with proper error
handling.
