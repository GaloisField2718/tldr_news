 THE ELEVENLABS AI MUSIC GENERATOR TURNS YOUR IDEAS INTO 3-MINUTE
SONGS (2 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 ElevenMusic is an iOS app that generates 3-minute songs from text
prompts, allowing users to specify mood, length, and lyrical content.
The app offers seven free songs daily, with a $9.99/month Pro
subscription providing up to 500 tracks per month and additional
features. ElevenMusic competes with Google's Lyria 3 Pro and
established platforms like Suno and Udio, leveraging ElevenLabs' voice
AI expertise as its competitive advantage. 

 IPHONE FOLD DIMENSIONS: HERE'S HOW THE FOLDABLE IPHONE SIZES UP NEXT
TO THE IPHONE 18 PRO (2 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Leaked dummy units shared by Sonny Dickson suggest Apple's foldable
iPhone will be wider and shorter than Pro Max models when closed, but
unfold into a ~7.8-inch display with a usable area closer to an iPad
mini, emphasizing a better landscape experience despite a potentially
awkward closed form. It's expected to feature an almost invisible
crease, use Touch ID instead of Face ID due to space constraints, and
be under 5 mm thick when unfolded but slightly thicker than current
Pro models when folded. 

 “JUSTICE FOR SERIFS!”: MARVEL FANS ROAST NEW WONDER MAN LOGO (1
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Marvel's Wonder Man series has been warmly received overall, but its
newly revealed minimalist logo has sparked backlash from fans who see
it as a significant downgrade from the original serif design. The
updated version, which features a gray gradient impact-style font, is
widely criticized as bland, sterile, and lacking the personality that
helped define other Marvel series branding. While minimalist logos can
be effective, many fans feel this redesign strips away the charm and
flair seen in shows like WandaVision and The Punisher, with some even
calling for a return to the older, more expressive serif style.

 THE SELF-HEALING DESIGN SYSTEM (8 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A design system architect built a self-healing AI infrastructure
using Claude Code as an orchestration layer, connecting six
specialized agents to Figma, GitHub, Storybook, and other tools via
MCP. The system automatically detects drift, scores violations,
generates fix PRs, and updates documentation — turning every human
correction into a learning signal that sharpens future suggestions.
Critically, full autonomy is never the goal: a trust-level matrix
ensures high-risk changes always require human review, since agents,
unlike human experts, don't explain their failures or take ownership
of mistakes. 

 VIBE CODING IS ACCELERATING THE EROSION OF DESIGN AUTHORITY (6 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Vibe coding tools like Google Stitch generate interfaces rapidly but
produce executable prototypes, not production-ready systems — a
distinction the term itself obscures. The industry's cultural shift
now demands visible, interactive output before architectural decisions
are trusted, effectively replacing invisible reasoning with the
appearance of progress. Renaming the practice "vibe prototyping" would
be more honest, but the deeper fix requires designers to explicitly
separate surface demonstrations from structural decisions. 

 THE INVISIBLE LAYER OF UX MOST DESIGNERS IGNORE (10 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Most designers focus on visual polish, but screen reader users
experience interfaces as a linear stream of spoken content, meaning
usability depends on proper structure (roles, names, and states)
rather than appearance. Many accessibility issues come from missing
semantic meaning—like vague labels, misuse of components, or
reliance on visual cues. Designers can greatly improve accessibility
by thinking beyond visuals, designing with structure in mind, and
testing their work with screen readers early in the process.

 UX WRITING FOR CONVERSION: HOW MICROCOPY INCREASES FORM SUBMISSIONS
AND REDUCES FRICTION (9 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 UX writing is the discipline of crafting interface text to reduce
user friction and increase conversions, with weak microcopy being the
root cause of most form abandonment issues. Effective UX writing
focuses on six key areas: field labels, helper text, error messages,
calls-to-action, confirmation messages, and privacy notices that build
trust and guide users clearly. Strategic microcopy transforms forms
from friction points into conversion tools by eliminating user
uncertainty and hesitation at each step.

 Loss of creative passion is likely caused by burnout driven by fear

aligning your work and habits with your values instead of chasing
