 DEVELOP A DEEPER UNDERSTANDING WITH INTERACTIVE IMAGES IN GEMINI (1
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Gemini introduced interactive images that let users tap or click on
parts of diagrams to access definitions, explanations, and deeper
content about complex academic concepts. This feature transforms
studying from passive viewing into active exploration, allowing
learners to gain more information and ask follow-up questions. The
capability makes learning more visual, dynamic, and accessible by
moving beyond simple text and static images.

 THE UX SKILLS GAP AND WHY EMPLOYERS NEED DESIGN THINKERS, NOT JUST
DEVELOPERS (5 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 UK organisations face a critical UX skills gap, with teams rich in
technical talent but lacking professionals who can connect digital
systems to actual user needs through design thinking. The Design
Council reports that firms that integrate design into business
processes grow twice as fast, yet many digital transformation projects
fail due to poor user experience rather than technical shortcomings.
Apprenticeships in UX and digital design offer employers a practical
solution for developing design thinkers internally, building a
workforce capable of combining analytical skills with human-centered
problem-solving. 

 HOW TO REWILD YOUR CREATIVE PRACTICE (7 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 A writer and artist who relocated from NYC to Upstate New York
discovered that gardening transformed her creative approach and helped
combat digital burnout. Through years of cultivating her yard, she
learned six transferable lessons: embrace creative ecosystems through
collaboration, start projects with available resources, ruthlessly
edit ideas like pruning plants, experiment abundantly without knowing
the outcomes, accept slow, nonlinear progress, and celebrate completed
work. The practice of working with soil, plants, and natural cycles
rewired her into a happier person while grounding her predominantly
digital creative life.

 THIS THING IS KILLING YOUR CREATIVE MOMENTUM (3 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Designers often scatter their creative energy across multiple complex
problems in a single day, believing this multitasking reflects
productivity, but each creative challenge demands its own distinct
mental space. The most effective creative work happens when someone
commits to solving one substantial problem per day, allowing their
mind to fully enter that world and make meaningful progress rather
than hovering superficially across several tasks. This isn't about
doing less work overallâ€”smaller tasks and refinements can still
happenâ€”but about recognizing that quality creative output requires
choosing which single problem deserves full attention today.

 NEW REPORT SHOWS DESIGN AGENCIES' INCOME UP BUT PROFITS FLAT (5
MINUTE READ) [14] 

 A new report shows UK design and branding agencies stabilizing after
a difficult period, with fee income rising 5.5% (3% after inflation)
and strong growth in retail, packaging, and brand identity work.
However, profitability remains weak: margins are flat at 6.9%,
employment costs keep climbing, and both income and profit per head
have fallen. Group-owned agencies now outperform independents. The
report warns that AI is pressuring fees while demanding new
investment, pushing agencies to shift gradually toward value-based
pricing to stay competitive.
