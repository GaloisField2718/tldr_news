 COMIC-CON BANS AI ART AFTER ARTIST PUSHBACK (5 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 San Diego Comic-Con reversed its AI-friendly art show policy after
artists publicly protested, now banning AI-generated material from the
exhibition altogether. Artists Tiana Oreglia and Karla Ortiz led the
backlash, arguing the policy normalized exploitative technology that
threatens their livelihoods and diminishes creative spaces. The
convention changed its rules within 24 hours, shifting from allowing
non-sale AI art with disclosure to prohibiting any AI-created work.

 DESIGNERS OFTEN DO INVISIBLE WORK THAT MATTERS. HERE'S HOW TO SHOW IT

 WHY MULTIMODAL UX IS BECOMING THE NEW STANDARD FOR DIGITAL PRODUCTS
(4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Multimodal UX enables users to interact with digital products through
multiple modes like touch, voice, gesture, and vision, working
together naturally, reflecting how people actually behave in
real-world contexts. This approach improves accessibility for over 1
billion people with disabilities and situational limitations while
increasing user satisfaction through choice and control. Successful
implementation requires harmonious design patterns, contextual testing
in messy environments, and careful restraint as AR, VR, and AI-powered
interfaces become mainstream.
