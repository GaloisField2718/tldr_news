 APPLE REFINES LIQUID GLASS DESIGN AND EXPANDS CHILD SAFETY TOOLS AT
WWDC (3 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Apple refined its Liquid Glass design language at WWDC 2026 with
improved readability, contrast, and user controls for transparency
levels. The company announced iOS 27 performance improvements,
including faster app launches, photo loading, and network transitions.
Apple significantly expanded its child safety tools with enhanced
parental controls for managing children's device usage, screen time,
and communication access. 

 AUDI UNVEILS SECRET SUPERCAR, NEW DESIGN DIRECTION (2 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Audi's Chief Creative Officer, Massimo Frascella, has unveiled the
Nuvolari supercar, representing the brand's new "Radical Next" design
direction focused on radical simplicity. The hybrid vehicle combines a
4-liter twin-turbo V8 with three electric motors to produce nearly
1,000 horsepower. Only 499 units will be produced at over $650,000
each. 

 APPLE'S PHOTOS APP IS GETTING THREE NEW AI-POWERED EDITING TOOLS (3
MINUTE READ) [6] 

 iOS 27 introduces three AI-powered photo editing upgrades in the
Photos app: a more capable Cleanup tool for removing unwanted objects,
Extend for expanding a photo beyond its original frame using
generative AI, and Reframe, which uses spatial data and AI to
reposition subjects and adjust composition after the photo is taken.
All three features are powered by Apple Intelligence and are expected
to arrive later this year.

 DIGITAL SKILLS AND TECH TRENDS: WHAT DESIGNERS MUST MASTER NOW (6
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Creative professionals must now blend traditional design skills with
technical and AI expertise, as 69% of marketing leaders say AI is
reshaping — not replacing — the skills their teams need. The four
most in-demand design roles are UX design, product design, front-end
development, and visual design. Beyond hard skills, cross-functional
collaboration and clear digital communication are what ultimately land
the job, since technical ability draws attention but interpersonal
skills close the deal. 

 WCAG COMPLIANCE LEVELS EXPLAINED: A, AA, AND AAA (8 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 WCAG defines three conformance levels — A, AA, and AAA — each
building on the previous, with Level AA being the standard most
organizations are expected to meet. Although WCAG itself is not a law,
Level AA is widely treated as the legal benchmark, with US ADA
enforcement and EU directives commonly referencing WCAG 2.1 or 2.2 at
that level. Level AAA, the most rigorous tier, is generally treated as
an aspirational target applied selectively rather than a universal
requirement. 

 THIS YEAR'S WWDC REVEALED SOMETHING SURPRISING ABOUT APPLE'S FUTURE
(3 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 At WWDC, Apple announced that the long-delayed AI-powered Siri, now
called Siri AI, will launch in September with its own dedicated app,
fulfilling promises first made two years ago. The keynote also marked
a major shift in presentation style, with Apple discussing software
updates across all devices together rather than separating them by
platform. The change led some viewers to feel confused about
platform-specific updates, but it may reflect Apple's growing focus on
a unified software ecosystem. By treating iPhone, iPad, Mac, and other
devices as parts of a single experience, Apple appears to be moving
toward a more integrated vision for its operating systems.

 HOW TO USE CULTURAL PROBES TO UNDERSTAND YOUR USERS (11 MINUTE READ)
[13] 

 Cultural probes are research tools that help designers understand
users when direct observation isn't possible or when deeper insights
are needed beyond what web conferencing and video recording can
provide. They allow researchers to dig deeper into problem domains and
contexts of use that technological solutions cannot fully capture.
Cultural probes serve as an alternative method to gain detailed
understanding of users and improve design decisions. 

 TOP UX DESIGN TRENDS: HOW USER EXPERIENCE DESIGN IS EVOLVING IN 2026
(6 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 UX design is evolving toward more intentional, personal approaches
that prioritize user-centered experiences over generic layouts. Key
trends include "slow browsing" that reduces stimulation and gives
products more breathing room, plus high-performance interfaces where
seamless micro-interactions signal brand quality. Brands are moving
away from homogeneous designs toward more textured, analog-inspired
interfaces that feel hyper-unique and immersive. 

 WEDGE DESIGNS STONE&SKILLET'S IDENTITY AROUND THE PAN IT'S NAMED
AFTER (4 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Stone&Skillet worked with Wedge to refresh its brand identity as it
expanded into thousands of stores. The redesign centers on a
simplified skillet symbol, bolder typography, richer colors, and more
distinctive photography, creating a stronger shelf presence while
preserving the brand's warmth and craftsmanship. The new system is
designed to be flexible and scalable, helping the brand stand out in a
crowded category and supporting future product growth.
