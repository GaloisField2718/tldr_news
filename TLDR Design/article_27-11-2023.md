 DESIGN SPRINTS ARE NOT ENOUGH, PREPARE FOR A UX MARATHON (10 MINUTE
READ) [9] 

 By embracing a UX marathon mindset, designers and product specialists
can reshape the narrative, driving UX from the periphery to the core
of business decisions. Such companies believe that product design does
not end after the development and launch of the digital service
application but that the product must be constantly improved and
updated based on user feedback. 

 MOVE FAST, BREAK PEOPLE? (6 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Creating negative urgency in design risks compromising the overall
user experience, potentially losing users and damaging the company's
reputation irreparably. In organizations, ultimately this could lead
to staff retention issues and even compromised employee brand
reputation.

MISCELLANEOUS

 SYSTEM USABILITY SCALE FOR DATA-DRIVEN UX (22 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The SUS, a questionnaire-based method to assess the usability of a
system or product, has become an indispensable component in the realm
of UX, shedding light on the user-friendliness and overall quality of
digital platforms. It enhances user-centered evaluation, provides
quantitative measurement, offers speed and simplicity, facilitates
iterative design, supports decision-making, promotes consistency,
encourages user feedback, and aids in resource allocation. 

 BRAILLE VS. BOSTON LINE TYPE: HOW DESIGN CAN TRULY BE INCLUSIVE (5
MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Louis Braille created his namesake reading system for the visually
impaired in 1824. Samuel Gridley Howe developed the Boston Line Type
in 1835 following a life-changing accident that blinded him. Based on
the Roman alphabet, the Boston Line Type proved too complex to use
effectively, leading to over a century‚Äôs delay in the adoption of
the more user-friendly Braille system in the US. 

 ACCESS REPORT: THE STATE OF PRODUCT ONBOARDING (10 MINUTE READ) [16] 

 In this interview, ex-Miro Growth leaders Kate Syuma and Viktoria
Kharlamova discuss their comprehensive Onboarding Report for 2023.
Their research deeply explores what makes onboarding empowered, how
different strategies work for different types of businesses (B2B and
B2C), and what we can learn from successful and unsuccessful
onboarding efforts.
