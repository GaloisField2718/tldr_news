NEWS & TRENDS

 THE FIGMA DESIGN AGENT IS HERE (8 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Figma launched a native design agent that lives directly on the
canvas, giving teams deep access to their components, tokens, and
design system without switching tools. It supports parallel prompting,
bulk edits, and feedback processing — handling repetitive tasks
while keeping designers in control of the craft. Currently rolling out
in beta at no credit cost, it will be available to Full seat users on
Professional, Organization, and Enterprise plans upon general
availability. 

 GOOGLE POMELLI CAN NOW BUILD YOUR ENTIRE BRAND FROM SCRATCH (2 MINUTE
READ) [5] 

 Google Pomelli, an AI marketing tool from Google Labs built with
DeepMind, now lets small businesses build a complete brand identity
from scratch through a new agentic feature. Beyond its existing social
media and ad creation capabilities, the update introduces brand books
and a one-click website builder. Together, these tools allow
businesses with no online presence to establish their brand, run
social campaigns, and launch a professional site without expensive
outside help. 

 CANVA AND ADOBE ARE COMING TO GEMINI, AND THEY WANT TO MAKE
EVERYTHING CHATTY (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Both Adobe and Canva are integrating with Google Gemini, allowing
creative work to begin via chat prompts before users open any design
app. Canva's Connected App is already rolling out in select
English-language markets, enabling users to generate, edit, and layer
designs directly from Gemini, while Adobe's connector arrives in the
coming weeks with a broader focus on image, design, and video
production through Firefly and Creative Cloud. The deeper question is
whether Google will emerge as a gatekeeper for creative decisions,
gaining influence over the first choice while both companies compete
to stay relevant once the work starts outside their own platforms.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 SMALL BY DESIGN: THE STRENGTH OF LEAN DESIGN-SYSTEM TEAMS (8 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 Small design-system teams of 2-5 people are the norm across all
company sizes and can operate more effectively than larger teams when
this is a strategic choice. These lean teams move faster because they
have shared context, fewer bottlenecks, and can make decisions quickly
without formal handoffs or extensive coordination. The small size
enables better collaboration through blurred roles, forces strategic
prioritization, and allows teams to scale their impact through
contribution models rather than headcount. 

 THE WAITING PROBLEM IN AI PRODUCTS (8 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Modern AI products and agents have largely ignored decades-old
usability principles showing that users lose focus when systems
respond too slowly, relying instead on vague spinners and minimal
feedback that force people to invent coping behaviors like repeatedly
checking tabs, refreshing pages, or monitoring long-running tasks
themselves. AI tools should adopt the same progress indicators, ETAs,
notifications, and detailed logs that operating systems and
professional software have used for years to make long waits feel
manageable and transparent. 

 MOST AI TOOLS MAKE USERS FASTER. THE BEST AI TOOLS MAKE USERS BETTER
(11 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 AI tools are increasingly taking over complex tasks, but the real
challenge isn't making AI flawless — it's designing systems that
keep users engaged, capable, and able to catch mistakes when the AI
fails. Drawing on decades of research in automation and cognitive
ergonomics, good human–AI collaboration requires balancing
automation with human control, carefully calibrating user trust, and
designing experiences that strengthen rather than erode people's
long-term skills and judgment. Instead of training users to blindly
accept outputs or repeatedly “pull the slot machine lever” with
prompts like “fix this,” the best AI systems should help users
stay thoughtful, informed, and actively involved in decision-making.

MISCELLANEOUS

 DESIGN ENGINEERING THEN VS. NOW: HOW AI IS RESHAPING THE
DESIGNER-DEVELOPER DIVIDE (9 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI is reshaping design engineering by introducing visual editors that
allow direct manipulation of web elements through drag-and-drop
interfaces and conversational prompts. Design engineering has evolved
from a bridge role during design-to-developer handoffs to an execution
role where professionals can both design and code. Tools like Cursor's
visual editor exemplify this shift by enabling users to "click on a
thing, talk to it, and iterate," bringing design and engineering
closer together. 

 HOW KIT STUDIO DREW ON ST. JOHN'S WALLS FOR AN ANTI-BLAND REBRAND (10
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Modern branding has become increasingly bland and simplified due to
digital optimization, often stripping historic identities of texture
and personality. In rebranding St. John's College, Kit Studio pushed
back against that trend by drawing from the college's real
architecture, archives, colors, and craftsmanship, creating detailed
hand-drawn designs that preserve warmth and heritage while still
working across modern digital platforms. 

 CANVA USES HANDCRAFTED ANIMATION TO CHALLENGE POLISHED DESIGN CULTURE
(4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Canva has launched a handcrafted stop-motion ad featuring a pigeon
creatively rebranding an Italian trattoria to promote the idea that
design should feel accessible rather than intimidating. Created with
tactile handmade animation by DUDE Milan and former Aardman Studios
animator Dan Richards, the campaign deliberately embraces imperfect,
human-made visuals as a contrast to increasingly AI-driven creative
tools — despite Canva itself heavily investing in AI features.
