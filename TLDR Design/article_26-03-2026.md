NEWS & TRENDS

 ADS IN APPLE MAPS ARE NOW OFFICIAL, AND THEY'RE COMING THIS SUMMER (2
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Apple has officially announced that ads are coming to Apple Maps in
the US and Canada starting this summer, ending one of its key
advantages over competitors like Google Maps. The ads will appear as
paid listings in search results and suggested places, clearly labeled
and based on relevance rather than just payment amount. Apple claims
the ads will maintain user privacy by keeping data on-device and not
tying it to Apple IDs, though this marks a significant shift toward
monetizing their services. 

 DESIGN WITH AGENTS ON THE FIGMA CANVAS, NOW IN BETA (1 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Figma has launched a beta feature that allows AI agents to create and
modify design assets directly on the Figma canvas using existing
components, variables, and tokens. The AI agents first read design
libraries to ensure their outputs reflect the established design
system. This feature is available across platforms, including Claude,
VS Code, Cursor, and others, with customizable skills that can be
authored by anyone familiar with Figma. 

 APPLE RELEASES IOS 26.4 WITH LONG-AWAITED KEYBOARD FIX (6 MINUTE
READ) [6] 

 iOS 26.4 is a refinement-focused update that improves everyday iPhone
use, led by a long-awaited keyboard accuracy fix that reduces mistypes
and autocorrect issues. It also enhances Apple Music with AI-generated
playlists and concert discovery, adds offline song recognition via
Shazam, introduces video playback in Apple Podcasts, expands
accessibility options, enables individual payments in Family Sharing,
and allows voice AI apps in CarPlay. Alongside small additions like
new emoji, the update also patches 34 security vulnerabilities, making
it a worthwhile install despite lacking major changes like a revamped
Siri.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 THE METAVERSE DIED, BUT SPATIAL DESIGN DIDN'T (WHAT DESIGNERS CAN
TAKE AWAY) (4 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Meta shut down Horizon Worlds VR after investing $80 billion, marking
the death of the traditional "metaverse" vision that nobody actually
wanted. However, spatial design principles remain valuable as the
industry shifts toward AR glasses, Apple's Vision Pro, and spatial
computing applications that enhance reality rather than replace it.
Designers should focus on spatial thinking for lightweight AR
experiences and tools that enhance real-world activities rather than
creating escapist virtual worlds. 

 WHAT HAPPENS TO THE DESIGN SYSTEM WHEN AI CHANGES THE PRODUCT? (9
MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI is reshaping design systems by shifting users from step-by-step
“operators” to decision-makers who review and act on AI-generated
suggestions, making traditional components like forms and linear
workflows less central. While foundational elements (like typography
and accessibility) remain stable, design systems must evolve to
support new interaction patterns such as suggestions, mixed AI/manual
control, uncertainty handling, and trust repair. In the long term,
they may move away from fixed component libraries toward flexible
rules or constraints, with consistency defined more by user
understanding than identical interfaces. 

 AR GLASSES ARE HERE, BUT WHAT ABOUT ACCESSIBILITY? (7 MINUTE READ)
[9] 

 New AR glasses from companies like Meta and XREAL are becoming more
practical, but their growth highlights the need to design
accessibility from the start rather than as an add-on. While current
features like speech-to-text and tools such as Be My Eyes already
help, AR's real potential lies in universal, multisensory
design—using vision, sound, and touch together to improve
understanding and usability. This approach can make AR experiences
more intuitive, inclusive, and immersive for everyone, not just users
with disabilities.

 DESIGNING BEYOND THE CHECKLIST (3 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 The Firefox UX team emphasizes that accessibility should go beyond
checklists and compliance requirements to become a fundamental design
mindset. Through user research with the Fable platform and direct
connections with people using assistive technologies, the team
discovered crucial insights into magnification software, voice
control, and other accessibility needs that traditional audits miss.
This approach has transformed their design process, ensuring
accessibility is considered from the beginning rather than as a final
review step, aligning with Mozilla's mission to make the internet open
and usable for everyone. 

 ROCKET LEAGUE'S ‘NEW' LOGO IS ONE BIG GAME OF SPOT THE DIFFERENCE
(2 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The esports team Ninjas in Pyjamas drew mixed reactions after
unveiling a “new” logo for Rocket League that appears almost
indistinguishable from the original design. While logo redesigns
usually introduce noticeable changes, this update was so subtle that
fans debated whether anything had changed at all, turning the
announcement into an ironic and somewhat humorous moment. The
situation highlights both the expectations around branding updates and
how even minor—or seemingly nonexistent—tweaks can spark strong
community discussion. 

 UNTOUCHED SINCE THE 1980S, JORDAN'S FURNITURE GETS A SLEEK REBRAND
WITH ERGONOMIC ICONS (4 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Jordan's Furniture has undergone its first major visual refresh in
over 30 years, led by Allie Hughes and TBWA\Chiat\Day, aiming to
modernize the brand while preserving its personality and strong
community identity. The redesign centers on a more human, playful
“J” symbol with a signature “wink,” a simplified wordmark that
drops “furniture,” and a system of icons derived from the logo,
while retaining a vibrant color palette inspired by its classic look.
Updated typography and visual elements strike a balance between fresh
and familiar, resulting in a brand that feels more contemporary,
approachable, and flexible beyond traditional furniture.
