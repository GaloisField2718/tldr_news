 YOUTUBE IS GIVING SOME CHANNELS A NETFLIX MAKEOVER (4 MINUTE READ)
[4] 

 YouTube is experimenting with a Netflix-style interface makeover for
select channels through its new Shows feature. The redesign features
supersized thumbnails, episode descriptions, "Watch Now" buttons, and
scheduled content shelves that resemble streaming services. The UX
aims to help creators with connected content appear more like the
programming traditional TV networks produce. 

 APPLE WATCH ULTRA 3'S LARGER SCREEN SIZE LEAKS IN NEW IOS 26 BETA (2
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 The upcoming Apple Watch Ultra 3 is expected to feature a slightly
larger display with a new 422 x 514 resolution, as revealed in the iOS
26 beta, suggesting either a bigger case or slimmer bezels. Rumored
upgrades include satellite messaging, high blood pressure detection, a
new LTPO3 OLED display, 5G support, and a next-gen S10 or S11 chip. It
will likely be unveiled at Apple's September 9 event. 

 APPLE'S LATEST LIQUID GLASS DESIGN SHOWS IT WON'T BACK DOWN WITH IOS
26 (3 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 iOS 26's standout feature is the new Liquid Glass design. Despite
some early backlash during the beta cycle, Apple has doubled down on
its original vision, refining and expanding it with each release. With
the latest beta showing minimal changes and Apple's typical pattern of
locking in final designs by August, Liquid Glass is all but confirmed
to ship in next month's official release, with potential refinements
likely to follow in later updates.

 WHAT I LOOK FOR IN DESIGN PORTFOLIOS (AND WHAT MOST PEOPLE MISS) (8
MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Design portfolios stand out in today's competitive job market through
strong craft, exceptionally high-quality typography, and creative
assets that build trust before interviews begin. The medium itself
becomes the message, with thoughtful presentation proving standards
and credibility to hiring managers. Portfolios should proactively
answer common interview questions about leadership style and approach
rather than waiting to be asked.

 FIGMA CEO'S PATH FROM COLLEGE DROPOUT AND THIEL FELLOW TO TECH
BILLIONAIRE (16 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Dylan Field dropped out of Brown University over a decade ago to join
the Thiel Fellowship program and co-founded design software company
Figma. Figma's stock more than tripled in its NYSE debut this week,
making Field's stake worth approximately $6.6 billion after a failed
$20 billion Adobe acquisition. The company now focuses on AI-powered
features like Figma Make, which uses Anthropic's Claude model to
generate coded prototypes from design prompts. 

 A TOP DESIGNER WAS BANNED FROM DRIBBBLE. NOW HE'S BUILDING HIS OWN
COMPETITOR (6 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Dribbble permanently banned dozens of designers, including prominent
designer Gleb Kuznetsov, following new marketplace rules that require
revenue sharing. Kuznetsov's 210 million-follower account was deleted
after he shared contact information with clients, violating the
platform's updated terms. The San Francisco-based studio founder now
plans to launch an AI-focused competitor platform within three to four
months. 

 'THINKING DIFFERENTLY IS A SUPERPOWER': SIMON DIXON ON HOW DIXONBAXI
WAS BUILT (8 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 DixonBaxi built long-term success through patient persistence, clear
values, and bold decisions like resigning misaligned clients. Rather
than chasing trends or profit, it thrives by designing with purpose,
fostering a human-centered, collaborative culture, and actively
shaping its future through initiatives like creative sabbaticals and
inclusive innovation.
