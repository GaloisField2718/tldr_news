 X RELAUNCHES A REBUILT ANDROID APP AFTER YEAR-LONG EFFORT (2 MINUTE
READ) [4]

 X has relaunched its Android app after nearly a year of rebuilding it
from scratch to improve loading, scrolling, notifications,
reliability, and future feature development. The effort was described
as one of the largest engineering projects in the company's history
and is aimed partly at strengthening X in Android-heavy global
markets. Some features are still unfinished, including better support
for older devices, Spaces, video editing, react-with-video, cashtags,
and custom timelines.

 APPLE BRIEFLY BECAME THE WORLD'S MOST VALUABLE COMPANY, TOPPLING
NVIDIA (1 MINUTE READ) [5]

 Apple briefly overtook Nvidia as the world's most valuable company
after investors sold off AI-related semiconductor stocks, reflecting
growing skepticism about the long-term returns of massive AI
infrastructure spending. Unlike many of its rivals, Apple has spent
relatively little on building AI infrastructure and instead benefits
from its installed base of 2.5 billion devices. As a result, it is
increasingly seen as well positioned to monetize AI features,
especially after the positive reception to its revamped Siri.

 AGENTIO'S AI-POWERED CREATOR ADS ARE COMING TO FACEBOOK AND INSTAGRAM
(2 MINUTE READ) [6]

 Agentio, an AI-powered creator advertising platform previously
focused on YouTube, has partnered with Meta to bring its tools to
Facebook and Instagram. Brands can now access AI-driven insights,
creator matching, and partnership ads across both platforms, with the
option to run cross-platform campaigns spanning Meta and Google
properties. Beta testing showed Agentio's Meta partnership ads
achieved 81% higher ROAS and 89% higher click-through rates compared
to brands' non-Agentio creator ads.

 PRODUCT QUALITY: A SHARED COMMITMENT TO CRAFT IN THE WAKE OF AI (10
MINUTE READ) [7]

 As AI collapses the gap between "nothing" and "adequate," genuine
craft becomes the only remaining differentiator between products.
Craft is a shared discipline across Utility, Usability, and
Feel—owned collectively by engineers, PMs, designers, and leaders,
not design alone. "Would I recommend this to someone I care about?"
should be the standard for judging whether a product has truly earned
people's affection, not just their approval.

 WHY THE FUTURE OF DESIGN MUST BEGIN WITH UNDERSTANDING PEOPLE (6
MINUTE READ) [8]

 User experience has always extended beyond visible interfaces, shaped
instead by trust, psychology, and human context. Drawing on
architecture, emergency medical care, and design experience, the
author contends AI heightens designers' responsibility to build
informed—not blind—trust, making AI literacy an essential design
skill. Design's future depends on understanding people rather than
mastering tools, since technology changes but human needs remain
constant.

 ACCESSIBILITY ISN'T A CHECKLIST. IT'S A DESIGN DECISION YOU'RE
AVOIDING (14 MINUTE READ) [9]

 A UX designer recounts watching a blind user struggle through an
11-minute flight booking process that should have taken 90 seconds,
revealing how years of seemingly reasonable decisions had quietly
excluded users with disabilities. The experience showed that
accessibility isn't primarily a technical challenge or a compliance
checklist—it requires making it a core product priority, testing
with real assistive technology users, and treating accessibility as
part of the definition of "done" rather than something to address
later.

 AGAINST DESIGN SYSTEM FEDERATION (6 MINUTE READ) [13]

 Federated design system governance—used by just 13% of teams—is
losing favor due to poor contribution rates, diffused accountability,
and inflated cost-saving expectations. Layoffs and AI-driven
productivity narratives have fueled interest in federation, but it
typically damages system quality, user experience, and team morale
rather than fixing organizational problems. Federation works best as
one aspect within a hybrid model, not as a standalone governance
approach, since it demands more organizational maturity than most
companies achieve.

 THE “MOCKED, THEN LOVED” AIRPODS EFFECT (6 MINUTE READ) [14]

 Apple's AirPods and Apple Watch both overcame early ridicule by
proving genuinely useful in everyday life, but the rumored
camera-equipped AirPods face a different challenge: concerns about
privacy and social acceptance rather than just unconventional design.
While Apple has a strong track record of turning skepticism into
success, the new AirPods will need to demonstrate clear, hands-free AI
benefits while convincing both wearers and those around them that the
cameras can be trusted.

 ASK YOURSELF WHERE YOU CAN ADD VALUE (2 MINUTE READ) [15]

 Designers should stop undervaluing in-house work and instead showcase
the business problems they solved, the constraints they worked within,
and the measurable results they achieved. Rather than waiting to build
a perfect portfolio, improve existing projects incrementally with
stronger storytelling, updated visuals, and small enhancements, making
steady progress in short bursts that fit around a busy schedule.
