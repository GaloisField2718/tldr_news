 A SCIENTIFIC PERSPECTIVE ON MINIMAL DESIGN (10 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Minimal design aligns with cognitive limitations identified by
scientific research, including restricted short-term memory,
inattentional blindness to excessive stimuli, and the Gestalt
principle. The brain processes information primarily through
automated, subconscious mechanisms that respond better to simplified,
immediate visual communication rather than complex interfaces. While
minimal design proves highly effective for novice users by reducing
cognitive load and enabling instant comprehension, it may fall short
for expert users who require richer information density and can
process complexity within their domains.
