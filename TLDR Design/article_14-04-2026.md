 AN ARGUMENT FOR INTERIOR DESIGN WITH NEUROAESTHETICS IN MIND (4
MINUTE READ) [7] 

 A Johns Hopkins University report advocates for "Neuroarchitecture,"
a new interior design approach that incorporates psychology to
understand how light, sound, texture, and natural elements affect
mental and physical health. The study argues that spaces should be
designed with scientific evidence rather than fleeting trends,
emphasizing how environments influence creativity, healing, and
interpersonal connections. The report provides a framework to overcome
current challenges like lack of universal terminology and siloed
expertise between designers and researchers. 

 REDUCING COGNITIVE LOAD: THE SECRET BEHIND SMOOTH AND INTUITIVE UX
(10 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Reducing cognitive load is crucial for creating smooth and intuitive
website experiences that increase conversions. High cognitive load
leads to information overload, decision fatigue, and user frustration,
resulting in high abandonment rates and poor conversion rates. Simple
tactics like reducing visual clutter, prioritizing content clarity,
and aligning CTAs with customer objectives can significantly improve
user experience and brand success.

toward building genuine relationships, focusing on meaningful content,
and prioritizing quality over quantityвЂ”using platforms as a gateway
to real connections rather than constant self-promotion.

 ALL CHANGE, PLEASE вЂ“ HOW GRANADA BARRERO ESTUDIO REINVENTED
WAYFINDING FOR SEVILLE'S METRO (5 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Seville's metro has introduced a unified wayfinding system by Granada
Barrero Estudio, replacing previously inconsistent signage with a
clear, cohesive visual language based on user research and design
thinking. The redesign improves legibility, safety, and inclusivity
