 INSTAGRAM TEASES AI EDITING TOOLS THAT WILL COMPLETELY REIMAGINE YOUR
VIDEOS (2 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Meta plans to introduce a generative AI video editing feature on
Instagram in 2025. Powered by Movie Gen AI technology, the tool will
enable users to transform videos using text prompts, modify
backgrounds and appearances, and seamlessly integrate new objects.
Designed to simplify video editing for creators, early previews have
demonstrated the tool's promising capabilities, although specific
launch details remain unconfirmed. 

 APPLE IS WORKING ON A DOORBELL CAMERA WITH FACE ID (2 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Apple is developing a smart doorbell camera with Face ID technology,
enabling automatic door unlocking upon recognizing residents. Slated
for potential release by late 2025, the device will feature Apple's
Secure Enclave chip to manage Face ID data securely and is expected to
integrate with existing third-party HomeKit smart locks. 

 DESIGNING ATLASSIAN'S NEW NAVIGATION (9 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Atlassian has redesigned its navigation for six products, unifying
user experiences and streamlining development. The new sidebar
navigation enhances consistency, customization, and efficiency,
improving usability and scalability. Built with reusable components,
the design simplifies tasks across tools while addressing
accessibility and responsiveness. Iterative testing ensured a balance
between flexibility and consistency.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 FEATURES SHOULDN'T FEEL LIKE FEATURES (12 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Experience-driven design focuses on seamless, intuitive interactions,
prioritizing users' workflows over feature complexity. While features
solve tasks, experiences integrate naturally, removing friction and
reducing cognitive load. Shifting from feature factories to cohesive
design eliminates bloat and enhances usability. Understanding user
needs, addressing pain points, and testing naturalness ensure products
feel effortless, elevating user satisfaction. 

 WHAT DOES IT REALLY MEAN TO HAVE AN “EXPERIENCE”? (10 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Don Norman and Jakob Nielsen define UX as the interaction between
users and a company's offerings. At the same time, philosophical
perspectives like Gadamer's hermeneutics and Merleau-Ponty's
phenomenology highlight the more profound, embodied, and interpretive
nature of these experiences. Cases like the Tinder Swindler underscore
the real-life consequences of design, emphasizing Sjors Timmer's call
to consider past impacts when shaping future projects. 

 IS KFC'S SAUCY NEW BRAND GENIUS OR DESPERATION? (3 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 KFC's new "Saucy" store concept in Orlando, Florida, features a bold
pink design that departs from its traditional look, aiming to attract
customers with its distinct style. This 2024 trend of offshoot brands
remains under evaluation, with the store's success yet to be
determined.

MISCELLANEOUS

 PUTTING AI TO THE (ACCESSIBILITY) TEST (11 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Generative AI aids in accessibility testing, supporting tasks like
analyzing screenshots, verifying alt attributes, and matching visuals
with programmatic information. Blind testers benefit from tools like
JAWS PictureSmart, which enhances independence while reducing reliance
on sighted colleagues. However, privacy, accuracy, and manual
validation remain crucial, ensuring AI complements, rather than
replaces, human input. 

 "FRICTION IS AT THE HEART OF CREATIVITY" SAYS PANEL AT THE NEW
STANDARD TALK (4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The Dezeen-hosted panel in New York highlighted the importance of
embracing difference, vulnerability, and dialogue in design and art
collaborations. Panelists, including Universal Design Studio and New
Balance representatives, emphasized fostering creativity through
tension and open communication. They discussed risks, cultural
dynamics, and creating spaces for honest exchanges to enhance
multidisciplinary innovation. 

 COMPLETE WITH A COFFEE-SIPPING MASCOT, HAPPYCENTRO BREWS A BRAND
WORLD FOR MUI COFFEE ROASTERY (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 MUI Coffee Roastery from Kawasaki City, Japan, collaborated with
Italian design studio Happycentro to create a refreshed brand identity
featuring a custom mascot inspired by the shop owner, Mr. Osawa, and a
wordmark incorporating coffee-themed elements. The design includes
vibrant red tones, illustrated all-paper packaging, and color-coded
labels that highlight the origins and craftsmanship of each coffee.
