 GOOGLE PHOTOS ADDS SUPPORT FOR NANO BANANA-POWERED AI EDITING
FEATURES (2 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Google Photos is rolling out major updates powered by its new Nano
Banana AI model, including "Help Me Edit" for prompt-based photo
modifications and the ability to transform still images into videos or
artistic styles. The "Ask Photos" feature is available in iOS in over
100 countries, offering 17 language options. Android users in the US
and India have access to AI templates that provide ready-made editing
prompts, such as professional headshots or fashion photoshoots. A new
"Ask" button enables quick Gemini conversations about specific images.
All features will become available to users this week. 

 OPENAI LOSES MUSIC COPYRIGHT BATTLE IN GERMAN COURT (3 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Munich's Regional Court ruled that OpenAI violated German copyright
laws by having its GPT-4 and 4o models memorize and reproduce song
lyrics from nine popular German artists when prompted. The court
determined that OpenAI, not users, is responsible for the outputs, as
lyrics were reproducibly contained in the models after training,
rejecting OpenAI's claim that it doesn't store specific training data.
GEMA, a German music rights organization that represents over 100,000
creators, brought the lawsuit in 2024. 

 FIGMA BETS ON INDIA TO EXPAND BEYOND DESIGN (4 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Figma has opened a new office in Bengaluru to deepen its presence in
India, its second-largest market after the US, aiming to engage more
closely with the country's vast developer community and strengthen
local sales and marketing. The company, which now gets about half its
revenue from outside the US, sees India as key to expanding beyond
design into end-to-end product creation. Strong adoption of tools like
Figma Make and Dev Mode is already driving growth among Indian
developers and enterprises.

 CONCEPTUAL MODELS: THE HIDDEN STRUCTURE BEHIND YOUR NEXT GREAT
INTERFACE (21 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Strong conceptual models make interfaces feel intuitive by aligning
with how users think, rather than exposing system complexity, as
demonstrated by Amazon's unchanged core model over 20 years. This
article contrasts user models (simple and task-focused) with engineer
models (technical and system-focused) using examples to show how
misalignment creates confusion. Designers who bridge this gap by
aligning user mental models with interface design create products that
feel instantly clear and naturally usable.

 WHY TECH NEEDS PERSONALIZATION (4 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Mapping technologies, such as Tesla's self-driving system, Google
Maps, and Apple Maps, fail to incorporate meaningful personalization
despite having the capability to learn users' preferred routes and
driving patterns. This absence of personalization reflects a broader
tension in which algorithmic efficiency prioritizes speed over
individual preferences, reducing users to data entries and causing
them to follow machines mindlessly. True personalization would
acknowledge life's texture and make technology more human by
recognizing routines as rituals, rather than optimizing everything
into predictable boxes that sacrifice the meaningful, messy aspects of
human experience. 

 THE FIVE HABITS OF THE WORST DESIGNERS (4 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The worst designers fail to read their workplace context and
understand organizational constraints, adopting pretentious attitudes
that alienate colleagues and remaining passively isolated rather than
actively promoting their ideas. They exhibit learned helplessness by
complaining without seeking solutions, lack curiosity beyond design
dogma, and ignore how being a professional minority makes reliability
and helpfulness crucial. Design skill matters less than effectiveness,
as even average designers can add significant value when they're
influential and respected.
