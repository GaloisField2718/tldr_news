 SPOTIFY'S WEAR OS OVERHAUL BRINGS CLEANER VISUALS AND QUICKER
CONTROLS TO YOUR WRIST (1 MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Spotify has launched a major visual overhaul of its Wear OS app
featuring cleaner visuals, swipe-based navigation, and easier access
to playback controls. The redesign includes a music-first mode that
highlights album art, simplified tap-and-double-tap controls, and
quick access to key sections like Home, Search, and Your Library. The
update is rolling out via the Google Play Store over the coming days,
with more features planned for future releases. 

 DESIGN UI USING AI WITH STITCH FROM GOOGLE LABS (5 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Google Labs has evolved Stitch into an AI-native design canvas that
lets users create high-fidelity UI designs from natural-language
descriptions via "vibe design." The platform features an infinite
canvas, a design agent that reasons across entire projects, and voice
capabilities for real-time collaboration and iteration. Stitch enables
rapid prototyping by transforming static designs into interactive
prototypes instantly and integrates with other workflow tools through
its MCP server and SDK. 

 YOU CAN NOW TEACH ADOBE FIREFLY TO GENERATE IMAGES IN YOUR OWN VISUAL
STYLE (1 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Adobe has launched Firefly custom models in public beta, allowing
users to train AI on their own visual style using 10 to 30 images,
enabling consistent generation of images that match their aesthetic
across projects. The feature is available to premium subscribers and
costs 500 credits per training, with support for photography,
illustration, and character styles.

OPINIONS & TUTORIALS

 DESIGN PROCESS ISN'T DEAD, IT'S COMPRESSED (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 The recent argument to abandon the design process in favor of
intuition and rapid building misrepresents how experienced designers
actually work. What appears to be "skipping process" is actually
process compression - experienced designers internalize and adapt
traditional frameworks, moving fluidly through discovery, ideation,
and evaluation stages. Solution-first approaches only work effectively
in mature problem spaces with established patterns, not when inventing
something entirely new. 

 GENERATIVE UI: USEFUL, OR JUST CHAOS WITH BETTER BRANDING? (3 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Generative UI refers to interfaces dynamically created by AI in
response to user input or context, rather than using fixed screens and
components. While it offers benefits like personalization, reduced
friction, and context-awareness, it risks undermining user confidence
through unpredictability and breaking muscle memory. Success requires
strict adherence to guardrails, including design system constraints,
user control options, accessibility planning, and stable core
navigation patterns. 

 DESIGNERS, THERE'S NO EXCUSE FOR ACCESSIBILITY AS AN AFTERTHOUGHT (3
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Accessible design is often overlooked in graphic design due to
habits, assumptions, and late-stage implementation, but it should be a
core part of the process because designing inclusively leads to better
outcomes for everyone. Start accessibility early, consult people with
lived experience, and use simple practices—like not relying on color
alone—to make designs more effective and inclusive.

 A DESIGNER'S THOUGHTS ABOUT THIS MOMENT IN AI (9 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 The concerning shift from academic AI research to rapid
commercialization has created a reckless arms race. This trajectory is
fundamentally opposed to the creators' mission of making the world
better, as it injects powerful, unknown technology into systems
designed to harm rather than help people. Designers and creators now
face unprecedented tension between their desire to improve the world
and the morally questionable origins of the AI tools they're pressured
to use. 

 FROM SYMBOLS TO SYSTEMS: DESIGNING BRANDS THAT HOLD UNDER PRESSURE (5
MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Strong brand systems fail when they're too rigid. Effective brands
focus on a few core, non-negotiable elements (like logo, color, and
typography) while allowing flexibility to adapt quickly to culture and
new media. Resilient branding comes from balancing a stable
“anchor” with creative freedom (“volume control”),
continuously testing across contexts, and empowering teams to evolve
the system rather than strictly follow rules. 

 THE IDENTITY FOR SPATIAL FESTIVAL FEELS APTLY THREE-DIMENSIONAL (4
MINUTE READ) [14] 

 The Spatial festival at Funkhaus Berlin commissioned SMLXL and Gerard
Mallandrich to create a dynamic, motion-led identity that reflects its
immersive, experimental sound environments. By treating motion as the
core of the design and building a responsive 3D visual space, the
identity became a living, interactive extension of the festival
experience—balancing avant-garde expression with clarity and
accessibility.
