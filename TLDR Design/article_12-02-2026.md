 AUTODESK IS SUING GOOGLE OVER THE NAME OF ITS FLOW AI VIDEOMAKER (2
MINUTE READ) [4] 

 Autodesk is suing Google over trademark infringement, claiming
Google's AI video generator, Flow, will confuse customers with
Autodesk's own "Flow" brand of AI-enabled filmmaking tools launched in
2022. Autodesk alleges that after requesting that Google stop using
the name, Google misrepresented its intentions and filed trademark
applications, even though confusion between the products had already
occurred on social media. The company is seeking a court order to
block Google from using the Flow trademark and unspecified damages.

 WHY USERS GET LOST IN YOUR PRODUCT (11 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Product taxonomy directly impacts user adoption, conversion, and
growth across PLG companies. Without deliberate structure (ideally 2-3
hierarchical levels, like Product > Tool > Feature), confusion
compounds from pricing pages to analytics, leading to drops in
conversion, slower time-to-value, and misalignment across teams.
Simple, consistent classification systems enable users to learn
faster, teams to build cohesively, and growth to scale without
accumulating "clarity debt."

 LINEAR DESIGN VS. MINIMALISM, BRUTALISM, AND NEUMORPHISM (5 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Linear design emerged from decades of UX research and represents a
practical approach to minimalism, featuring monochrome color palettes,
single sans-serif fonts, and subtle animations that prioritize
usability and accessibility. Unlike brutalism, which uses extreme
contrast for bold visual impact, or neumorphism, which creates
artificial lighting effects through shadows, linear design focuses on
clear boundaries and visual affordances that enhance user experience.
Linear design's minimalist traits serve functional rather than purely
artistic purposes, making it more effective for usability than other
design trends. 

 CLAUDE CODE GUIDE FOR DESIGNERS (7 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Claude Code is a coding agent that executes plain English
instructions, enabling designers without coding backgrounds to build
and deploy functional apps in days rather than months. This guide
covers installation, basic terminal commands, project creation,
iterative prompting, version control with GitHub, and live deployment
through Vercel. Advanced steps include adding authentication,
databases, and AI features using services like Supabase to transform
static sites into full web applications.
