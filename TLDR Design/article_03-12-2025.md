 HOW TO TURN METRICS INTO ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS (6 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Design system teams often measure component adoption rates but miss
whether users can actually complete tasks, leading to situations where
high adoption masks poor user experiences. Connecting product
analytics tools like PostHog via the Model Context Protocol (MCP)
enables teams to query real user behavior data and automatically
generate contextual feedback in Figma design files. This approach
transforms weeks of manual analysis into seconds by linking
quantitative behavior metrics with design decisions. Qualitative
research remains essential for understanding why problems occur. 

 FIVE REASONS WHY WCAG AA COMPLIANCE DOES NOT MEAN YOUR WEBSITE IS
ACCESSIBLE (5 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 WCAG 2.2 AA compliance serves as a baseline rather than a guarantee
of true accessibility, since websites can technically pass all
criteria while remaining unusable for everyone. The guidelines focus
on ensuring people with impairments aren't disadvantaged compared to
others, not on creating genuinely good user experiences. Examples
include links indistinguishable from text, 1-pixel fonts with proper
contrast ratios, and incomprehensible audio with transcripts, all of
which pass WCAG AA despite being practically inaccessible to all
users.
