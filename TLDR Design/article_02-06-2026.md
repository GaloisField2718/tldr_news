 INTRODUCING A NEW DESIGN FOR MICROSOFT 365 COPILOT (6 MINUTE READ)
[4] 

 Microsoft 365 Copilot has been redesigned to move with how work
actually happens — cleaner, faster, and better connected across
apps. The updated app, which loads over 50% faster, features a
task-aware prompt workspace and a "Work IQ" layer that draws on
emails, files, chats, and meetings. Since rolling out the new in-app
experiences, Copilot usage rose 27% in Word, 33% in Excel, 43% in
PowerPoint, and 30% in Outlook. 

 AUTODESK BUYS MAINTAINX FOR $3.6BN TO PUSH FROM DESIGN INTO
OPERATIONS (2 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Autodesk is acquiring MaintainX, a maintenance and operations
platform, for $3.6 billion in an all-cash deal. Founded in 2018 and
last privately valued at $2.5 billion, MaintainX serves over 500,000
frontline workers with mobile-first maintenance software. The purchase
fills the missing "operate" layer in Autodesk's design-to-operations
strategy, while also giving it a stream of maintenance data to fuel
its AI ambitions. 

 IPHONE 18 PRO DUMMY UNITS REVEAL FOUR COLOR OPTIONS (2 MINUTE READ)
[6] 

 Leaked iPhone 18 Pro dummy units suggest Apple will offer four colors
this year: dark cherry (a deep wine-red), black, silver, and light
blue. The lineup appears to drop the popular cosmic orange option from
the iPhone 17 Pro, though the colors shown on dummy units may not
exactly match Apple's final production models since they are primarily
used by accessory manufacturers.

 DESIGNING FOR AI MEANS DESIGNING LIKE IT'S 1999 (13 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 The unsettled state of AI design mirrors the early web of 1999 —
standards still shifting, no killer app yet, business models unproven.
Chat is today's default interface but likely a placeholder, since 95%
of generative-AI pilots still deliver no measurable business impact.
Designers who thrive will treat this moment as an invitation to invent
new conventions, just as their predecessors did in that earlier era. 

 THE PART OF AGILE DESIGNERS FEAR THE MOST: IMPERFECT WORK (4 MINUTE
READ) [8] 

 Agile's promise of continuous improvement exposes designers' deepest
fear — shipping imperfect work — because teams frequently move on
without iterating on released features. The word "viable" in MVP is
often sidelined by "minimum," yet a poorly designed release yields
unreliable feedback — users reject the experience, not the idea.
True iteration — cycles of release, observation, and structural
refinement — is what makes early releases worth shipping, freeing
designers to build responsibly for the present. 

 DESIGNING HOW DESIGNERS MASTER AI (13 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 A Salesforce UX team discovered that mastering AI tools is less about
learning fixed workflows and more about developing personal ways of
working that amplify individual strengths, experiences, and thinking
styles. Through six months of peer-led learning sessions, they found
that the most valuable knowledge came not from formal training but
from colleagues sharing real-world experiments, failures, and
workflows, helping others see "a window into someone's thinking" and
build their own relationship with AI tools.

 FOUR NEW WAYS TO GO FROM IDEA TO PRODUCT WITH AI TOOLS (9 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 AI tools are reshaping how product teams at FloQast, Merkle, Affirm,
and Accor move from idea to production through four distinct
workflows. These include testing in code before designing, exploring
layout variations at scale on canvas, prototyping before writing
specs, and shipping with design system context intact. Rather than a
fixed sequence, successful teams choose their starting point based on
the question they need to answer, moving fluidly between tools. 

 EY STUDIO AVOIDED ‘GOURMET' TRENDS FOR SOMETHING MORE AUTHENTIC TO
REBRAND ITALY'S MOST ICONIC PANETTONE (3 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Italian design agency Ey Studio rebranded the iconic confectionery
brand Alemagna by drawing inspiration from Italy's post-war modernist
design era, combining handcrafted geometric typography, archival
imagery, and updated packaging to honor the brand's heritage while
making it feel contemporary. The studio deliberately avoided both
luxury and experimental design trends, aiming instead for an
authentic, accessible identity that reflects Alemagna's long-standing
connection with everyday Italian culture. 

 APPLE'S NEW SPORTS APP SHOWS US EXACTLY WHAT GOOD DATA VISUALISATION
LOOKS LIKE (4 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Apple has expanded its Apple Sports app to more than 170 countries
ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, introducing features like a
tournament bracket view, visual team formations, Live Activities, and
widgets that make following complex competitions easier. The app is a
strong example of effective information design. It uses clear
hierarchy, visual cues, and simplicity to present large amounts of
real-time data without overwhelming users.
