 DESIGNING SOFTWARE FOR AI AGENTS (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 AI agents increasingly handle computing tasks but struggle with
software designed for humans, creating opportunities to redesign
systems for machine use. Web search APIs limit results and provide
condensed snippets suited for human scanning, while agents could
benefit from more comprehensive results and larger document summaries.
AgentDB exemplifies agent-first design by utilizing UUIDs for instant
database creation, storing databases as files rather than hosting them
as services, and integrating with MCP servers to make data easily
accessible as AI model context. 

 AI TOOLS + AI FLUENCY + HUMAN ADVANTAGE = AI-NATIVE DESIGNER (8
MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Product designers can thrive in the AI era by mastering three key
areas: leveraging AI tools for faster shipping, developing AI fluency
for designing probabilistic systems, and harnessing human advantages
such as craft and creative direction. While AI handles routine tasks
and generation, designers must evolve into orchestrators who provide
vision, set constraints, and make high-level decisions that guide AI
outputs. The future belongs to AI-native designers who combine
technical literacy with uniquely human skills like strategic thinking,
storytelling, and the ability to create memorable experiences that
transcend generic AI outputs.

 HOW SEMANTICS AND ARIA ATTRIBUTES SUPPORT ACCESSIBLE DESIGN (5 MINUTE
READ) [14] 

 Accessibility issues stem from missing semantic meaning rather than
visual problems, as elements may look correct but fail to communicate
their purpose to assistive technologies. Proper semantics using HTML
elements, consistent ARIA design patterns, and appropriate attributes
work together to create interfaces that screen readers and keyboard
users can navigate effectively. Comprehensive accessibility audits
must test beyond visual appearance to ensure semantic roles match user
expectations and that interactive elements behave predictably for all
users. 

 DESIGN TOKENS FOR LINKS (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Design systems face an architectural challenge when organizing
hyperlinks within token structures, as links occupy a unique position
between atomic elements that can exist independently or integrate into
complex components. Two approaches emerge: treating links as semantic
tokens (reusing text category tokens for flexibility and reusability)
versus specific tokens (creating dedicated link namespaces for clarity
and explicit purpose). The choice depends on system complexity, with
semantic tokens being better suited for scalable systems that support
multiple themes, while specific tokens work well for simpler systems
with clearly differentiated link behaviors.
