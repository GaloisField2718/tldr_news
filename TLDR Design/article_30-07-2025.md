 A QUICK PRODUCT SIMPLICITY TEST: REMOVE ALL THE EXPLAINERS (2 MINUTE
READ) [7] 

 A product simplicity test involves removing all explainers and seeing
if users can still navigate intuitively. The New User Experience (NUX)
often serves as a crutch rather than addressing underlying design
clarity issues. This mental exercise of eliminating explanatory
content makes products more usable even when users skip through
tutorials. 

 INTERFACES THAT AUGMENT OR REPLACE? (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Complementary interfaces strengthen cognitive abilities. Competitive
interfaces replace functions without revealing their logic. Recent MIT
research suggests frequent ChatGPT use can impair memory, original
thinking, and idea generation over time. Interface designers should
create tools that help users grow rather than just complete tasks
efficiently.

 HOW FORMATTING TEXT IN WEB DESIGN INCREASES CONVERSIONS (6 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Strategic text formatting techniques like bold emphasis and short
sentences can significantly boost website conversion rates. Making
content scannable through subheadings, bullet points, and proper
whitespace helps users quickly find key information. Adding
high-quality images, videos, and interactive elements further enhances
user engagement and drives conversions. 

 IMPLEMENTING ACCESSIBLE SVG ELEMENTS (8 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 SVGs offer superior accessibility compared to bitmap images through
infinite scalability and support for semantic elements and ARIA
attributes. Successful implementation requires distinguishing between
decorative SVGs (hidden with aria-hidden) and informative ones needing
proper accessible markup. Best practices include ensuring sufficient
contrast, keyboard accessibility for interactive elements, and
thorough testing with real assistive technologies. 

 ATHLETICS LAYERS PLAYFUL TACTILE ELEMENTS ONTO MOMA'S BRAND TO
WELCOME YOUNG MUSEUM VISITORS (3 MINUTE READ) [15] 

 Athletics developed a layered design system for MoMA that makes the
museum experience more engaging and less intimidating for students by
blending MoMA's clean identity with playful elements like doodles,
hand-drawn stickers, and conversational text. Informed by
on-the-ground research, the adaptable system meets diverse student
needs and includes practical tools like editable templates and
brochures, earning praise from MoMA's internal team for its
flexibility and ease of use.
