 NVIDIA SEEKS TO MAKE HUMANOID AI ROBOTS SAFER AROUND HUMANS (3 MINUTE
READ) [5] 

 Nvidia has created a lab where robot makers and customers can run
safety tests before going to regulators to receive the necessary
certifications. Engineers at the lab can help with pre-inspection work
and engineering tweaks as needed. Robot safety design is much more
complicated than safety design for autonomous vehicles, which
generally just involves avoiding contact with people and other
objects. Robots need to be able to know what things they can or can't
touch, move, or exert force on. 

 INSTAGRAM LOOKS TO TAKE ON STREAMING SERVICES WITH LONGER-FORM,
EPISODIC, AND LIVE FORMATS FOR ITS TV APP (2 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Instagram is experimenting with longer-form content, episodic series,
and Live TV. It is testing a Series feature for Reels designed to make
it easier to keep up with serialized content. Instagram's TV app is
being rolled out to Samsung TVs. The app has received several new
features, including channels, the ability to cast from a mobile
device, and support for horizontal videos and stories.

 SPACEX IS LAUNCHING A SECRET SPACECRAFT THAT COULD CHANGE HOW THINGS
ARE MADE IN SPACE (3 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 SpaceX's Starfall is a reentry capsule that is capable of returning
up to 2,200 pounds of payload from orbit. Its disk-shaped geometry
maximizes structural efficiency and payload volume relative to mass.
Starfall is designed to be mass-produced and launched on either Falcon
9 or Starship. SpaceX plans to launch the first flight of Starfall
today from Space Launch Complex 40.

 THE OPTIMAL AMOUNT OF SLOP IS NON-ZERO (19 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Software requires different levels of verification depending on its
use case. Casual software has limited distribution and loose quality
constraints. Business software needs to work, or organizations suffer
losses. Acute software is mission-critical and merits the highest
levels of scrutiny. The amount of permissible AI-slop decreases as the
risks of the software failing increase. 

 YOU SHOULD USE AI FOR REVIEWING CODE ESPECIALLY WHEN THE DIFF IS HUGE
(2 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 It's a mistake to use AI to nitpick a few lines of code here and
there. LLMs now catch high-severity vulnerabilities, so individual
line reviews aren't needed. A human code reviewer's role is now to
transfer knowledge to the AI that it doesn't know. This workflow is
best in places where each line of code isn't sacred.
