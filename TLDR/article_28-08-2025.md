 FULLY FUNCTIONING HUMAN SKIN GROWN IN LAB, COMPLETE WITH VESSELS AND
PIGMENTATION (3 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Researchers from the University of Queensland have successfully grown
fully functional human skin in a laboratory. They used stem cells to
create a replica of human skin with blood vessels, capillaries, hair
follicles, multiple layers of tissue, and immune cells. The skin model
took six years to develop. It will be transformative for skin graft
transplants, wound healing, and the study of skin disorders.
