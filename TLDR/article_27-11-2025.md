 CHINA LAUNCHES AN EMERGENCY LIFEBOAT TO BRING THREE ASTRONAUTS BACK
TO EARTH (6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 On November 4, astronauts on the Tiangong space station discovered
that one of the two docked ferry ships had a damaged window. The
discovery was made during a crew handover, so it left three astronauts
on the Tiangong station without a safe return craft. China
successfully launched an unpiloted spacecraft late Monday to the space
station to provide a lifeboat for the astronauts. The astronauts will
return next year, soon after the arrival of their replacements. 

 A CELL SO MINIMAL THAT IT CHALLENGES DEFINITIONS OF LIFE (15 MINUTE
READ) [9] 

 Scientists have discovered a single-celled organism with a
fantastically small genome. The cell is so stripped of essential
functions that it challenges biologists' definitions of life. Its lost
genes include those central to cell metabolism - the cell can neither
process nutrients nor grow its own. The cell appears to be a parasite
entirely dependent on a host or cellular community.

MISCELLANEOUS

 MIT STUDY FINDS AI CAN ALREADY REPLACE 11.7% OF US WORKFORCE (6
MINUTE READ) [12] 

 MIT has released a study that shows that AI can already replace 11.7%
of the US labor market across finance, health care, and professional
services. The study used a labor simulation tool called the Iceberg
Index to simulate how workers interact across the country and how they
are affected by AI and corresponding policy. The index offers a
detailed map of where disruption is forming, down to the zip code. It
is designed to provide a skills-centered snapshot of what today's AI
systems can do to give policymakers a structured way to explore
scenarios before they commit real money and legislation. 

 THE UNTOLD STORY OF CHARLIE MUNGER'S FINAL YEARS (14 MINUTE READ)
[13] 

 Charlie Munger died two years ago in his longtime home in Los
Angeles, close to people he liked and projects he found stimulating.
Rather than a quiet life by the sea, Munger spent his final years
chasing investments, forging friendships, and facing new challenges.
In the year before his death, he made over $50 million on a bet on
coal stocks, revved up his real-estate activities, and dealt with
health issues. His final years provide a blueprint for investors on
how to age with grace, equanimity, and purpose.
