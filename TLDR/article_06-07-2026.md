 AGENTIC AUTONOMY LEVELS (19 MINUTE READ) [9] 

 Low autonomy limits risk and increases reversibility. Higher autonomy
is better for explicit activities, as well as for fleets of parallel
agents refactoring massive codebases. The edge of the frontier is the
manager agent that delegates to helpers while continuously verifying
their output, and only returning with the decisions that must be made
by a human. This setup can be used to run hundreds or thousands of
agents.

before they get expensive. This post shows readers how to use Claude
to help find their unknowns.
