 APPLE ANNOUNCES $599 'MACBOOK NEO' WITH A18 PRO CHIP (2 MINUTE READ)
[5] 

 Apple's new MacBook Neo is the first Mac to be powered by an iPhone
chip. Using the A18 Pro that debuted in 2024's iPhone 16 Pro models,
the MacBook Neo is 50% faster for everyday tasks than the best-selling
PC with the latest shipping Intel Core Ultra 5. Available in Silver,
Indigo, Blush, and Citrus color options, the device has a 13-inch
Liquid Retina display without a notch. It has a 16-hour battery life,
8GB of unified memory, two USB-C ports, and a headphone jack. The
MacBook Neo starts at $599 for 256GB of storage and is available for
preorder now, with availability beginning on March 11. 

 GOOGLE ENDS ITS 30 PERCENT APP STORE FEE AND WELCOMES THIRD-PARTY APP
STORES (4 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 Google will lower its Play Store fee to 20%, and 15% for new installs
of apps from developers participating in its new App Experience
program or updated Google Play Games Level Up program. Subscription
fees have been lowered to 10%. Google will now charge developers in
the UK, US, and European Economic Area a 5% fee for using its billing
system and a market-specific rate in other regions. Developers can
offer alternative billing systems or guide users to their own websites
for purchases. Third-party app stores can now apply to Google's
Registered App Stores program, which gives them a streamlined
installation interface in Android if they meet certain quality and
safety benchmarks.

 TERRAPOWER GETS OK TO START CONSTRUCTION OF ITS FIRST NUCLEAR PLANT
(2 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 The US Nuclear Regulatory Commission has issued a construction
approval to TerraPower to begin working on a site in Kemmerer,
Wyoming. TerraPower, financially backed by Bill Gates, is attempting
to build a sodium-cooled reactor that incorporates energy storage as
part of its design. The project is expected to be completed in 2030,
but delays are expected as it is a first-of-its-kind project. It is
being built as a joint public-private partnership as part of the
Department of Energy's Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program.

 KRAKEN BECOMES FIRST CRYPTO FIRM TO WIN ACCESS TO FED'S CORE PAYMENTS
SYSTEM (3 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Crypto firm Kraken's banking unit has won access to the Federal
Reserve's core payment systems. The approval of the master account
will allow Kraken Financial to handle transactions more quickly and
seamlessly for big clients and professional traders. The approval is a
major win for the crypto industry, which has been rebuffed for years
in its efforts to tap into the system. It makes Kraken the first
crypto firm to be able to move money on the same rails used by
thousands of banks and credit unions. 

 AI THOUGHTS (9 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 The runaway intelligence case probably won't happen because the
amount of electricity we can generate and the number of chips we can
make is pretty limited. Models will certainly be useful in creating
the next generation of models in the same way computers are useful
tools when designing the next generation of computers, and it seems
reasonable to expect that we'll eventually make an AI that is better
than any human at anything. However, it is very possible that
artificial intelligence will either plateau, just like human
intelligence, or we'll run out of resources to compute the next
iteration of intelligence.
