BIG TECH & STARTUPS

 GOOGLE IS BUILDING A CHIP WITH GEMINI BAKED INTO THE SILICON (3
MINUTE READ) [8]

 Google is reportedly working on a project that would bake Gemini's
neural-network architecture right into the circuitry of a chip. This
would lock the model into the chip, increasing efficiency and
decreasing overhead. The model can still be refreshed by loading new
weights, but the underlying structure will stay fixed. While the
project may not end up as a finished product, it shows how the custom
AI silicon industry may be moving away from running any model to
fusing one model with the metal.

 AMD LAUNCHES HELIOS, ITS FIRST RACK AI SYSTEM TO RIVAL NVIDIA, ADDING
MICROSOFT AS NEWEST BUYER (7 MINUTE READ) [9]

 Helios is a rack-scale system that combines AMD's GPUs, CPUs,
networking, and software to power frontier model inference. It will
start shipping to customers later this year. AMD hasn't released any
details about cost, but it is estimated that Helios will cost between
$5 million and $5.5 million. AMD only holds around 4.5% of the data
center GPU market, but Helios could change that.

 THE PRODUCTIVITY-EXPERIENCE PARADOX (17 MINUTE READ) [14]

 AI is brilliant for external goods - money, status, and output - and
potentially corrosive for internal ones - the skill, the mastery, and
the satisfaction you can only get by doing the thing itself. People
after external goods see AI as close to a superpower, as it makes
people more productive and produces more output. However, for those
interested in the internal goods, AI can feel like a loss, as it takes
the parts they loved and hands it to a machine, leaving only
supervision and accountability. While the output still arrives, the
pride and satisfaction of owning the work is gone.
