BIG TECH & STARTUPS

 META PLATFORMS ACQUIRES HUMANOID ROBOT STARTUP ASSURED ROBOT
INTELLIGENCE (3 MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Meta has acquired a startup that is trying to build humanoid robots.
Assured Robot Intelligence is a company at the frontier of robotic
intelligence. Its team will join Meta to focus on optimizing models
for robotics applications. Investors are skeptical of Meta's spending
plans - the company's stock was down following the announcement of the
acquisition. 

 SPACEX HAS SPENT MORE THAN $15 BILLION ON STARSHIP AND IS RACING TO
MAKE ROCKETRY RESEMBLE AN AIRLINE SCHEDULE (9 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 SpaceX has so far spent more than $15 billion developing Starship.
The company's capital expenditure surged nearly fivefold between 2024
and 2025 to $20.7 billion. Of that spend, $12.7 billion was directed
at AI initiatives. Starlink is still the company's financial engine,
making $11.4 billion in revenue in 2025.

SCIENCE & FUTURISTIC TECHNOLOGY

 THE COMPANY THAT BUILT TIKTOK'S ALGORITHM IS NOW DESIGNING DRUGS FOR
DISEASES PHARMA CALLED UNDRUGGABLE (10 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 ByteDance has entered the AI drug discovery race. The company's drug
discovery unit, Anew Labs, recently presented its first AI-designed
therapy at a major immunology conference. Its work targeted a
protein-protein interaction long thought to be undruggable. The unit
has published a generative model that it claims can design functional
molecules across all scales. 

 AMERICA'S ELECTRICITY GAP (13 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Power access is incredibly important to tech companies' gamble on AI
development. The US was always going to need more power investment to
meet its next phase of economic growth and was already struggling to
meet rising electricity demand well before the AI boom. The costs of
the electricity gap are mounting as power becomes more important to
the national economy. More of the US will have to permit the fast
deployment of solar and batteries if they want to meet their own
increasing electricity needs.

 AGENT SKILLS (16 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 AI coding agents are extremely capable, but they have no instinct for
the parts of the job that don't show up in the diff. Agents will skip
the senior-engineering work unless you make it impossible to skip. The
job increasingly is to encode discipline as something agents can't
talk themselves out of. Skills are one way to do that. The
senior-engineer parts of the job are no longer optional, even when the
engineer is a model.

MISCELLANEOUS

 SOMETIMES SMART PLANNERS LOSE TO SIMPLE MARKETS (11 MINUTE READ) [13]


 People think of the multi-agent future as analogous to our companies,
just autonomous. However, AI agents are not like human agents, and the
hub isn't just an equivalent of a human manager. If any of the
subtasks are wrong, workers can be individually competent and the
final answer will still be wrong. Markets work because people have
local information that can't be easily shared. 

 OPENAI WANTS TO GO PUBLIC. FIRST SARAH FRIAR NEEDS TO GET IT TO GROW
UP (14 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Sarah Friar was brought into OpenAI to give it the financial maturity
needed to go public. Over the past few months, she has balanced the
company's spending expectations and questioned the wisdom of spending
even more money on data centers. It is up to Friar to pull off what
could be one of the biggest IPOs ever. If she moves too slowly, OpenAI
could fall behind and hit the market after Anthropic. Moving quickly
risks stretching the business too far.
