BIG TECH & STARTUPS

 BEFORE MASS LAYOFFS, META REASSIGNS 7,000 WORKERS TO FOCUS ON AI (4
MINUTE READ) [5] 

 Meta is moving 7,000 employees to four new organizations focused on
building new AI tools and apps. The organizations will use AI native
design structures and have fewer managers per employee than other
parts of the company. CEO Mark Zuckerberg has bet the future of Meta
on AI. The company plans to spend up to $135 billion in capital
expenditures this year, much of it going to building computing
facilities that power AI and hiring researchers. 

 JURY REJECTS MUSK'S CLAIMS AGAINST OPENAI (8 MINUTE READ) [6] 

 A judge has dismissed Elon Musk's claims against OpenAI. A jury found
that Musk brought the lawsuit against the company and Sam Altman after
the statute of limitations expired. Musk says he plans to appeal and
described the ruling as a destructive precedent. OpenAI now has a
clear path to a public listing.

 HERE'S WHAT THE WI-FI ROUTER FOR AMAZON'S STARLINK RIVAL LOOKS LIKE
(6 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 The FCC has published images of Amazon's Wi-Fi router for Leo. The
router supports Wi-Fi 6 and a mesh mode. The basic-looking, box-shaped
router has a power port and two Ethernet ports, one specifically for
the Leo dish. It supports Bluetooth Low Energy and the ZigBee wireless
protocol.

 AI EATS THE WORLD (16 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 Big tech is pouring around $700 billion into AI capital expenditures
this year. Under-investing is seen as the bigger risk. Foundation
models are commoditizing fast, and real value is shifting up the stack
to applications, agents, and workflows. Adoption is wide, but shallow.
Deep integration is still rare outside of tech and finance. 

 META'S GIANT AI DATA CENTER IS RESHAPING RURAL LOUISIANA (30 MINUTE
READ) [13] 

 Meta's plans to build the world's biggest AI facility have entangled
it deeply into Louisiana's politics, culture, and economy. The
facility is expected to reach 5 gigawatts of compute capacity. The
company plans to spend more than $200 billion on the project. This
article looks at the series of deals Meta had to make to even start
the project and how the project has impacted the locals in the area.
