 NASA TEST FLIGHT SEEKS TO HELP BRING COMMERCIAL SUPERSONIC TRAVEL
BACK (7 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 NASA's newest experimental supersonic jet, the X-59 Quesst (Quiet
SuperSonic Technology), made its first flight last Tuesday. The
inaugural flight validated the jet's airworthiness and safety. The
X-59 generates a lower 'sonic thump' compared to other supersonic jet
designs thanks to its long, slender nose, which breaks up pressure
waves that would otherwise merge on parts of the airplane. Its design
limits shock waves and directs sound waves up to the sky rather than
down toward the ground. The single-seat, single-engine jet can cruise
at Mach 1.4 at an altitude of 55,000 feet, nearly twice as high and
twice as fast as commercial airliners typically fly.

 IMMUTABLE RELEASES ARE NOW GENERALLY AVAILABLE (1 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 GitHub releases now support immutability. Immutable releases protect
assets and tags from being tampered with after publication. This
ensures that the software remains secure and trustworthy. Immutable
releases can be enabled at the repository or organizational level.
Once enabled, all new releases will be immutable - existing releases
remain mutable unless they are republished. Disabling immutability
doesn't affect releases created while it was enabled.

in ways that no other state management solution can match. URLs can be
state containers, user interfaces, and contracts all rolled into one.
State management libraries still have their place, but the best
solution is just to use the URL.

 AI BROKE INTERVIEWS (25 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 AI caused the entire remote-interview infrastructure to collapse by
breaking the basic assumption that the person answering was the person
thinking. Companies should start testing for things only real
engineers can do. They need high-signal, human-centric interviews that
reflect how engineering actually works. Future interviews will feel
like a conversation between people who want to build something
together.
