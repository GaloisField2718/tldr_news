 APPLE CHOOSES AMAZON SATELLITES FOR IPHONE, YEARS AFTER REJECTING
STARLINK OFFER (6 MINUTE READ) [7] 

 Amazon has entered into an agreement with Apple to provide satellite
service for its devices using the Amazon Leo network. Amazon recently
signed a merger agreement to buy satellite operator Globalstar, which
had already partnered with Apple for satellite messaging on the
iPhone. Buying Globalstar will help Amazon enter the Direct-to-Device
market, in which satellites provide connectivity to mobile phones.
Apple is working on a framework for third-party apps to let developers
add satellite connections to their apps. 

 MAX HODAK'S SCIENCE CORP. IS PREPARING TO PLACE ITS FIRST SENSOR IN A
HUMAN BRAIN (5 MINUTE READ) [8] 

 Science Corporation plans to start human trials for its biohybrid
brain-computer interface. It has hired a top neurobiologist to
surgically place a sensor for a future interface into a patient's
brain. The sensor will be implanted into the skull, but rest on top of
the brain. The final device will be embedded with lab-grown neurons
that can be stimulated with pulses of light and are designed to
naturally integrate with the neurons in a patient's brain.

 ANTHROPIC ADDS ROUTINES TO REDESIGNED CLAUDE CODE, HERE'S HOW IT
WORKS (2 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 Anthropic's new repeatable routines feature for Claude Code runs
tasks on Claude Code's web infrastructure, so users' Macs don't need
to be online for each task. It is now available as a research preview.
The Pro plan supports running 5 routines per day, the Max plan

sessions side by side in a single window and manage them all from the
sidebar. 

 MATT MULLENWEG SAYS “THE WHEELS HAVE FALLEN OFF” IN WIDE-RANGING
WORDPRESS CRITIQUE (15 MINUTE READ) [11] 

 Matt Mullenweg has criticised the WordPress project, saying it spent
years doing damage to itself and that it has a release culture that
produces 'boring or mediocre crap'. He claims that the project isn't
being beaten by competition but that it was in a state of
self-inflicted decline. Some contributors believe that the project's
slowdown was due to Mullenweg's public dispute with WP Engine in
September 2024, but Mullenweg rejected the notion.

 INSIDE GITHUB'S FAKE STAR ECONOMY (16 MINUTE READ) [12] 

 A study found 6 million fake stars across 18,617 repositories. AI/LLM
repositories were the largest non-malicious category. There are at
least a dozen websites selling stars for between $0.03 to $0.85 each.
The FTC introduced a rule in 2024 banning fake social influence
metrics with a penalty of $53,088 per violation. The SEC has already
charged startup founders for inflating traction metrics during
fundraising. 

 GOOGLE INTRODUCES “SKILLS” IN CHROME TO MAKE GEMINI PROMPTS
INSTANTLY REUSABLE (2 MINUTE READ) [13] 

 Chrome has implemented 'Skills' in Chrome - reusable prompts that can
be accessed while browsing with a single click. Skills make it easier
to repeat tasks that were already possible with Gemini in Chrome. The
feature makes it easy to save favorite prompts, making them easy to
quickly access. The desktop version of Chrome will remember saved
Skills across devices.
