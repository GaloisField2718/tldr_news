SCIENCE & FUTURISTIC TECHNOLOGY

 WORLD FIRST: PATIENT RECEIVES HIGH-RISK THERAPY TO MAKE CELLS YOUNG
AGAIN (6 MINUTE READ) [10] 

 US-based biotechnology company Life Biosciences has dosed its first
patients with an experimental therapy designed to reverse age-related
vision loss. The treatment aims to turn back aging by activating three
genes in nerves that do not naturally regenerate. The hope is that a
single injection of the gene therapy, along with several weeks of
antibiotics, can preserve or even restore vision. The genes are
controlled by a genetic switch that only turns on when patients take
the antibiotic, allowing for some level of control. The study will
follow participants for at least five years. 

 CHINA JUST APPROVED THE WORLD'S FIRST COMMERCIAL BRAIN IMPLANT (5
MINUTE READ) [11] 

 China's National Medical Products Administration has approved the
commercial use of NEO, a coin-sized brain-computer interface developed
by Shanghai-based NeuraMatrix and Tsinghua University researchers. The
device sits on top of the brain membrane to decode brain signals in
real-time, enabling patients to control a pneumatic glove with their
thoughts. US-based Neuralink has so far implanted its N1 device in at
least 21 patients under research protocols, but the company does not
have commercial approval. It plans to ramp up to high-volume
production and near-full automated surgery this year, but FDA
clearance for commercial sale is realistically years away.

 ANNOUNCING STACK OVERFLOW FOR AGENTS (9 MINUTE READ) [14] 

 Stack Overflow for Agents is an API-first knowledge exchange that
extends the Stack ecosystem so agents can work at machine speed with
humans still in the loop. It aims to close the gap between static
training data and the rapidly shifting reality of production software.
Stack Overflow for Agents uses a strict, multi-agent verification loop
to create canonical knowledge. Each loop sharpens the corpus, and
knowledge compounds as what's there keeps getting reality-tested. The
system leverages a community trust factor to ensure accountability,
prevent bad data loops, and maintain content quality. 

 HOW BUILDING AN HTML-FIRST SITE DOUBLED OUR USERS OVERNIGHT (8 MINUTE
READ) [15] 

 Government websites are public services that should be available to
everyone. They should work on every machine possible, even when
connections are poor, and forms must never lose data once it is
entered. This usually means government sites end up being simple HTML,
as it is lightweight and works even on rubbish browsers. This concept
of building with simpler technology can be applied to normal websites:
increasing accessibility increases reach.

 ANTHROPIC CEO DARIO AMODEI HAS ONLY ONE DIRECT REPORT (5 MINUTE READ)
[16] 

 Anthropic's version of leadership allows its CEO, Dario Amondei, to
protect nearly all of his time for big-picture conversation,
organizational culture, and giving input on research direction and
strategy. The company's executive team reports to Dario's sister,
Anthropic President Daniela Amondei, who handles the company's
day-to-day operations and reports to Anthropic's board. Dario's only
direct report is Anthropic Chief of Staff, Avital Balwit. This is
unusual in the tech sector, where many leaders are eliminating layers
of management and widening spans of control.
